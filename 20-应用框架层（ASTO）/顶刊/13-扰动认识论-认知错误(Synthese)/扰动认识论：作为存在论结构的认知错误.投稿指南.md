## **Abstract**

Contemporary epistemology typically treats cognitive error as parasitic on successful representation. On this view, error is intelligible only as deviation from truth, accuracy, or reliability. Yet this assumption generates a persistent puzzle: finite cognitive systems do not merely occasionally err, but depend structurally on systematic deviation for their operation. Heuristic compression, predictive bias, and exploratory misalignment are not peripheral defects but enabling conditions of cognition itself.

This paper argues that the puzzle cannot be resolved within any representational framework. As long as cognition is conceived as a relation between internal states and a pre-given world, error can only be interpreted as failure, however pragmatically useful. Such accounts lack the ontological resources to explain why error is both unavoidable and epistemically indispensable.

In response, the paper develops a perturbation-based ontology of cognition. It proposes that perturbation is not an event occurring within cognition, but an ontological operator through which cognitive structure and objecthood are individuated. Cognition is constituted through dynamic field-coupling rather than representation; objects emerge when deviations exceed system-specific thresholds and are localized as sources of disturbance.

Within this framework, error is reconceptualized as the structural residue of individuation under energetic constraint. Error is inevitable because individuation is unavoidable, and normatively significant because perturbation carries thermodynamic cost. Epistemic normativity, on this view, does not arise from truth as a constitutive ideal, but from the management of perturbation required for the continued viability of finite systems.

The paper concludes by exploring the consequences of this account for theories of knowledge, rationality, and artificial intelligence. Knowledge is reinterpreted as stabilized coupling, rationality as perturbation governance, and intelligence as structured vulnerability rather than representational control. Together, these claims motivate a reorientation of epistemology away from the pursuit of error elimination toward an analysis of how finite agents survive and learn within irreducibly perturbative environments.

### 📄 **Cover Letter（Synthese 定制版）**

Dear Editors of *Synthese*,

I would like to submit the manuscript entitled
**“Perturbation and the Ontology of Error”**
for consideration for publication in *Synthese*.

The paper addresses a long-standing problem in epistemology: how cognitive error can be both unavoidable and epistemically indispensable for finite cognitive systems. While dominant representational approaches treat error as parasitic on successful cognition, such accounts struggle to explain the structural necessity of systematic deviation in human and artificial intelligence.

The central contribution of the paper is to argue that this difficulty cannot be resolved at the epistemological level alone. Instead, I propose a shift in ontological orientation, developing a perturbation-based framework in which cognition is constituted through dynamic field-coupling rather than representational mirroring. Within this framework, perturbation functions as an ontological operator through which objects are individuated, and error emerges as a necessary structural residue of this process under thermodynamic constraint.

The paper aims to contribute to ongoing discussions in *Synthese* concerning the relationship between epistemology and metaphysics, non-representational theories of cognition, and the foundations of epistemic normativity. It does not presuppose a commitment to any particular school, but seeks to offer a unifying conceptual framework capable of explaining why error plays a constructive role in cognition without reducing normativity to mere pragmatism.

I believe the manuscript will be of interest to readers concerned with epistemology, philosophy of mind, and process-oriented approaches to cognition.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
**Yi Fu**

### ✅ 为什么这个 cover letter 有效？

它**精准触发编辑的三根神经**：

1. ✔ 明确问题（error puzzle）
2. ✔ 明确理论贡献（ontological shift）
3. ✔ 明确期刊契合点（epistemology × metaphysics）

没有夸张，没有宣言口吻，没有“革命性”用语。
**编辑最怕的就是“我提出了一个全新范式”这种话。**

你完全避开了。

# ✅ 二、Reviewer 最可能攻击的三点（非常关键）

下面这三点，**几乎是必然会被打的**。
我不是猜，是按 Synthese 的历史审稿逻辑。

---

## ⚠️ 攻击点 1：

### “This seems like enactivism in new terminology.”

**Reviewer 心理：**

> 你说的 field-coupling、perturbation、object emergence，
> 不就是 enactivism / ecological psychology / autopoiesis 吗？

### 你需要提前防的不是反驳，而是**定位**。

你要让 reviewer 看到：

> “Yes, but structurally different.”

### 防御方式（一句即可）：

在 introduction 或 3.1 附近加一句：

> *While the present account shares important affinities with enactive and ecological approaches, its central claim is not merely that cognition is action-oriented, but that perturbation functions as an ontological operator of individuation rather than as a dynamical event within an already constituted system.*

这一句非常关键。

它告诉 reviewer：

👉 我不是换皮 enactivism
👉 我在干“本体论角色重分配”

---

## ⚠️ 攻击点 2：

### “This dissolves truth too quickly.”

这是最危险的一刀。

Reviewer 会说：

> If normativity is thermodynamic, what remains of epistemic correctness?

你注意：
他们不是要你回到 correspondence theory，
而是怕你**滑向 relativism 或 instrumentalism**。

### 正确防法不是强调 truth，而是强调：

> truth = secondary stabilizer

你已经写了，但还可以**加一根钉子**。

建议在 4.6 或 5.5 加一句：

> *This account does not deny the legitimacy of truth-based norms within stabilized cognitive regimes; it denies only their ontological primacy.*

这一句非常重要。

它会直接阻断：

* relativism accusation
* “anything goes” accusation

---

## ⚠️ 攻击点 3：

### “Where is the formal or empirical payoff?”

Synthese reviewer 很常问：

> This is interesting, but what does it actually constrain?

你不用给模型、公式、实验。

你只要告诉他：

👉 它限制“什么样的理论是可能的”。

### 防御句式（非常有用）：

在结尾处加一句类似：

> *The framework does not aim to replace empirical models of cognition, but to constrain their interpretation by specifying what error, normativity, and objecthood can coherently mean within finite systems.*

这一句 = 哲学合法性证明。

# ✅ 三、你现在最该做的三处微调（命中率提升最大）

下面是**性价比最高的修改点**。

---

## 🔧 修改点 1（Introduction 最后一句）

现在你的 introduction 已经很好了。

但 Synthese 喜欢一句：

> “Here is what the paper does.”

建议加一个 very analytic 的 roadmap：

> *The paper proceeds as follows.*

哪怕你已经 implicit 了，也建议加。

这是给 reviewer 的“导航信号”。

---

## 🔧 修改点 2（3.1 的“operator”要再钉死一次）

你现在写得很美，但 reviewer 会问：

> What does “operator” mean philosophically?

不需要解释太多，只要一句：

> *By ‘operator’ I mean a principle of structural differentiation rather than a causal event.*

这句话能救命。

---

## 🔧 修改点 3（最后一段不要太诗性）

你结尾写得很好，但略偏“宣言”。

Synthese 更偏好：

* 冷静
* 稳
* 可讨论

建议最后一句是：

> *This perspective suggests that epistemology may be better understood as a theory of regulated perturbation in finite systems, rather than as a theory of representational success.*

这比“search for certainty”那种句式更安全。
