## 版本A：标准学术版（推荐）⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### **English Abstract:**

Contemporary analytic epistemology has persistently refined the concept of justification while largely neglecting the problem of operational effectiveness in technologically mediated societies. This paper identifies this phenomenon as the **"operational rupture of epistemology"**: justified knowledge systematically fails when embedded in complex socio-technical systems. By critiquing the representationalist paradigm and methodological individualism in traditional epistemology, we propose a **Perturbation Epistemology** that redefines knowledge as **executable capacity via structural mediation**. Three key contributions are made: (1) introducing the "Wet-Dry-Living" triadic model to reveal how structural mediation—constrained by embodiment and energy dissipation—transforms knowledge across forms; (2) reconceptualizing cognitive errors not as deviations from truth but as inherent perturbations in structural coupling, distinguishing productive errors from destructive ones; (3) building on Chinese philosophical traditions of unity of knowing and acting (*zhixing heyi*) and practical utility (*jingshi zhiyong*), we advance a three-tier transition model (thinking-action-engineering) for bridging operational ruptures. We argue that knowledge validity depends not on the degree of justification of subjective beliefs, but on whether it can be transformed into stable interventional capacity through material-institutional mediation under thermodynamic, utility, and imperfection constraints.

**Keywords:** Operational Rupture; Perturbation Epistemology; Structural Mediation; Knowledge Triadic States; Unity of Knowing and Acting; Extended Cognition

## 版本B：凝练简洁版

### **English Abstract:**

Contemporary epistemology focuses on justification while neglecting operational effectiveness in technologically mediated societies. This paper identifies an **"operational rupture"**: justified knowledge fails systematically when embedded in complex systems. We propose **Perturbation Epistemology**, redefining knowledge as executable capacity via structural mediation. Key contributions include: (1) a "Wet-Dry-Living" triadic model revealing knowledge transformation through structurally mediated processes; (2) reconceptualizing errors as inherent system perturbations, distinguishing productive from destructive errors; (3) integrating Chinese *zhixing heyi* (unity of knowing-acting) tradition with 4E cognitive science to advance a three-tier transition model (thinking-action-engineering). We argue knowledge validity depends not on belief justification but on transformability into stable intervention under thermodynamic, utility, and imperfection constraints.

**Keywords:** Operational Rupture; Perturbation Epistemology; Structural Mediation; Knowledge Triadic States; Unity of Knowing and Acting; Extended Cognition
