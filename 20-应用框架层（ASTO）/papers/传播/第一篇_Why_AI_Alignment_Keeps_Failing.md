# Why AI Alignment Keeps Failing: A Structural View

---

## 1. The Surface Conflict

AI must be powerful.
AI must be safe.
The two seem compatible.
But in practice, instability keeps emerging.

The problem is not insufficient technology.
The problem is structure.

---

## 2. The Hidden Tensions

Two core tensions define this conflict:

- **Capability Expansion** — the drive to increase AI's problem-solving capacity
- **Safety Constraint** — the need to bound AI's action space

When the capability space expands, the constraint space must expand with it.
Otherwise, the system becomes imbalanced.

---

## 3. Structural Mapping (ASTO Framework)

Abstract the problem into:

- **Attribute Set A**: The capability growth set
- **Attribute Set B**: The constraint and alignment set

When the expansion rate of A exceeds the expansion rate of B:

> Structural tension overflows.

This is not a moral failure.
It is a structural problem.

---

## 4. What If We Change the Constraints?

Hypothetical scenarios:

- **Increase constraint strength** → Innovation speed declines
- **Relax constraints** → Risk指数 rises

This is structural conservation.
Not a matter of will.

---

## 5. The ASTO Advantage

> Alignment is not a moral failure.
> It is a structural imbalance between expanding capability and lagging constraint space.

Traditional analysis gets stuck in "who is right or wrong."
ASTO reveals this is a structural inevitability: as long as capability and safety exist as independent attribute sets, conflict is unavoidable.

The solution is not better intentions.
The solution is structural redesign.
