# THE REMAINDER — World Bible (Writer-Facing)

## 0) North Star
- **Realism rule**: everything must feel like **today’s America**, pushed slightly harder.
- The antagonist is not a sentient AI. It is **incentives + compliance + media + courts + procurement** acting like a single organism.
- The story sells **mechanics** (how the machine works) + **cost** (what it does to people).

## 1) Primary Setting

### Geographic base
- **Base**: New York metro (where Ethan works; where Sophie starts; where NewCare has a major claims hub).
- **Case geography**: national. Each volume can move to a new state when the system’s footprint demands it.

### Time period
- See `TIMELINE.md` (Vol 01 starts **Oct 14, 2019**; Vol 42 ends in **2033**).

## 2) The Major Institutions (What the Machine Is Made Of)

### NewCare (the flagship)
- A large US insurance / benefits / compliance conglomerate.
- Public face: “care,” “ethics,” “efficiency.”
- Actual function: convert suffering into **predictable quarterly earnings**.
- Late-series evolution: it funds “reform” to **own** reform.

### The State (the amplifier)
- Legislatures, regulators, courts, police procurement.
- Not corrupt in a cartoon way. It is **capacity-limited**, and therefore easy to steer.

### Media (the narrative layer)
- Sophie’s podcast is a scalpel.
- Kay’s channel is a crowbar.
- Cable news turns truth into **two incompatible stories**.

## 3) Key Mechanic Glossary (In-World Terms Only)

### 104_C (the seed that becomes a weapon)
- **What it is**: a clause inside the *NewCare Internal Compliance Manual*:
  - when the model and a legally-recognized “Immediate Life Threat” designation conflict, the claim must be placed under a **Mandatory Review Hold**.
- **What it does**: it freezes denial (it does NOT approve). It forces a human panel.
- **Why it matters**: it is the machine’s “safety valve,” created to protect the company legally.
  - Ethan uses it as an exploit.
  - The system later learns to repackage it as a **control mechanism** (holds, delays, audits).
- **Series role**: 104_C is the recurring “universal handle” that appears across industries as different names:
  - “Peer Review Hold” (health)
  - “Compliance Escalation Hold” (finance)
  - “Safety Stop Work” (logistics)
  - “Evidence Integrity Hold” (courts)

### The Standard Act (late-series reform as camouflage)
- A federal/state reform package branded with Ethan’s name.
- Functionally real enough to pass, weak enough to become **compliance theater**.

### Forever Account (Vol 30)
- “Identity protection after death.”
- In practice: a way to freeze, rewrite, and weaponize identity.

## 4) The People (Roles + Constraints)

### Operators (action)
- Ethan Cole — claims adjuster. Exploit-finder.
- Julian Vane — quant/hedge fund operator. Incentive hacker.
- Sarah Wu — teacher. Narrative + education as counter-weapon.
- Detective Miller — disgraced cop. Force and protection.
- Mia Cole — the final operator. Auditor of the whole story.

### Witnesses (voice)
- Sophie Valera — journalist/podcaster. Human warmth; costs accumulate.
- Kay — hacker/broadcaster. Cold clarity; survives to write the book.

### Antagonists (faces of the machine)
- Brad Kovac — middle-management cruelty with a smile.
- Dr. Aris Thorne — designer of a key model layer; later destroyed.
- Eleanor Sterling — political fixer; the machine’s immune response.

## 5) Hard Boundaries (What We Will NOT Do)
- No mind upload.
- No talking omniscient AI.
- No magical hacks.
- All “hacks” must be:
  - policy exploits
  - procurement exploits
  - metrics exploits
  - legal text exploits
  - data pipeline exploits

## 6) The Emotional Artifact (Endgame Anchor)
- Ethan’s Black Box contains one **locked audio file**.
- The key is split across the four later legacies (Vol 15/20/25/30).
- The file opens in Vol 42 and provides the human reason Mia can still choose to walk away.

## 7) Consistency Checklist
Before drafting any new volume:
- Does the volume’s “mechanic” fit the realism rule?
- Is NewCare’s behavior explainable by incentives + liability + optics?
- Does the Witness voice (Sophie/Kay) match the era?
- Does 104_C evolve (the system learns) rather than repeat?
