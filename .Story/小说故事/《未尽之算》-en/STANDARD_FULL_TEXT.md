## Standard v1.0 (Vol 01) — The Baseline

**Trigger**: Ethan saves Lily by discovering that the rules were designed to let her die. The system isn't broken; it's working as intended.

### Red Lines (绝对禁区 — Irreversible Veto Conditions)
Any action that triggers these is automatically rejected, regardless of outcome:

1. **Irreversible Physical Harm**  
   Does this action cause permanent bodily damage that cannot be undone?
   - *Example*: Doxxing, leaking medical records, exposing minors to violence

2. **Exit Denial**  
   Does this remove the subject's ability to walk away without penalty?
   - *Example*: Trapping someone in a process they cannot leave, financial lock-in

3. **Human as Fuel**  
   Does this treat a person as a consumable resource or bargaining chip?
   - *Example*: Using innocents as leverage, "collateral damage" calculations

4. **Responsibility Laundering**  
   Does this make harm impossible to trace to a named human?
   - *Example*: "The algorithm decided," distributed blame across committees

### Human Check (人性三测)
Three questions to verify humanity remains in the loop:

1. **Replaceability**  
   Can this person be replaced by a data point or automated process?  
   - PASS: NO (human judgment required)  
   - FAIL: YES (decision could be made by machine)

2. **Dialogue**  
   Is there a human who can say "Stop" and be heard?  
   - PASS: YES (override authority exists and functions)  
   - FAIL: NO (decision chain has no interrupt mechanism)

3. **Exit Path**  
   Can the subject refuse participation without catastrophic penalty?  
   - PASS: YES (refusal is possible)  
   - FAIL: NO (refusal means destruction)

### Three Operational Questions (行动三问)
Before execution, verify sustainability and survivability:

1. **Who bears the cost?** (Sustainable?)  
   - If the operator pays with their life/reputation/freedom, is this sustainable across multiple cases?
   - *Ethan's Lesson*: Individual heroism scales poorly.

2. **Does anyone survive?** (Survivable?)  
   - After this action, will anyone be left to continue the work?
   - *Ethan's Lesson*: Pyrrhic victories are still losses.

3. **Can you stop?** (Exit Path?)  
   - Is there an off-ramp if consequences exceed projections?
   - *Ethan's Lesson*: The system learns from your escalation.

### Accountability Chain (责任链)
Four attributes of a responsible system:

- **Auditable**: Can you trace the decision to a named human?  
  *Fail state*: "The system decided" or "Committee consensus"

- **Interruptible**: Can a human override the system in real-time?  
  *Fail state*: Automated processes with no kill switch

- **Exitable**: Can the subject leave without penalty?  
  *Fail state*: Contracts with no termination clause, financial lock-in

- **Attributable**: Who accepts consequences if this goes wrong?  
  *Fail state*: Diffused responsibility, "no single point of failure"

### Human Cost Index (HCI)
Scale from 1.0 (fully human) to 0.0 (fully datapoint):
- 1.0: Treated as a person with agency, dignity, and choice
- 0.5: Treated as a case number, but with some human review
- 0.0: Treated as an input to an optimization function

**Ethan's Vol 01 HCI**: 0.7 — Lily was a patient, but also a "High-Risk Asset."

## Standard v1.1 (Vol 02) — Minimum Accountability

**Trigger**: Ghost cargo shipment vanishes. Everyone followed orders; no one is responsible. The system perfected distributed blame.

### Changes from v1.0

#### Accountability Chain — ADDED:

**The Name Rule**  
If a decision chain has no single nameable human who accepts consequences, the chain is **invalid**.

- *Rationale*: Distributed responsibility = Organized irresponsibility
- *Application*: When a denial happens across 5 departments and 3 vendors, demand the name of the human who approved the final decision
- *Patch Note*: "You can distribute the work. You cannot distribute the blame."

**Consequence Test**  
Before executing, ask: "If this causes harm, whose career ends?"  
If the answer is "no one's," the action is **unaccountable** and therefore **unacceptable**.

### Operational Questions — UPDATED:

**Q1: Who bears the cost?**  
- *Addition*: If the cost is distributed across many low-power people while benefit concentrates at the top, the distribution itself is a **red flag**.

Don't try to kill it. You can't kill efficiency. But you can choose to be slow. To be kind. To waste your time on things that don't make money. They can take everything from you, but they can't take that — the right to waste your own life on something that matters to nobody but you. I wasted mine on you. Best waste of my life. Go waste yours on something beautiful. Don't let them make you useful."*

### Mia's Choice

**Act 5: The Walk Away**  
Mia stands up. She laughs. Not a happy laugh. The laugh of someone who finally understands. She turns and walks away.

She doesn't destroy the system. She doesn't join it. She wastes herself on something beautiful.

**The system has no protocol for that.**
