# THE REMAINDER — Translation & Style Guide (v1.0)

**Purpose**: To ensure the English prose feels like "Clinical Noir" — detached, precise, and brutal.
**Target Audience**: Western readers accustomed to LitRPG/Progression Fantasy, but seeking a "System" based on realism.

---

## 1. The Core Lexicon ("Lao-A" Vocabulary)

We use gaming/internet slang **sparingly** to recontextualize real-world horror. The goal is to make the reader see the system as a game engine.

### Tier 1: The Essential Replacements (Use Freely)
These terms define the worldview.

| Concept | English Term | Do Not Use | Context / Nuance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **贫困线/生死线** | **The Kill Line** | Poverty Line / Threshold | "It's not poverty; it's the point where the system stops keeping you alive." |
| **漏洞/后门** | **Exploit** | Loophole / Backdoor | "A loophole is an accident. An exploit is a tool." |
| **死亡/被裁员** | **Despawn** | Die / Fire / Terminate | Used by Operators to show detachment. "He didn't get fired. He got despawned." |
| **合规/规则** | **The Patch** | Regulations / Laws | "New legislation is just a balance patch." |
| **甚至/起码/底线** | **The Standard** | Morals / Ethics | "We don't have morals. We have The Standard." |
| **官僚流程** | **Lag** | Red tape / Delay | "The court has a 6-month lag." |
| **仇恨值/关注度** | **Aggro** | Attention / Heat | "You pulled aggro from Legal." |

## 2. Sentence Patterns: The "Clinical Noir" Rhythm

Chinese narrative often flows. English Clinical Noir must **cut**.

### Pattern A: The Jab (Subject-Verb-Object)
Use this for **brutal facts**. No adjectives. No emotion.
*   *Chinese*: 他看着那份拒赔通知单，心里感到一阵冰冷，因为这意味着Lily的治疗费没了。
*   *Bad English*: He looked at the denial letter and felt cold because it meant Lily's treatment was gone.
*   *Clinical English*: **He read the letter. It was a denial. Lily's funding was zero.** (Three jabs).

### Pattern B: The Corporate Punch (The Run-on)
Use this for **System Logic**. Long, passive voice, full of Latinate words.
*   *Context*: Brad explaining why he can't help.
*   *English*: "The utilization review algorithm has determined that the projected quality-adjusted life years do not meet the threshold for experimental intervention reimbursement under Section 104." (Suffocating).

### Pattern C: The "Lao-A" Contrast
Combine A and B. The System speaks complex nonsense; the Operator translates it into brutal slang.
*   *Brad*: "We are currently experiencing a liquidity optimization event."
*   *Ethan*: **"You're broke."**

## 3. Cultural Localization (China → West)

We are setting this in a **Generic Western Metropolis** (like Gotham or "The City"), modeled on New York/Chicago.

### Institution Mapping

| Chinese Concept | Western Equivalent | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **医保局/社保** | **NewCare (The Company)** | Privatized, corporate, profit-driven. Not the government. |
| **信访办** | **Ombudsman / Compliance Office** | The place where complaints go to die. |
| **编制** | **Tenure / The Plan** | The status of being "safe" inside the system. |
| **关系/走后门** | **Networking / Legacy Access** | It's not "corruption"; it's "access". |
| **单位** | **The Firm / The Corp** | The all-encompassing employer. |

### Social Dynamics
*   **Face (面子)** → **Credibility / Optics**. Westerners care about "how it looks in court/press," not "shame."
*   **Filial Piety (孝)** → **Legacy / Debt**. Children don't owe parents "obedience"; they owe them "care costs."
*   **Guanxi (关系)** → **Leverage**. You don't help people because you are friends; you help because they have leverage on you.

## 4. Character Voices

### Ethan Cole (The Paladin)
*   **Tone**: Exhausted, precise. Speaks in "Bullet Points."
*   **Keywords**: Cost, Risk, Outcome, Corpse.
*   **Sample**: "I don't care if you meant well. I care about the math. The math says she dies on Tuesday."

### Sophie Valera (The Witness)
*   **Tone**: Journalistic, then cracking. Public voice is NPR/BBC; private voice is panicked.
*   **Keywords**: Source, Record, Verify... Help.
*   **Sample**: "Recording start. October 14th. Subject is... [pause]... Subject is a 7-year-old girl. God."

### Brad Kovac (The NPC)
*   **Tone**: Cheerful corporate sociopathy. Never uses negative words.
*   **Keywords**: Synergy, Bandwidth, Alignment, Great.
*   **Sample**: "Ethan! Great to see you. We have a fantastic opportunity to realign our loss ratios this quarter."

### Mia Cole (The Virus)
*   **Tone**: Gen Z Nihilism + Coder precision. Short. Lowercase energy.
*   **Keywords**: cringe, delete, burn, lol.
*   **Sample**: "ur system is trash. i fixed it. ur welcome."

## 5. Formatting Standards

*   **Dialogue**: Use double quotes `""`.
*   **Thoughts**: Use italics *without* quotes. *He knew it was a lie.*
*   **System Text**: Use code blocks or blockquotes.
    ```text
    ALERT: SEVERITY CRITICAL
    ```
*   **Sophie's Board**: Always blockquoted.

---

## 6. The "Golden Rule" of Translation

**Do not translate the sentence. Translate the function.**

*   If the Chinese sentence makes the reader cry...
*   The English sentence should make the reader **shiver**.
*   We trade "Tragedy" (tears) for "Horror" (coldness).

*Example*:
*   *CN*: "可怜的Lily再也看不到明天的太阳了。" (Tragedy)
*   *EN*: **"Lily's subscription to sunlight has expired."** (Horror)
