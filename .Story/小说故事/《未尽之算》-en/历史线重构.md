# The Remainder - History Reconstruction Map

## Core Principle
Shift from "Chinese History + Western Modern" to **"Western History (Primary) + Global History (Secondary)"**.
History is introduced via **Sophie's History Files** (Part I–III) and **Kay's Dark Archive** (Part IV), not magical memory.

## Integration Method

### Sophie's History Files (Part I–III)
- 120–200 words, podcast intro/outro or private file note.
- Always tied to the current volume’s mechanic.
- No lectures. Just a parallel that makes the reader feel: *"this has happened before."*

**Example (Vol 01)**:
> "You think pricing a child is new? In 1911, they locked factory doors to prevent theft. They priced stolen fabric higher than human life. The fire just closed the spreadsheet for them."

### Kay's Dark Archive (Part IV)
- Short, cold pulls. Feels like leaked FOIA, a screenshot, a log.
- The point is not education. The point is dread.

**Example (Vol 41)**:
> "ARCHIVE PULL: hearing footage. Two edits. Two realities. Same words. Different war."
