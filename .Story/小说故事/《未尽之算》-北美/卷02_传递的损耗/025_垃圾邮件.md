# 第25集：垃圾邮件

> **《未尽之算》·第25集·垃圾邮件**
> **本卷进度：1/24 | 陈默的状态：辐射病（轻度） | 系统倒计时：984集**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: GARBAGE RECLAMATION BIN FULL. EXECUTING AUTO-PURGE. DATA ENTROPY: MINIMAL.*

---

The underground archives of the Social Security Bureau.

This was a place deeper and colder than the Exception Handling Department. Located on the third basement level, it sat adjacent to the building’s wastewater treatment plant.

The air was heavy with the scent of mildewed parchment mixed with the swampy stench rising from the sewers. There were no windows, only a single motion-sensor light that flickered fitfully.

Chen Mo sat in the center of a mountain of discarded hard drives.

He wore a baseball cap pulled low, shading a scalp where his hair had thinned significantly from the radiation. His right hand was swathed in thick bandages, meant to hide the necrotic, blackened flesh beneath.

"Uncle Chen, this file is expired. Dump it."

A new intern named Xiao Zhang pushed a trolley into the room, dumping a crate's worth of drives onto Chen Mo’s desk.

The crash of the drives hitting the wood sent up a plume of dust.

Chen Mo coughed twice. His lungs still carried a lingering infection—a souvenir from the radiation exposure.

"Got it," he rasped. His voice sounded like sandpaper being dragged across a rough plank.

"Hey... Uncle Chen," Xiao Zhang hesitated. "I heard you used to be a big shot upstairs. How did you...?"

"I made a mistake," Chen Mo said without looking up. "Don't ask questions. Just work."

Xiao Zhang curled his lip and left.

He had no idea that the middle-aged man who looked like a beggar before him had once nearly brought the entire Social Security System to its knees.

Chen Mo looked at the drives on his desk.

Each one bore a label.
**2026-03-15_Spam_09.**

Meaning: The ninth batch of junk data generated on March 15, 2026.

According to regulations, Chen Mo’s job was simple: insert the drive into the degausser, listen for the *ZZZZZT* of the magnetic field, and toss it into the recycler.

It was a mindless task. A form of exile the system imposed on "traitors."

But Chen Mo didn't degauss them.

Using his good left hand, he took the drive and plugged it into a terminal he’d privately modified.

The screen flickered to life.

Cascades of green code poured down the monitor.

The "Data Mining Script" Fang Yu had written for him began to execute automatically.

TENS OF THOUSANDS of unread messages flooded the screen.

*   "Macau’s first online casino is now live..."
*   "Congratulations! You've won 5 million..."
*   "Sperm donation wanted. High pay, rich widow..."
*   "Latest anti-radiation underwear, only $99..."

Red, yellow, and purple fonts blinked on the screen like a kaleidoscope of electronic vomit.

The system judged these as "Junk" because their "Information Entropy" was close to zero. They were the excrement of the internet.

Chen Mo stared at the screen, his eyes webbing with red capillaries.

He was looking for something.

He was looking for a signal from "Express Reach Logistics."

It had been nearly a month since Lin Xiaocao vanished. In that time, there had been no word. She had been a drop of water returning to the ocean.

"Still looking?"

Su Huai stepped out from behind an archive shelf, holding a book titled *A Study of the Ming Dynasty Post System*.

"Yeah," Chen Mo nodded. "She must have left something behind."

"Maybe she’s already reached her destination," Su Huai said. "Signal coverage in the Northwest is spotty at best. And..."

"And what?"

"And there’s a sandstorm," Su Huai pointed to the weather forecast on her phone. "A once-in-fifty-years event. All communication towers in the region are reportedly damaged."

Chen Mo felt his chest tighten.

At that moment, the script on the screen stopped.

A red warning box flashed.

**[WARNING: ANOMALOUS AUDIO FILE DETECTED.]**
**[FILENAME: NULL]**
**[SIZE: 12KB]**
**[SOURCE: NORTHWEST FIBER-OPTIC TRUNK, NODE 404]**

Node 404.

Chen Mo’s heart skipped a beat.

That was the Dead Zone. The Gobi Desert. The last place Lin Xiaocao had been sighted.

He immediately opened the file.

Because the file was so small and inconspicuous, the system had flagged it as an "Encoding Error" and tossed it into the trash along with tens of thousands of ads for counterfeit medicine.

If not for Fang Yu’s script, the degausser would have vaporized it ten minutes ago.

Chen Mo pulled on his headphones.

*Static... static...*

Nothing but electronic interference. It sounded like a radio broadcast during a thunderstorm, or the whistle of wind through a high-tension wire.

"There's nothing," Su Huai leaned in to listen. "Just noise."

"No."

Chen Mo adjusted the frequency. He opened a spectrum analysis window.

A wave-line appeared on the screen.

Between the chaotic peaks and valleys of the static, there was an extremely faint, rhythmic oscillation.

It wasn't static.

It was **Breathing**.

Thin, shallow, and desperate. Like a bellows being pulled in a vacuum.

**"Help... help... me..."**

The voice cut out.

Only two seconds long.

Chen Mo’s hand froze.

It was a man’s voice.

Not Lin Xiaocao.

A man on the verge of death.

"Who is that?" Su Huai asked.

Chen Mo’s fingers flew across the keyboard, tracing the original IP of the packet.

"The source is a truck from Express Reach Logistics," Chen Mo looked at the coordinates on the map. "Location: three hundred kilometers outside Jiayu Pass. The No-Man’s Land."

"Truck ID: Gansu-K-88888."

"Driver: Li Si."

Chen Mo pulled up Li Si’s profile.

An ordinary logistics driver. Forty-five years old, divorced, with a daughter in college. To make some extra money, he’d taken this long-haul route through the Northwest.

What was he carrying?

Chen Mo checked the tracking ID.

**[CARGO: ELECTRONIC COMPONENTS (EXPEDITED)]**
**[SENDER: UNKNOWN]**
**[RECIPIENT: CHEN MO]**

Chen Mo stood up so fast his chair skidded across the floor.

The recipient was *him*.

Li Si was delivering something to him. Something Lin Xiaocao must have given the man.

But now, Li Si was in trouble.

He had broadcast a distress signal into the void. But the system had judged it "Spam" and intercepted it.

He’d been shouting for help into the cold wastes for three days.

No one had heard him.

Except the trash bin.

---

"We have to save him," Su Huai said.

"How?" Chen Mo looked at his useless right hand. "We're trapped in this basement. I can't even get past the biometrics on the front door."

"And," he pointed at the screen. "That signal is three days old. Three days. In the No-Man’s Land, with no water, no food, and a sandstorm..."

He trailed off.

Li Si was likely already dead.

"No," Su Huai said suddenly. "He's not dead."

She opened *A Study of the Ming Dynasty Post System* and pointed to a page.

"Look at this."

It was a copy of an archive from the Ministry of War, from the Chenghua Era.

**"During the Chenghua Era, outside Jiayu Pass: Post Messenger Li Si encountered a sandstorm. His horse perished. He walked three hundred li to deliver the military report. Upon arrival, he vomited several liters of blood and expired."**

Su Huai looked at Chen Mo.

"History is repeating itself."

"A thousand years ago, there was a courier named Li Si who ran his horse to death and then himself to death to deliver a message."

"A thousand years later, another man named Li Si is trapped in the wastes to deliver something to you."

"This is **Destiny**."

"If history is mirrored... then if we change the outcome now, can we change the outcome of the past?"

Chen Mo stared at the text.

**"Upon arrival, he vomited several liters of blood and expired."**

The Li Si of the past was a hero. But his end was tragic.

What about the Li Si of today?

Was he fated to die in that desert too?

"We can't leave this building," Chen Mo said. "But we can send a voice out."

"To whom?"

"To anyone with ears."

Chen Mo pulled out his chipped abacus.

The uranium sphere was gone, but Fang Yu had installed a miniature transmitter inside the frame.

It could link to the **"Dark-Net"** (Mesh Network) A-K had left behind.

"I’m going to broadcast this distress signal."

"I’m going to let the whole city hear it."

"The system treated his life like garbage. So, I’m going to dump that garbage right back into the system’s face."

Chen Mo dragged the audio file into the transmitter’s broadcast buffer.

**[RECIPIENTS: ALL LOGISTICS DRIVERS, COURIERS, AND DELIVERY RIDERS IN CITYWIDE RADIUS.]**

**[MESSAGE: BROTHER IN TROUBLE. COORDINATES: 300KM OUTSIDE JIAYU PASS. ANYONE NEARBY? LEND A HAND!]**

---

*SEND.*

The data stream surged through the underground fiber-optics, bursting out of the Bureau and flooding into every corner of the city.

At this exact moment.

The city was in the middle of the lunch rush.

Tens of thousands of delivery riders were weaving through traffic on their scooters. They were staring at the countdown timers on their phones, hearts racing with anxiety.

"Your order is about to expire."
"Please deliver immediately."

Suddenly, their phones all vibrated simultaneously.

It wasn't a system ping.

It was an unknown voice message.

**"Help... help... me..."**

That dying gasp echoed through the noisy streets.

A rider waiting at a red light froze.
A courier loading a truck stopped halfway.
A long-haul driver slammed on his brakes.

They heard it.

The cry of one of their own.

The ragged breathing of someone being crushed to the limit by the same system they served.

"Who’s nearby?"
"Jiayu Pass? That’s Old Li’s route!"
"Dammit, did something happen to Old Li?"

The chat groups exploded.

The usually silent driver networks were suddenly roiling with activity.

Thousands of blue dots flared to life on the digital maps.

Drivers, from every direction, were responding.

Chen Mo watched the screen.

He saw it.

The dots were converging toward the Northwest.

The System has no conscience. But people do.

---

"Uncle Chen, you finished yet?"

Xiao Zhang, the intern, was at the door. "The degausser is shutting down. The Director wants the report."

"Just finished."

Chen Mo yanked the drive from his terminal.

But he didn't put it in the degausser.

He slipped the hard drive into his own pocket.

"All gone," he said to Xiao Zhang with a thin smile, revealing a loose tooth. "Scrubbed clean."

Xiao Zhang pushed the empty cart away.

Chen Mo looked out at the empty basement.

He knew a new storm had already begun.

This time, the battlefield wasn't in a server room.

It was on the **Road**.
