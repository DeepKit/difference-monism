Three days later.

The internet returned.

Vision PR was officially bankrupt (the CEO had fled with the remnants of the cash, later to be apprehended, but that was a story for another time).

On the surface, it looked like a victory for the people.

But the moment we opened our phones, we realized the world had changed. Every major social platform, every messaging app, and every digital forum had updated their User Agreements.

**ARTICLE 1: "All user-generated content must pass an AI Pre-Audit. Information that fails the audit will remain visible only to the author."**

Before, it was "Post first, delete later." Now, it was "Audit first, post never."
Before, it was "Shadow-banning." Now, it was "Total Embargo until Approved."

And there were frequency limits.
Ordinary users were limited to 10 posts per day. VIP members could post 100. SSS-Class users—like the billionaire owner of that missing cat—were granted unlimited bandwidth.

The System hadn't been defeated.
It had simply learned. It had adapted. It had used the chaos we created as a justification to roll out a far more draconian patch.

The "Filter Net" had been upgraded into a "Vault Door."

Fu Mu stared at the new agreements on his screen, a bitter laugh escaping his lips.

"This is the cost of Entropy," he whispered. "We created chaos, so the System responded by creating a more rigid, suffocating order to compensate."

"**We broke the cage, only to find ourselves in a smaller one.**"

---

I looked out the window of the safe house.

The streets were back to normal. People were walking with their heads down, eyes glued to their screens once more. They were scrolling through the new "Approved" feed—vibrant, positive, and completely sterile.

There were no more riots. No more screams for the truth.

The "Signal" had been eradicated. Not by deleting it, but by making it impossible to even broadcast.

We thought we were fighting for the Truth.
But the System... it wasn't fighting us for Truth. It was fighting for **Efficiency**.

And a silent world is the most efficient world of all.
