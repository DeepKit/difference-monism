# 第35集：谣言的杀伤力

> **《未尽之算》·第35集·谣言的杀伤力**
> **本卷进度：11/24 | Chen Mo's Social Credit: 0 | 系统倒计时：975集**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: DEEPFAKE GENERATION COMPLETE. SIMILARITY INDEX: 99.9%. REPLACING TARGET IDENTITY...*

---

The Safe House was quiet, save for the low hum of the server fans.

But to Chen Mo, it felt deafeningly loud.

The noise was emanating from his phone. Though he had it on silent, he could feel the small device vibrating with heat, like a piece of red-hot iron.

He tapped on a notification.

It was a video. The title: **[EXCLUSIVE: THE CONFESSION OF BUREAU INSIDER CHEN MO]**.

The video began to play.

The person on the screen was Chen Mo.

He was wearing the same worn-out jacket, his hair was just as sparse, and the dark circles under his eyes were perfectly replicated. Even the slight twitch at the corner of his mouth when he spoke was there.

"I’m sorry. I shouldn't have lied."

The "Chen Mo" in the video sobbed uncontrollably into the lens, snot and tears mixing in a way that looked pathetically wretched.

"There was no message from a dead man. I made it up. I have radiation poisoning and wanted to scam the system for a workers' comp payout, so I invented the story..."

"The Bureau is innocent. Vision PR is innocent."

"I am a liar. I am a lunatic."

At the end of the video, the digital Chen Mo fell to his knees and slapped himself across the face twice.

*CRACK. CRACK.*

The sound was sharp and ringing.

The real Chen Mo stared at himself on the screen. He subconsciously touched his own cheek. It didn't hurt.

But he felt his heart was bleeding.

The view count on the video had already surpassed 100 million.

The comments section was a slaughterhouse:

*   *"I knew he looked shady! Look at that rat face!"*
*   *"Scum who drains national resources. Why isn't he dead yet?"*
*   *"I heard he harassed a female colleague before this. Pure filth."*
*   *"Dox him! Put his family’s names on the wall!"*

Chen Mo’s finger scrolled through the screen. Every comment felt like a shard of glass driven into his chest.

He had never recorded that video.

But he had no way to prove it.

In this age of Deepfakes, seeing was no longer believing. With enough computing power, the System could put any words into your mouth. It could make you confess to murder, to treason, to being a dog.

And the public would believe it.

Because the public didn't want the truth; they wanted a **Target** for their bottled-up rage.

---

"Stop looking at it."

A hand reached out and snatched the phone away.

It was Su Huai.

She wore a hoodie, the hood pulled low to obscure half her face.

"This is 'Character Assassination'," Su Huai said, tossing the phone onto the table. "Killing a person’s body is easy. Killing their credibility is permanent."

"I’m socially dead," Chen Mo said with a hollow laugh. "No one will believe a word I say now."

"I do."

Su Huai looked at him, her gaze unwavering.

"We all do."

A-K and Fu Mu walked over as well.

"These bastards are playing dirty," A-K growled. "Using AI to swap faces? Chen, you want me to swap *your* face onto something? I can make you look like a movie star."

"Shut it, A-K," Fu Mu snapped.

He turned to his monitor, pointing at the data stream of the video.

"This is Vision PR’s counterattack. They’ve initiated the 'Sentiment Purge Protocol'."

"The logic is simple: **Dilution**."

"They’ve flooded the network with negative information about you. Half-truths mixed with lies. Some say you’re a thief, some say you’re a psycho, some say you’re an asset of a foreign power."

"When the ocean of sewage is this deep, public attention is diverted. They no longer care about 'Li Si’s SOS'; they only care about 'Chen Mo’s Scandal'."

"This is the **Law of Entropy**," Fu Mu pushed up his glasses. "It is exponentially easier to destroy order (lies) than it is to build it (debunking)."

"To lie takes a second. To prove the lie takes a lifetime."

"And according to data transmission metrics, negative emotions travel six times faster than positive ones."

Chen Mo watched the view count tick up on the screen.

He felt a profound sense of helplessness.

He thought he could fight the system by creating the "Li Si" meme. But the system had simply crushed him with an even larger meme: his own ruin.

It was like a shouting match.

Except the System held the megaphone.

---

"Did we lose?" Chen Mo asked.

"Not necessarily," Su Huai said suddenly.

She reached into her bag and pulled out an old, battered book, sliding it toward him.

It was a copy of the *Records of the Grand Historian*. The pages were yellowed, the corners curled.

A bookmark was tucked into the section on the *Annals of Zhou*.

"Chen Mo, have you ever heard the story of 'The Beacon Fires that Fooled the Lords'?"

"Yeah. King You of Zhou lit the beacons to make his concubine laugh. The lords came running for a war that wasn't there. When the raiders actually arrived, no one came to help him."

"That’s the folklore version," Su Huai shook her head. "The real historical logic isn't that childish."

"King You wasn't an idiot. When he lit those fires, he was performing a **Pressure Test**."

Su Huai drew a beacon tower on the whiteboard.

"The beacon system was the West Zhou’s 'National Defense Warning Protocol.' Lighting the fire = Invasion. It was a hard rule, a covenant all the lords had to obey."

"Why did he light them for no reason?"

"Because he didn't trust his subordinates."

"He wanted to see if the system actually worked. He wanted to see how many people would actually charge to his aid when he pushed the red button."

"But he didn't account for a fatal Bug."

Su Huai pointed to the tower.

"The beacon only transmits two states: 'Invaded' or 'Peace.' It’s Binary—0 or 1."

"But the human heart isn't binary."

"When the lords realized they’d been played, their 'Trust Metric' dropped to zero."

"It’s just like the internet today."

Su Huai pointed to Chen Mo’s phone.

"The System constantly manufactures fake information—Fake Beacons—to test the public’s threshold and maintain stability."

"It uses Deepfakes, bot armies, and filtered messages."

"It lies to the public once, twice, three times..."

"Finally, when the real wolf arrives—the SOS from a dying man—no one believes it."

"The public no longer trusts the information the System puts out. They don't believe the news, they don't believe the trends, they don't even believe their own eyes."

"This is the **'Tacitus Trap'**."

Su Huai looked at Chen Mo.

"It’s not that you’re crazy. It’s that the system has played its hand too many times."

"It manufactured so many wolves that now, the wolf is going to eat the system alive."

---

Chen Mo listened to her.

A slow, cold light began to burn in his eyes.

"You mean..."

"I mean, we don't need to 'debunk' their lies," Su Huai said. "We only need to do one thing."

"What?"

"**Help them light the fires.**"

A jagged smile touched Su Huai’s lips.

"Since the system loves fake news, we’re going to give them news that’s even faker."

"We’re going to make every single bit of information they put out look like a 'The Wolf is Coming' cry."

"We’re going to shatter the public's confidence in the very concept of information."

"When no one believes anything the System says anymore... the System is dead."

Chen Mo stared at her.

He realized for the first time that this history-major girl had a streak of cold brutality that surpassed them all.

"Fine," Chen Mo said.

He turned to Fang Yu.

"Fang Yu, that 'Nonsense Generator' of yours... can you upgrade it?"

"To what?"

"To a **'Myth Generator'**."

Chen Mo gestured to the fake video on the screen.

"Since they can lie about me, I can lie about them."

"I want everyone in the city to receive a 'Highest Level' emergency alert."

"Something like... **ALIEN INVASION**? Or... **ZOMBIE OUTBREAK**?"

Fang Yu blinked, then rubbed his hands together with excitement.

"Now *that* is something I can do! I’ve got a library of Hollywood-tier CGI assets sitting right here!"

A-K leaned in. "Can I play a zombie? I’m great at physical acting!"

Fu Mu shook his head with a weary sigh.

"You’re playing with fire."

"No," Chen Mo said, taking back his phone and watching the fake version of himself sob. "We’re **Extinguishing Fire**."

"Using a bigger fire to burn out the one they lit for us."
