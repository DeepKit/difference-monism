**STATION 1: JUYONG PASS.**

On the ramparts, Commander Wang Deren was drenched in blood.

He had just hacked the leg off a Tatar scout’s horse, but his own shoulder had been sliced by a scimitar. Blood streamed down his iron plate, dripping onto the frozen bricks of the rampart.

"My Lord! We can't hold them!"

A lieutenant rushed over, kneeling before him in tears. "The East Gate is breached! The Tatar heavy cavalry is inside!"

Wang Deren wiped the blood from his eyes.

"We hold, even if we can't!"

He retreated to the command tent, his hand shaking as he took up the brush.

He had to write an urgent request for aid.

**"TATAR VANGUARD: FIFTY THOUSAND HEAVY CAVALRY. FIRST LINE OF DEFENSE BREACHED. ARROWS DEPLETED. RATIONS FOR THREE DAYS ONLY. REQUEST IMMEDIATE SUPPORT. EXTREME URGENCY."**

He pressed hard, each character looking as if it had been carved into the paper with a knife.

He sealed the envelope, attached three red feathers, and handed it to his only remaining courier.

"Brother," Wang Deren gripped the courier’s shoulder. "The lives of three thousand brothers depend on you. Get this there!"

The courier nodded heavily.

"The message lives while I breathe!"

He mounted his horse and charged into the white-out of the blizzard.

In that moment, the message was Truth. It carried blood, warmth, and the lethal chill of fifty thousand horsemen.

---

**STATION 2: XUANFU GARRISON.**

The courier ran his first horse to death and finally reached Xuanfu.

The Regional Commander of Xuanfu was drinking. He wore sable furs, cradling a dainty singing girl in his lap.

"Report—Urgent dispatch from Juyong Pass!"

The Commander set down his wine cup and took the letter.

He tore it open and glanced through.

"Fifty thousand?" The Commander’s brow furrowed.

He pushed the girl away and walked to the wall map.

"Has Wang Deren lost his nerve? Where would the Tatars get fifty thousand? Their main force is still in the deep north. This is thirty thousand at most."

"My Lord, the situation is dire..." The courier knelt on the floor, shivering from the cold.

"What’s the rush?"

The Commander stroked his chin.

He was thinking of a different problem.

If he reported fifty thousand, the court would send a massive army. And when a massive army arrived, they would audit the grain. They would audit the armory.

But the granaries in Xuanfu were already half empty. He had sold the grain to passing merchant caravans in exchange for silver.

If an Imperial Inspector arrived and found the bins empty, it was a death sentence.

"No. We can't report fifty thousand."

The Commander took up a brush.

"Say... thirty thousand Tatar raiders at the border."

He changed the "5" to a "3."

"Why, My Lord?" his clerk whispered.

"If we report thirty thousand, the court will only send a small detachment. Small detachments don't eat much grain. I can fudge the numbers easily," the Commander smiled. "Once the campaign is over, I’ll refill the silos. No one will ever know."

The courier didn't dare speak. He was just a runner.

He resealed the letter, changed horses, and kept running.

At this moment, the Truth had died once.

---

**STATION 3: DATONG PREFECTURE.**

The courier reached Datong.

The Governor of Datong was a civil official. He was currently hosting a poetry circle for the local gentry.

"Report—Urgent dispatch via Xuanfu, from Juyong Pass!"

The Governor took the letter, his frown deepening as he read.

"Thirty thousand? That’s still quite high."

He glanced at the gentry below. These men had all sent him gifts, hoping to travel to the capital for the upcoming Imperial Birthday Festival to present their treasures and seek favor.

"Gentlemen," the Governor sighed. "It seems we won't be attending the birthday festival this year. There is conflict at the border; we must remain at our posts."

A collective moan rose from the gentry.

"My Lord, that won't do!" a portly merchant stood up. "My jade Buddha is already packed and ready for His Majesty. If we don't go, all our preparations are for nothing!"

The Governor was conflicted.

If he reported thirty thousand, the Emperor would be furious, and the birthday celebrations would surely be canceled. His own performance review would be ruined.

"Perhaps... it’s not that serious?" the Governor mused to himself.

"The Tatars... they come every winter to pillage a few things. They leave once they’re full. This is likely just a few bandits looking for a warm meal."

He took up the brush.

He changed "Thirty thousand heavy cavalry" to "**MOBILE BANDITS AT THE BORDER.**"

"My Lord, this..." the clerk gasped. "Bandits and heavy cavalry are two very different things."

"What are you afraid of? Bandits are still enemies. As long as the pass hasn't fallen, it’s a minor matter," the Governor waved him off. "Seal it up. Send it to the capital with haste."

The courier took the letter.

He felt the letter grow lighter in his hand.

What was once a staggering army of fifty thousand was now "a few bandits."

But he didn't dare ask. He could only keep running.

At this moment, the Truth had died twice.

---

**STATION 4: THE MINISTRY OF WAR, BEIJING.**

The letter finally reached the hands of Registrar Zhao.

By then, the contents had become:

**"CIVILIAN ALTERCATION AT THE BORDER MARKET. A FEW BANDITS TOOK THE OPPORTUNITY TO CAUSE TROUBLE. TROOPS HAVE BEEN DISPATCHED TO DISPERSE THEM."**

Zhao looked at the letter and smiled.

"Just as I thought. Conflict in a golden age? Preposterous. Just a couple of peasants getting into a scrap."

He didn't even present the letter to the Minister.

Because this was a "Routine Security Incident," not something that needed to disturb the high command.

He took a vermilion brush and marked the page with two words:

**"NOTED."**

Then, he tossed the letter into the mahogany chest bound with the yellow seal.

And with that, the message was officially dead.

---

This is the game of "Telephone."

The original truth was a Tiger. It was fierce and ready to kill.

By the second station, to hide corruption, it became a pack of Wolves.
By the third station, to save a career, it became a pack of Dogs.
By the fourth station, to maintain a facade, it became a House Cat.

Every person in the chain had their own abacus.

Some for money, some for status, some simply to avoid a headache.

Each person only cut away a tiny piece of the truth. It seemed insignificant.

But added together, they cut away three thousand lives.

Juyong Pass fell.

Wang Deren died in battle. Three thousand defenders were annihilated.

But in the archives of the Ministry of War, it was recorded only as a "Brawl."

---

The Modern Day. The Safe House.

Su Huai closed the book on *Ming Dynasty Post Systems*.

"It’s horrifying," she said. "This is **Entropy Incarnate**."

"Information inherently degrades during transmission. But in a bureaucratic system, that loss is manually accelerated."

"Everyone adds a 'Filter' to the data. By the time it reaches the decision-maker’s ear, it has been distorted into a fairy tale."

A-K was eating instant noodles. He didn't understand entropy.

But he understood the story.

"This is just that game 'Telephone' we played as kids," A-K said.

"The teacher tells the first kid: 'I want an apple.' By the time it reaches the last kid, it’s: 'The teacher says she *is* an apple'."

They all laughed.

But the laughter died quickly, replaced by a heavy silence.

Because they thought of their own situation.

Chen Mo stared at the monitor.

Vision PR was purging the entire web. They were changing "Chemical Leak" to "Trace Element Spike."

"Modern society is the same," Chen Mo said.

He pulled up a PDF. It was a training manual Fang Yu had stolen from the PR firm.

**[THE THREE PRINCIPLES OF CRISIS MANAGEMENT]**

1.  **DOWNGRADE**: Call a "Catastrophe" an "Incident." Call "Death" a "Loss of Connection."
2.  **DECOUPLE**: Shift responsibility to a "Contractor" or a "Third Party."
3.  **FORGET**: Launch a new trending topic (like that cat) to bury the old one.

"See?" Chen Mo pointed. "They even have a standardized script template."

**[TEMPLATE A: ENVIRONMENTAL POLLUTION]**
*   *Original*: Toxic gas leak, villagers developing cancer.
*   *PR Script*: **"Tests indicate that environmental metrics in the surrounding area comply with National Standards (Note: referencing Industrial Zone standards, not Residential). No obvious correlation has been established."**

**[TEMPLATE B: SYSTEM FAILURE]**
*   *Original*: Algorithm kills a person, insurance claim rejected.
*   *PR Script*: **"Occasional system logic anomalies have occurred. A technical upgrade is underway. We always place user experience at the forefront."**

"This is the modern version of a 'Civilian Altercation'," Chen Mo said.

"They use professional jargon, complex logic, and seemingly objective data to build a massive labyrinth of lies."

"In this labyrinth, the truth isn't hidden; it’s **Diluted**."

"Diluted by countless excuses of 'compliance,' 'upgrades,' and 'contractors'."

---

"So what do we do?" Su Huai asked.

"We can't play the game of Telephone with them," Chen Mo said. "They own the phone lines. We’ll never win."

"Then what game do we play?"

"**The Megaphone.**"

Chen Mo pulled out his modified abacus.

"We bypass every middleman."

"Bypass the Commander, bypass the Governor, bypass Registrar Zhao."

"We shout directly at the Emperor—the entire city."

"How?"

"By using **Myths** to fight their Lies."

Chen Mo’s gaze had a hint of madness.

"Since they love telling stories, we’ll tell a bigger one."

"A story they can't Downgrade, Decouple, or Forget."

"We’re going to turn that cat into a Tiger."
