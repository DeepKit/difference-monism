# 第47集：漂流瓶（绝笔）

> **《未尽之算》·第47集·漂流瓶**
> **本卷进度：47/48 | Chen Mo Vitals: CRITICAL | Countdown: 10 HOURS**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: CARBON-BASED ORGANISM FAILURE DETECTED. RECOMMENDING DISPOSAL PROTOCOL.*

---

Level B3. The Shredding Workshop.

This was the crematorium of information. Every day, tons of "Sensitive Material" were pulverized, compressed, and baled here, turning into rigid, white cubes of silence.

Chen Mo pushed the heavy cart, every step feeling like a blade twisting in his lungs.

**Cough. Cough. Cough.**

He covered his mouth, but when he opened his hand, his palm was slick with dark blood.

Radiation sickness. It was the souvenir he’d earned at the end of Volume One, for forcing that reactor shut with his bare hands. The doctors said he had three months; it seemed the System didn't even want to give him that long.

His hair was gone, his teeth were loose, and his retinas had begun to detach, leaving his field of vision swimming with black, amoeba-like spots.

"Old Chen! Hurry it up! This batch from HR needs to be ash by eight o’clock!"

The foreman at the door shouted, checking his watch with an impatient scowl.

Chen Mo didn't answer. He could only hear the heavy, wet rasp of his own breathing, sounding like a pair of rusted bellows.

He knew this was the final stand.

He touched his pocket. There were the last hundred USB drives.

Legacy from A-K. Truths organized by Su Huai. The future calculated by Fu Mu.

This was **Kindling**.

And he had to deliver it before he fell.

---

**THE PLAN: MESSAGE IN A BOTTLE**

Chen Mo watched the massive conveyor belt.

Waste paper was fed into the shredder, turning into snow-like confetti, then compressed by a hydraulic ram into tight, dense cubes—**Recycled Pulp Bricks**.

These bricks were sent to paper mills outside the city or directly to landfills.

Under ordinary logic, it was a dead end.

But Chen Mo had found a vulnerability.

**The Density of the Pulp Brick.**

If he threw a USB drive directly into the shredder, the hydraulic pressure would crush the chip. But if... he tucked the drives into the **Gaps** of the already formed brick?

No, not just the gaps.

Chen Mo picked up a drive. With a trembling hand, he wrapped it in a thick layer of chewing gum—gum he’d been saving for a month, chewed until it was tasteless.

**The Buffer Layer.**

Then, as the foreman turned his head, Chen Mo jammed the "gum-ball" into the core of a newly pressed paper brick.

The brick was still warm. The gum softened, bonding the drive to the internal core of the bale.

One. Two. Three.

Chen Mo moved like a clumsy, dying thief, conducting a grand gamble in his final hours.

He bet that these bricks would break apart during transport.
He bet that a scavenger would find the hard lump and be curious.
He bet that in this city, there was still someone left who wanted to listen to the voices of the dead.

---

**DISSOLUTION OF THE FLESH**

As the 99th drive was sent away, Chen Mo’s legs finally gave out.

His knees buckled, and he collapsed onto the floor covered in paper dust.

The black spots in his vision grew, merging into a singular void that swallowed the harsh fluorescent lights.

The roar in his ears drowned out the screaming of the shredder.

He started to hallucinate.

He saw Zhang San.

The little coroner from the Song Dynasty, covered in blood, standing at the other end of the conveyor belt, watching him in silence.

Zhang San held a crimson-stained bone.
Chen Mo held a black USB drive.

A gaze that spanned a thousand years.

"Did you finish the calculation?" Zhang San asked.

"Not even close," Chen Mo laughed hoarsely, blood trickling from the corner of his mouth. "The debt... it’s too large."

"I couldn't finish it either," Zhang San sighed, throwing the bone into the shredder. "But I left the abacus behind."

"So did I."

Chen Mo touched his chest. Hanging there was a heavy weight.

It wasn't a USB drive. It was **the abacus with the chipped corner**.

This was his final token.
He couldn't leave this in a trash heap. He had to give it to someone.
Someone who could take over the reckoning.

---

**THE FINAL DELIVERY**

"Hey! Old man! What’s wrong with you? Don't you dare die here! It’s bad luck!"

The foreman spotted Chen Mo on the floor and walked over, cursing. He gave the body a sharp kick.

Chen Mo didn't move.

He was actually only thirty-five years old, but in the harsh light, he looked seventy.

"Dammit, is he really dead?" The foreman panicked, grabbing his radio. "Dispatch! No, send the cleanup crew! One of the temps just checked out!"

Chen Mo’s consciousness was fading fast.

In the final darkness, he heard a steady footfall.

The sound of leather soles treading on shredded paper. *Kr-chk. Kr-chk.*

It was rhythmic. Cold. Powerful.

Not a doctor. Not a janitor.

A **Actuary**. Or... a **Logistics Officer**.

Chen Mo forced open his one eye that could still see a sliver of light.

He saw the owner of the shoes.

A man in a sharp, black uniform, wearing gold-rimmed spectacles, holding an electronic tablet.

The man looked down at Chen Mo as if he were looking at a bag of defective merchandise.

He tilted his head, glancing at the ID badge on Chen Mo’s chest:

**ID: X-01**
**NAME: CHEN MO**
**STATUS: DECOMMISSIONED.**

The man made a checkmark on his tablet.

"Dispose of it," the man’s voice had no inflection.

Chen Mo wanted to smile.
He wanted to hand the abacus to this man.
But he no longer had the strength to raise his arm.

The abacus slipped from his coat, hitting the concrete floor.

*CLACK.*

The man in the black uniform stopped.

He looked down at the broken abacus.
The chipped corner, the blood of Chen Mo staining the wooden frame.

In the reflection of his polished black boots, the abacus looked entirely out of place.

The man frowned.

He bent down.

His hand reached for the abacus.

(End of Chapter 47)
