### 01. THE LOGISTICS OFFICER

Luo Sheng hated chaos.

As the "Minister of Logistics and Distribution" for the Social Security Bureau, his world was built from straight lines, spreadsheets, and progress bars.

To him, any unpredictable variable was nothing more than **"Loss (Entropy)."**

Now, he was staring down at a massive "Loss"—the cooling corpse of Old Chen, the waste paper disposal worker.

The body was cold. The death had been ugly—shriveled and curled like a salted shrimp. The floor was slick with irradiated blood.

"Minister, sir..." the janitor beside him hovered, uncertain. "What’s the protocol for this one?"

Luo Sheng adjusted his gold-rimmed spectacles. The lenses flashed with cold, sterile light.

"Follow the 'Biohazardous Waste Disposal Regulations'," his voice sounded like stainless steel tapping on glass. "Seal, bale, and send to the incinerator. Use Level 3 radiation protection. Deduct the expenses from his pension fund."

"Understood, sir."

The janitor began stuffing Chen Mo into a black body bag.

Luo Sheng turned to leave.
His time was valuable. He had a three-thousand-ton resource allocation table to audit before midnight.

Suddenly, his foot caught on something.

*CLACK.*

He looked down.

It was an abacus.
A wooden, paint-chipped, ancient eyesore of a tool with a missing corner. It lay where Chen Mo had fallen, stained with dark blood.

Luo Sheng frowned.
He was a master of logistics; he possessed a natural, professional contempt for such low-efficiency, archaic calculating tools.

"Garbage."

He raised his foot, intending to kick it aside.

But the moment the tip of his shoe touched the frame, he saw it.
In the gap of the chipped corner, a faint blue light pulsed.

Not a reflection. A **Signal LED**.

Luo Sheng froze.
He was a logistics man. He was hypersensitive to signal lights. That was an active power source.

This piece of junk contained electronics?

Driven by professional instinct (checking for contraband), he reached down and picked it up.

---

### 02. THE INHERITANCE

The abacus was heavy. The wood felt as if it had been injected with lead.

Luo Sheng pulled out a sanitizing wipe and fastidiously cleaned the blood from the frame.
He felt around the chipped corner, pressing a microscopic protrusion.

*BEEP.*

The abacus spoke.
But it wasn't the sound of sliding beads. It was a synthesized mechanical voice, transmitted via bone conduction through his fingertips.

**[BIOMETRIC RECOGNITION ACTIVE.]**
**[ACCESS PERMISSION: FAILED.]**
**[INITIATING GUEST MODE PROTOCOL.]**

Luo Sheng blinked.
This wasn't an abacus. It was a high-grade, encrypted terminal disguised as one. Where did a trash-sorter like Chen Mo get something like this?

Then, the voice continued. It was a recording.

*"Cough... cough..."*

It was Chen Mo’s voice. From the timber, recorded only hours before his death.

*"To the one who follows. If you’re hearing this, I’m logged off."*

*"Don't be so quick to toss this. Inside this frame are all the 'disappeared' accounts from this building."*

*"I know what you’re thinking. 'Not my problem.' You’re a screw in the machine. You don't want trouble."*

Luo Sheng gave a dismissive snort. The man had some self-awareness, at least. He prepared to shut the recording off and toss the thing back into the bin.

*"However,"*

The voice on the recording became eerily calm.

*"Have you ever actually audited your own books?"*
*"Minister Luo Sheng."*

Luo Sheng’s hand gave a violent twitch.
*He knew my name?*

*"I’ve seen your file. A logistics genius. Your 'Hive Dispatch System' saved the Bureau three billion dollars."*
*"But do you know where those three billion came from?"*
*"Go check Granary Number 73. The one you’re responsible for. Go see where the 'Loss' actually went."*

*"The abacus is yours. How you calculate the final sum... that’s up to you."*

The recording ended.

Luo Sheng stood in the empty, hollow basement, a chill creeping up his spine.

Granary 73.
That was his pride. Even during a catastrophe on the level of the Great Famine, his Granary 73 had guaranteed supply. The books were perfect. Unimpeachable.

How could there be a problem?

This had to be the final, delusional ramblings of a dying man. An attempt to drag someone down with him.

Luo Sheng threw the abacus into the trash bin.

"Madman."

He turned and walked away. The body bag had already been hauled off. The basement returned to its deathly silence.

---

### 03. THE SEED OF DOUBT

Back in his office. Luo Sheng opened his pride and joy: the "Hive Dispatch Grid."

Green lines flowed across the screen, representing the endless, smooth torrent of resources. Perfect. Efficient.

But as he stared at the screen, Chen Mo’s words wouldn't leave him.

*"Go check Granary Number 73."*

Luo Sheng’s fingers typed a few commands. He pulled up the live feed for Granary 73.

The screen showed: Maximum Capacity. Mountains of grain sacks stacked to the rafters.

"Hmph. Blustering nonsense."

Luo Sheng went to close the window.
Suddenly, his eye caught a detail.

The timestamp in the corner of the monitor.

**2026-04-01 12:00:01**
**2026-04-01 12:00:02**

The seconds were ticking. But the shadow of the worker in the background... **it hadn't moved an inch.**
Even the laziest worker has a micro-sway when standing for two seconds. But this man was a statue.

Luo Sheng squinted.
He was a pro.
It was a **Looped Video Feed**.

Someone had breached his security and replaced the footage.
Why? To hide the real inventory?

Was Granary 73... empty?

Luo Sheng felt a wave of suffocation. If 73 was empty, where did the tens of thousands of tons of grain on his books go? Where did that "saved" three billion actually end up?

Who had he, the "Logistics Genius," been shipping air for all this time?

No matter how much he wanted to avoid trouble, he could not tolerate this insult to his professional integrity.
He had climbed to this position because his calculations were flawless. Now, someone was telling him his equation had been wrong from the very first variable.

Luo Sheng stood up abruptly.
He sprinted out of his office, back down to the freezing basement.

The trash bin was still there.
He didn't care about the filth. He dove into the bin, throwing waste aside.

Found it.
The blood-stained abacus.

He gripped the frame tightly.
This time, there was no recording. Instead, he felt the abacus vibrate.

Through the chipped corner, he felt as if a pair of eyes were watching him.
Eyes from a thousand years ago, watching the fires in the granary.

**[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION: NEW USER CONNECTED.]**
**[ID: X-02 (LUO SHENG).]**
**[CURRENT TASK: AUDIT.]**

---

### 04. THE NEW BEGINNING

Luo Sheng tucked the abacus into his coat pocket.

He adjusted his tie and wiped the dust from his spectacles.
He was once again the cold, precise Minister of Logistics.

But he knew something had fundamentally shifted.
He was no longer a part of the machine. He was the machine’s **Auditor**.

"Chen Mo," he whispered to the empty air. "If this was your revenge, you’ve succeeded."

"I accept the debt."

He turned and walked toward the elevator.
The doors slid shut. The red floor numbers began to throb.

**B3 -> F1 -> F2...**

In his pocket, the abacus gave a soft, final sound.

*CLACK.*

The sound of a new countdown beginning.
