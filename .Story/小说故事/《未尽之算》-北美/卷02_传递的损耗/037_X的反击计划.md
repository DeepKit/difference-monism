The Safe House.

A-K sat at his terminal, a graveyard of empty instant noodle cups stacked by his feet. His eyes were shot through with red capillaries from staring at the screens too long, but his fingers danced across the keys with the frantic grace of a pianist in the middle of a crescendo.

"A-K, you’re splashed broth all over the keyboard," Su Huai reminded him.

"It’s fine. This thing’s waterproof."

A-K slurped down the last of his noodles without looking up.

On the screen, a green progress bar was inching toward completion. This was the "Nuclear Option" he’d spent the entire night building.

"Is it done?" Chen Mo asked.

"Done," A-K pointed at the screen. "I call it 'Bulls*** Generator 2.0'."

"Show me."

A-K hit Enter.

A dialog box popped up, asking for a sample of raw text.

A-K typed in the most mundane sentence imaginable: **"What's for dinner tonight?"**

Then, he clicked the "INJECT" button.

The screen flickered. The text transformed.

**`Original: What's for dinner tonight?`**
**`Injected: TONIGHT at [21:00], TARGET: [CAFETERIA]. Execute [TERMINATION] of Hunger Protocol.`**

Chen Mo blinked.

"What exactly does that do?"

"It’s **Keyword Poisoning**," A-K explained. "Vision PR’s system has a list of 'Sensitive Terms.' Anything containing those words is flagged as High Risk and sent for manual review."

"I’ve randomized the insertion of these terms into thousands of mundane chat logs."

"To a human, it’s just cringey, edgy nonsense. But to a machine? It looks like a **Terrorist Incident Manifesto**."

A-K demonstrated again.

Input: **"You asleep yet?"**
Output: **`TARGET is [DORMANT]. Monitoring system [OFFLINE]. Requesting [INFILTRATION].`**

Input: **"Gotta hit the restroom."**
Output: **`Proceeding to [EXCLUSION ZONE]. Release [BIO-WEAPON]. Countdown: 10 SECONDS.`**

Su Huai couldn't help but stifle a laugh.

"That is genuinely diabolical."

"It’s diabolical, but it works," A-K said with a smug grin. "Once this hits the grid, the System’s semantic analysis will overload instantly. It won't be able to tell a joke from a genuine threat."

"To play it safe, it’ll have to intercept everything and dump it into the manual review queue."

"And Vision PR only has five hundred human auditors."

"They could work until they go blind and they won't even scratch the surface."

---

"But," Fu Mu pushed up his glasses, "how do you get this out? There are only four of us. Even if we typed until our fingers fell off, we couldn't generate enough volume to matter."

"Who said we’re typing it?"

A-K gave a mysterious smirk.

He pulled up a chat group window.

The group name was long and chaotic: **"FREE DISCOUNT CODES & GROUP DEALS (500 Members)"**.

That was just one.

A-K’s sidebar was filled with hundreds of similar groups.

**"Part-time Daily Gigs"**
**"Stay-at-Home Mom Support Network"**
**"Student Task-Running Group"**
**"Gaming Boosters & Carry Services"**

These groups were the collective reservoir of the city’s "Low-Value" digital traffic. Students looking for pocket change, mothers taking care of kids, unemployed workers.

The System usually dismissed them as noise. In A-K’s eyes, they were an army.

"I don't have a botnet," A-K said. "Those are expensive and easy to trace. I have something more powerful than a botnet."

"What?"

"**Digital Red Envelopes (Cash Rewards).**"

A-K pulled a bank card from his pocket. It was the one Chen Mo had given him—Chen Mo’s entire life savings. A hundred thousand dollars.

"If I drop a cash reward into a group with a 'Keyphrase' requirement..."

"These people will share it like their lives depend on it to get the payout."

"They don't care what the message says. They only care about the few dollars in the envelope."

A-K looked at Chen Mo.

"Chen, are you sure about this? Once this hundred grand is gone, it’s not coming back. That’s your mother’s retirement fund."

Chen Mo stared at the card.

He thought of his mother’s vacant, wandering gaze in the nursing home.

If he didn't save Li Si, if he didn't get the truth out, what kind of world would his mother be living in?

"Spend it," Chen Mo said.

"Spend every cent."

"I want to buy every bit of noise this city can produce."

---

A-K took a deep breath.

His finger hovered over the Enter key.

This wasn't just a button. It was a detonator.

Once pressed, the entire city would become a massive "The Wolf is Coming" crime scene.

The System would be buried in false alarms. Auditors would collapse staring at screens filled with "Terminations" and "Infiltrations."

The *real* SOS—the audio recording of the dead man—would be hidden in this mountain of noise, walking right through the front gate.

"It’s called 'Muddying the Waters'," Su Huai said.

"No," Fu Mu corrected her. "It’s called **'Saturated Rescue.'**"

"To save one fish, we must turn the entire river into mud."

"Ready?" Chen Mo asked.

The trio nodded.

"Then let the storm break."

A-K hit Enter.

---

**[CASH REWARDS SENT.]**
**[REQUIRED KEYPHRASE: TONIGHT [ACTION], TARGET [SYSTEM], EXECUTE [TERMINATION].]**

Instantly.

Hundreds of groups erupted.

Tens of thousands of people clicked for the cash. Tens of thousands of people copied the keyphrase, pasting it to their Moments, their feeds, their group chats, every corner of the web.

Data flooded the backbone like a broken dam.

At the same time, Chen Mo’s phone pinged with a citywide (fake) public service announcement:

**"Temperature dropping. All citizens advised to [HIDE]. Cold front [INVASION] imminent. Warming [ACTION] initiated."**

Then, a food delivery note popped up on a random terminal:

**"Give me extra chili! Burn me alive! Execute [IMMOLATION]!"**

A ride-sharing comment:

**"Hurry up! [EVACUATION] in progress! Requesting [REINFORCEMENTS]!"**

The city’s entire linguistics system was being reconstructed in real-time.

Every mundane greeting became a wartime code.

The System was going insane.

Commander Wang Deren stood atop the beacon tower, watching his soldiers below.

They were filling black boxes with anything they could find.

Rocks. Leaves. Scraps of old cloth. In some, they even tossed the gnawed sheep bones from their last meal.

"Commander," the lieutenant asked, confused. "What are we doing? Why are we sending garbage to the Ministry of War?"

"Because those old bastards in the capital only recognize the box, not the man," Wang Deren said coldly.

"They love to audit? Fine. Let them audit until their eyes bleed."

"I am sending one hundred boxes."

"Every single one attached with three red feathers. Every one sealed with wax. Every one marked 'EXTREME URGENCY'."

"I’ll let Registrar Zhao break his fingers opening them and go blind reading them."

"By the time he’s been broken by this mountain of nonsense, he’ll treat the one true letter like a drowning man treats a straw."

The Commander picked up the hundredth box.

The only one containing the real message.

He handed it to Li Si.

"Go," the Commander said. "Turn the water into mud."

"Let those well-fed lords in the capital taste the meaning of **'Information Overload'**."
