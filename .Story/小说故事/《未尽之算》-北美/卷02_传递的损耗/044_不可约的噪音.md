I stood at the edge of the mob.

I watched the people clawing for the fallen cash. I watched the CEO standing high above, hurling bills into the wind like he was feeding pigeons.

I felt a wave of nausea so strong I almost doubled over.

Is this the "Public" we risked everything to wake?
Is this the "Moment of Truth" we bought with the total shutdown of our city’s digital life?

Under the deafening roar of currency hitting the street, the reporter’s cry for help had been buried once more.

"Let’s go," Su Huai said, tugging lightly at my sleeve. "It’s over. There’s nothing left to save."

"Wait."

I pulled the abacus from my coat.

In the middle of the cacophony—the shouting, the sirens, the high-pitched screams of greed—the single white bead on the frame suddenly pulsed with light.

It had caught a frequency.

It was a fragment of ambient noise from the very end of the dead reporter’s audio file—a part that even I had dismissed as interference.

I put on my headphones and cranked the volume to the limit.

After the gunshot. After the sound of the body hitting the dry earth.

I heard a voice. It was so light, so faint, it was almost a ghost of a sound.

It was the very last thing he had said into the mic before his life flickered out.

**"Don't... hate them. They just... don't know..."**

I froze.

Who was he talking to?
Was he talking to the man who had pulled the trigger? Or to the masses who had so easily been misled?
Or... was he talking to the System itself?

In his final heartbeat, he had chosen forgiveness.

That act of forgiveness was the only **Irreducible Noise** in this loud, chaotic, and ravenous world.

It was faint, but it possessed a clarity that pierced through every firewall, every barrier, every stack of money. It was the part of humanity that could not be computed, could not be optimized, and could not be erased.
