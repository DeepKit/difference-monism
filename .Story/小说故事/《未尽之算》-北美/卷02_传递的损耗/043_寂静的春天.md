Outside the Vision PR headquarters, it was a sea of people.

An angry crowd milled through the streets, clutching their phones, livestreaming the chaos, and screaming slogans.

"Come out, you liars!"
"Give us the truth!"

The glass on the ground floor had already been shattered. Molotov cocktails were being hurled into the lobby, lighting the marble in stuttering bursts of orange.

The CEO stood at the floor-to-ceiling windows of his penthouse office, looking down at the mob.

He wasn't afraid. He simply found it amusing.

"Look at them," he said to the secretary beside him. "The very targets of our daily 'Optimization' routines."

"Yesterday, they were watching cat videos. Today, they want to commit murder. Tomorrow? We’ll drop a few rewards in their laps, and they’ll be back to shouting 'Our CEO is the best!'"

The CEO opened a heavy floor-safe.

It was packed tight with bricks of cold hard cash.

"Throw it down," he said.

"Sir?"

"Throw it! Let them fight for it! The moment they start grabbing for the bills, they’ll forget all about some dead reporter."

The secretary opened the window and began hurling bundles of cash out into the night.

The red bills fluttered through the air like a localized storm of crimson snow.

The crowd erupted.

The slogans stopped. The brick-throwing stopped. Every head in the mob snapped down, people diving into the slush to scramble for the money.

Someone’s hand was stepped on and snapped as another person lunged for a bill. Someone else dropped their phone—the one they’d been using to "demand justice"—to free up both hands for the cash.

On a massive nearby screen, the reporter’s video was still looping, a silent ghost in the machinery. But nobody was looking anymore.

**The Truth is expensive, but it’s rarely worth more than a hundred-dollar bill.**

The CEO smiled, lighting a cigarette with a steady hand.

"See? That... is Public Relations."
