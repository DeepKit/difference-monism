Vision PR Headquarters.

The building sat in the heart of the city’s CBD—sleek, vibrant, and covered entirely in glass curtain walls. But two levels beneath the polished marble of the lobby lay an undisclosed "Factory."

Fu Mu and I had slipped inside using forged badges.

I’d expected something out of *The Matrix*—rows of black code and agents in tailored suits.

I was wrong.

It looked like an internet cafe. One of those cheap, grimy ones located on the outskirts of town.

Hundreds of computers were crammed into a windowless hall. The air was a stagnant soup of instant noodles, stale sweat, smelly feet, and the suffocating heat of unventilated hardware.

Hundreds of young people sat at the terminals, wearing headphones and staring blankly as their fingers flew across the keyboards.

Their fingers moved with the speed of a nervous tic.

"What are they doing?" I whispered.

"They’re 'Optimizing' the world," Fu Mu said, gesturing toward the nearest screen.

---

The kid at the monitor looked barely twenty. His hair was slick with oil, and dark circles bruised the skin beneath his eyes. On his desk sat a can of Red Bull and a half-empty pack of cigarettes.

His screen was a backend management dashboard. The interface was crude: "Negative Information Pool" on the left, "Positive Information Pool" on the right.

His task was simple:

Whenever a post with "Sensitive Keywords" (e.g., Chemical Plant, Pollution, SOS) appeared on the left, he clicked a red button: "Fold."
Whenever a "Positive Energy" post (e.g., Scenery, Food, Celebrities) appeared on the right, he clicked a green button: "Pin."

He didn't need to think. The system had already color-coded the world for him.

Red was Bad. Green was Good.

He only had to behave like Pavlov's dog—click left for red, click right for green.

*Click. Click. Click.*

The sound of mice hitting pads was so dense it sounded like a hailstorm.

"Five cents a click," Fu Mu muttered. "A skilled worker can pull in fifty bucks a day. Night shift bonuses can get them up to fifteen hundred a month."

"For fifty dollars?" I looked at the young man’s numbed expression.

"Yeah. For fifty dollars, they’ll erase a disaster from the internet. They’ll turn a man’s scream into silence."

"Do they even know what they’re deleting?"

"They know. They just don't care." Fu Mu’s voice was like ice. "To them, it’s just a task. Deleting a life feels no different than deleting a spam ad for gambling."

"This is the **Banality of Evil**."

"We don't need demons. We just need a legion of ordinary people who give up thinking to make rent."

---

In the exact center of the hall hung a gargantuan LED screen.

**"REAL-TIME GLOBAL OPINION HEAT MAP."**

It displayed the top ten trending topics and their "Sentiment Bias" (Red for Angry, Green for Happy).

1.  **The Billionaire’s Cat is Found!** (Green, Heat: 10M+)
2.  **Celebrity Infidelity Scandal!** (Red, Heat: 8M+)
3.  **This Milk Tea is Pure Fire!** (Green, Heat: 5M+)
4.  **Property Prices Rising? Experts weigh in...** (Yellow, Heat: 3M+)
...

And Wang Wu—the dead reporter, the man who’d gasped for life in the Dead Zone? He wasn't even in the top thousand.

Occasionally, a tiny red dot would flicker—a post about "Northwest Pollution."

Before anyone even noticed it, one of the kids below would click it away.

It was like a game of whack-a-mole.

*Paff.* The red dot vanished.

"The truth?" I asked. "Does it just... disappear?"

"No," Fu Mu pointed toward the bottom of the screen. "The truth is still there."

"Where?"

"On **Page 2**."

Fu Mu smiled, a cold, jagged expression.

"The second page of the search results. The 'Folded' section of social media. The truth is all there. But no one looks for it."

"**The best place to hide a leaf is in the forest. The best place to hide a single truth is in a mountain of garbage.**"

"The System doesn't need to delete every truth. It just needs to drown you in an ocean of noise—cats, celebrities, milk tea."

"When you’ve spent two hours scrolling through TikTok, laughing like a lobotomized idiot, are you going to care about a reporter who died in the desert?"

I stared at the screen.

I felt like I was watching a massive magic trick.

The magician (The System) waved a vibrant silk scarf (The Cat) in his left hand to draw everyone's eyes.
While in his right, he quietly strangled the pigeon (The Truth).

---

Suddenly, the screen flickered.

A new command flashed in bold, urgent red:

**[URGENT TASK: PURGE KEYWORD "CHEN MO." ASSOCIATED TERMS: BUREAU, LUNATIC, RADIATION.]**

I froze.

That was my name.

Instantly, the hall became a cacophony of typing.

Hundreds of kids began their work simultaneously.

They were posting.

*   *"I heard that Chen Mo guy from the Bureau is a psycho. He was disciplined for sexual harassment last month!"*
*   *"He blew up the server just to scam the insurance! He deserves the chair!"*
*   *"Inside info: Chen Mo is actually being paid by foreign entities to sabotage the system!"*
*   *"Dox him! Post his family’s address!"*

In seconds, thousands of lies flooded the grid like a toxic tide.

The heat map began to shift.

**#ChenMoTheTerrorist** began to climb the charts at visible speed.

I watched the monitor.

My name was being buried in filth. My rebellion was being twisted into a crime. My attempt to save lives was being rebranded as a slaughter.

I stood there, watching hundreds of people curse me, and they didn't even know who I was.

They were doing it for five cents.

"See that?" Fu Mu clapped me on the shoulder. "That’s the modern echo."

"In the old days, Registrar Zhao could only burn a letter."

"In the modern day, they can burn *you*."

"They don't need to kill you. They just need to rot your reputation until you're a pariah. By the time they're done, even if every word you say is gospel, no one will believe a syllable."

I clenched my fists.

Fury.

A fire I’d never felt before roared in my chest.

"Let’s go," I said to Fu Mu.

"Where?"

"Back to the Safe House."

"To do what?"

"**To strike back.**"

I looked back at the massive screen one last time, at the kids still clicking.

"If they want to play with traffic, I’ll play a bigger game."

"If they want to drown the truth in garbage, I’ll give them a tsunami."

"I’m going to show them what a **Real Flood** looks like."
