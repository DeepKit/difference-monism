# 第39集：饱和攻击

> **《未尽之算》·第39集·饱和攻击**
> **本卷进度：15/24 | System Misjudgment Rate: 99.9% | 系统倒计时：971集**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: CRITICAL. MANUAL AUDIT QUEUE OVERFLOW. WAIT TIME: ∞. INITIATING NETWORK BREAKER PROTOCOL.*

---

Vision PR Headquarters. The Audit Hall.

This place was originally the quietest room in the city. Hundreds of auditors sat in silence, wearing noise-canceling headphones, clicking their mice like rhythmic machines.

Now, it was a bazaar.

Alarms wailed in a discordant symphony. Red alert lamps flashed at every workstation.

"Supervisor! My backend just exploded!" a young auditor ripped off his headset, shouting to be heard. "It’s all High-Risk flags! A thousand new entries every second!"

"Same here! System Alert: Detecting citywide 'Organized Insurrection'!"

"My terminal says someone’s planting a bomb at City Hall! Do we call 911?!"

The Supervisor sprinted to the central data wall.

The heat map was a solid, throbbing red. The entire city looked like a massive battlefield. Dense clusters of ruby-red dots covered every street, every neighborhood, every apartment block.

He randomly clicked on several "High-Risk" entries:

1.  **User A**: ACTION tonight. Target: TOILET. Execute DETONATION. (Note: Food poisoning).
2.  **User B**: Enemy INVASION. Requesting REINFORCEMENTS. (Note: Playing League of Legends).
3.  **User C**: Coordinates LOCKED. Prepare for ANNIHILATION. (Note: Killing a mosquito).
4.  **User D**: All hands EVACUATE. Bring the NUKES. (Note: Moving house, bring the rice cooker).

---

"Madness... it’s absolute madness..." the Supervisor whispered to himself.

In the System’s algorithm, these were all blueprints for a terrorist attack. Every keyword—Action, Detonation, Invasion, Annihilation—triggered the highest risk coefficients.

By protocol, every single one had to be manually reviewed and reported to the Internet Police.

But now, tens of thousands of these messages were flooding in every second.

The auditors' fingers were cramping. Over a dozen mice had been clicked into oblivion.

"We can't keep up!" a female auditor started to sob. "I finish one and a hundred more pop up!"

People started to vomit. One man collapsed directly onto his keyboard, unconscious from the sensory overload.

This was the reality of a **DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) Attack**.

No hacking required. No botnets.

You only needed to mobilize enough ordinary people to say enough nonsense.

When the tide of noise swept over the levees of compute power, the system simply cracked.

---

"SHUT IT DOWN!"

The CEO burst into the hall, roaring with a raw, hysterical fury. His tie was lopsided, his hair a mess; he looked like a cornered beast.

"Shut down the keyword library! Pull the filter nets!"

"We can't, sir!" the Technical Director broke in, frantic. "If we disable the keyword library, the *actual* harmful information—the reporter’s video, Li Si’s SOS—will slip through! If the higher-ups investigate, we’re dead!"

"Then what?!" the CEO grabbed the Director by the collar. "The servers are going to catch fire at this rate!"

"There’s only one way... **Scorched Earth**."

The Technical Director gritted his teeth and pointed to a large red button on the master console.

This was the **CIRCUIT BREAKER**.

"Enable 'Silent Mode.' Intercept every single message containing a sensitive keyword. Truth, lie, joke—it doesn't matter. None of it passes. We turn the entire city’s network into read-only mode."

The CEO froze.

"Total blackout?"

"Yes. Better to kill ten thousand innocents than let one threat through."

The CEO’s grip loosened.

He watched the frantic, throbbing red dots on the map.

"Kill it," he said, his voice cold.

The Technical Director slammed the red button.

*CLICK.*

The hall went silent.

The red lights on the screens vanished.

Every outgoing message in the city was intercepted. The metropolitan network became a stagnant pool of dead data.

Whether you wanted to send "HELP" or "Have you eaten?", it was gone. Struck from the record.

The CEO exhaled, a long, shaky breath. He slumped into a chair and wiped the sweat from his brow.

"That’s better. If no one can speak, there is no risk."

He thought he had won.

But he didn't know that this was exactly what Chen Mo had been waiting for.

---

The Archives Room, Social Security Bureau.

"They tripped the breaker," Fu Mu said, looking at the dead screens. "Nothing is getting out now. Not even the nonsense."

"Good," Chen Mo smiled.

He pulled the abacus from his jacket.

"They think they’ve blocked every road, so they think they’ve blocked the truth."

"But they forgot one thing."

"When the main highway is closed, every eye in the world turns to the only side-path left."

Chen Mo opened the reporter’s audio file and launched it through the **"Dark-Net Channel"** A-K had constructed—a mesh network that utilized smart-home Bluetooth protocols to leapfrog from one device to another, like a digital virus.

This channel was narrow. In ordinary times, it was too slow to be useful.

But against the backdrop of a total citywide silence, it was the only source of sound.

**When the world is silent, even a whisper is deafening.**
