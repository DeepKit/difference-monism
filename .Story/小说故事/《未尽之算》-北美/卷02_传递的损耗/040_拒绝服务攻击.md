# 第40集：拒绝服务攻击

> **《未尽之算》·第40集·拒绝服务攻击**
> **本卷进度：16/24 | Network Connectivity: 0% (Mesh Mode) | 系统倒计时：970集**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: BACKBONE DISCONNECTED. NODE ISOLATION ACTIVE. ANALOG SURVIVAL MODE ENGAGED.*

---

8:00 PM.

The city plunged into darkness.

It wasn't a power outage; it was a digital blackout.

The "Circuit Breaker" operation initiated by Vision PR had triggered a massive anomaly in the municipal backbone. To prevent the "virus" from spreading to national servers, the service providers had severed all routing to the metropolitan area.

Everywhere, people stood on street corners, clutching their phones, raising them high into the air in a desperate search for a signal.

They looked like a legion of soul-less zombies, drifting aimlessly.

"Got anything?"
"Nothing. 4G, 5G, public WiFi—it’s all dead."
"Great. I was in the middle of a limited-edition drop. That bag’s gone now."
"I was on a video call with my mom..."

Panic began to seep into the silence.

In this hyper-informed era, losing the internet was like losing oxygen. People suddenly realized that without that glowing glass rectangle to guide them, they didn't even know how to live.

In the subway stations, massive lines formed because no one could scan their QR codes to enter.
In the department stores, checkout counters were piled high with abandoned merchandise because phone payments were offline.
On the roads, ride-share drivers circled like blind flies, unable to navigate without their GPS.

There were no riots. No looting.

There was only **Confusion**.

A massive, silent, suffocating fog of confusion.

People stood in place, not knowing where to walk, not knowing what to do next.

They were conditioned to be pushed by algorithms, guided by navigation, and fed by information streams. Now that the feeder had vanished, they were abandoned infants.

---

The Basement, Social Security Bureau.

"This is the ultimate 'Denial of Service' attack," Fu Mu said, looking out at the dazed crowds through the window. "The system has denied everyone. And everyone has denied reality."

Chen Mo said nothing.

He looked at his phone. The screen displayed "No Service."

"Turn on the Bluetooth."

He broadcast the command over the Bureau’s internal PA system.

A-K initiated the backup **"Mesh-Net Protocol."**

It utilized the Bluetooth functionality of every phone within range to build a temporary, decentralized Mesh network.

No base stations. No servers.

As long as two people stood close enough, the data could hop from one device to the next.

*PING.*

Su Huai’s phone chirped.

Then Fu Mu’s.

Then a passerby outside the window.

Device to device, like ripples expanding across a pond, the network began to "wake up."

The city breathed again.

But this newly awakened network was not the one they were used to.

---

Chen Mo watched the data flow on the backend.

Without Vision PR’s audits, without keyword filtering.

This was absolute, unrefined freedom.

But he didn't see a thoughtful debate on truth.

He saw **Slander**. He saw **Malevolence**.

*   *"Where does that PR firm CEO live? I’m going to go hack him to pieces!"*
*   *"Dox that auditor! I’ve got his photo right here!"*
*   *"They definitely killed that reporter! We want blood for blood!"*

And even wilder rumors:

*   *"I heard this is the precursor to a bio-hazard outbreak!"*
*   *"Go buy salt! Buy water! Hoard everything!"*
*   *"The government has abandoned us!"*

Without the System’s suppression, the darker side of human nature erupted like a flood.

People were sharing the reporter’s video frantically, but they didn't care about the dead man. They cared about **"Finding a living target for our rage."**

They needed a scapegoat.

A trash can where they could dump all their frustrations, anxieties, and the generic failures of their own lives.

Vision PR became that trash can.

Chen Mo saw messages being coordinated to lay siege to the PR firm’s tower.

"Is this what you wanted?" Fu Mu asked him.

He pointed to a message on the terminal:

**[ASSEMBLE! 9:00 PM tonight at the Vision PR Tower! BRING WEAPONS!]**

"I just wanted them to see the truth," Chen Mo said.

"You overestimated them," Fu Mu said coldly. "**The masses don't need truth. They only need an excuse.**"

"Before, the System fed them 'Soma'—cats, celebrities, milk tea—and they stayed quiet."

"Now, you’ve ripped the pacifier out, and they’ve turned into beasts."

"They won't stop to think about why the reporter died. They’ll only think about how much they want to smash that building."

Chen Mo watched the heat map throb.

The red of anger was gathering into a lethal current.

This current would wash away Vision PR, but it would also drown the innocent people inside.

He realized suddenly: **That cold, heartless audit system... was actually a levee.**

It held back the truth, yes. But it also held back the flood.

And now, the levee had broken.

---

"We have to stop them," Chen Mo said.

"How?" A-K asked. "The internet’s gone. Only our Bluetooth mesh works. You want to send out a PSA and tell everyone to go back to sleep?"

"No."

Chen Mo shook his head.

"We need to give this flood a new outlet."

"What outlet?"

"**Li Si.**"

Chen Mo pointed to the GPS coordinates still flickering in the dead zones of the Northwest.

"Broadcast Li Si’s location."

"Tell them: There is a living human being out there, waiting for us to save him."

"We’re going to turn their rage into a **Rescue Operation**."

Chen Mo’s fingers flew across the keyboard.

**[URGENT: THE TRUTH VISION PR TRIED TO HIDE!]**

**[IT’S NOT A CONSPIRACY! IT’S A HUMAN LIFE!]**

**[THAT REPORTER DIED TRYING TO SAVE A MESSENGER TRAPPED IN THE DESERT! THE MESSENGER IS STILL ALIVE! COORDINATES: 300KM OUTSIDE JIAYU PASS!]**

**[HE IS OUR BROTHER! OUR FATHER! HE IS EVERY ORDINARY PERSON DRIVING ON THE ROAD!]**

**[WHO IS NEARBY? PULL HIM OUT!]**

*CLICK. SEND.*

---

The message rippled through the Bluetooth mesh like an electric current.

On the streets, the crowds stopped.

Those who were reaching for bricks to throw at the tower paused.

They looked at their phones.

They looked at the photo of the courier—exhausted, face caked in dust and blood, but still smiling.

They looked at the words "HELP."

A different emotion, long buried, began to stir in their chests.

It wasn't anger.

It was **Empathy**.

"This is too much..."
"He almost died just to deliver a package?"
"I’m a long-haul driver too. I know that road!"

Voices began to rise in the chat groups.

"Brothers on the Northwest route! Anyone driving empty?!"
"I’m in Lanzhou! I’m heading out now!"
"I’m in Jiuquan! I’ve got medicine and supplies!"

On the map, the red clusters around the Vision PR tower began to break apart.

They transformed into single, blue points of light, all moving toward the Northwest.

The rescue caravans were forming.

Chen Mo watched the screen.

His eyes grew wet.

"See that?" he said to Fu Mu.

"The masses might be ignorant sometimes. But they are also **Kind**."

"As long as you give them a direction that matters, they can build a miracle."

Fu Mu pushed up his glasses and said nothing.

But the look in his eyes as he watched the monitor had softened.

"Maybe you’re right," Fu Mu whispered. "Sometimes, chaos is its own kind of order."
