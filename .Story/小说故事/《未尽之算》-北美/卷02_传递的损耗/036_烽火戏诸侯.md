# 第36集：烽火戏诸侯

> **《未尽之算》·第36集·烽火戏诸侯**

---

The Safe House.

Chen Mo, Su Huai, Fu Mu, and A-K were huddled around the monitor.

On the screen were video clips Fang Yu had scraped from a Hollywood asset library:

*   **Clip A**: A gargantuan UFO hovering over the metropolitan skyline, firing laser beams into city landmarks.
*   **Clip B**: A swarm of zombies rushing through a subway station, tearing at commuters.
*   **Clip C**: A pale-faced news anchor reading an "EARLY WARNING: UNKNOWN VIRUS OUTBREAK! SHELTER IN PLACE!"

"This... this looks too fake, doesn't it?" A-K scratched his head. "I mean, even I can tell it’s CGI."

"It’s *supposed* to be fake," Chen Mo said, his hands moving quickly through his video editing software.

"We don't need *everyone* to believe it. We only need the System to have no choice but to believe it."

"Meaning what?"

"The System’s algorithm is built on 'Keywords' and 'Image Identification.' Once the network is flooded with thousand of videos tagged with 'Zombie,' 'Virus,' and 'Invasion,' the System automatically triggers the highest level of **Public Safety Alert (SSS-Class)**."

"It flags these data packets as priority one and pushes them straight to the Emergency Monitoring Bureau."

"The Bureau *has* to verify them. They can't gamble that it’s a hoax. They can't afford a false negative."

"It’s just like the story of the Beacon Fires. The lords knew it was probably a trick, but they didn't dare *not* come. What if the war was real this time?"

Chen Mo hit the Enter key.

**[VIDEO SYNTHESIS COMPLETE.]**
**[INJECTION NODES: ALL CITYWIDE OUTDOOR LED SCREENS, SUBWAY TVS, BUS MEDIA DISPLAYS.]**

"Fang Yu, is the backdoor open?"

"Done," Fang Yu’s fingers danced across the secondary terminal. "I’ve breached the central ad-distribution hub. With one command, thirty thousand screens across the city will broadcast this simultaneously."

Chen Mo took a deep, steadying breath.

"Launch it."

---

The City Square.

It was the peak of the evening rush hour. A human ocean moved through the crosswalks.

High above, a massive LED screen was currently looping a famous actress’s shampoo commercial.

Suddenly, the image flickered.

Black screen.

Then, a piercing, high-pitched alarm wailed through the square.

*BZZZZT—BZZZZT—*

The screen flashed back to life. But the smiling actress was gone.

In her place was a pale, terrified news anchor. The background was a wall of fire.

"URGENT EMERGENCY NEWS!" the anchor’s voice wavered. "Unknown lifeforms are attacking the city! All citizens are advised to find immediate shelter! Repeat! This is NOT a drill!"

The camera cut away.

Zombies sprinting through subway cars. UFOs opening fire from of the clouds.

The crowd froze.

Every single person stopped in their tracks, their heads tilting back, eyes wide, staring at the screens.

"Is... is this real?"
"It looks like a movie, right?"
"But the news channel’s watermark is legitimate!"

Dozens of people pulled out their phones to check for confirmation.

But in that instant, the screens in the subway stations changed too. The displays on the city buses followed suit.

Even the little order-screens at the bubble tea shops, the department store display cases, the elevator ad-pads...

Every screen in the city was broadcasting the same nightmare.

**Zombies. Viruses. Invasion.**

Panic began to spread like spilled ink.

If it were just one screen, people would assume a prank. But if it was *every* screen?

This was the visual version of "A Lie Told Often Enough."

When the entire world tells you the wolf is at the door, you have no choice but to start locking it.

---

Vision PR Headquarters.

Alarms shrieked through the monitoring hall.

"What is happening?!" the CEO roared as he burst into the control room.

"Sir! It’s absolute chaos!" the Technical Director pointed at a data wall. "The entire network is trending with 'Zombie Outbreak'! The heat index has shattered the records!"

"It’s a hoax! It’s a hack!"

"We *know* it’s a hoax! But the System doesn't!"

The Technical Director was drenched in sweat.

"The System’s 'Public Safety Protocol' has been fully engaged. It’s aggressively scraping every one of these videos—analyzing, comparing, tracing."

"Because the volume is so high—delivered through every city-owned screen—the System’s core compute is maxed out!"

"Then issue a debunking! Now!"

"We can't!" the Director shouted in despair. "Our debunking post was buried the millisecond it hit the grid. Drowned in the ocean of zombie footage."

"And... our auditors are losing it."

The CEO turned around.

The hall full of young auditors, who usually worked like silent machines, had stopped.

They were staring at their own phones, looking out the windows at the city screens, their faces contorted with terror.

"Sir... my mom’s calling me to ask if it’s real..."
"My girlfriend is crying on the train..."
"We need to leave! We need to get out of the city!"

The lines had broken.

The CEO slumped into his chair.

He watched a digital zombie sprint across the monitor.

He knew it wasn't a zombie. It was Chen Mo’s fist.

Chen Mo had used an absurd lie to punch through the "Reality" they had so carefully constructed.

---

The Safe House.

"The effect is even better than projected," Fu Mu said, eyes on the diagnostic terminal. "The System’s cpu occupancy is at 95%. The Monitoring Bureau has called an emergency cabinet meeting."

"What are they investigating?" A-K asked.

"They're investigating whether there are *actually* zombies," Fu Mu chuckled. "As ridiculous as it is, protocol dictates they must send human assets to the 'Event Sites' to verify."

"And every time they verify a null-event, they waste massive amounts of human capital and time."

"There are hundreds of 'Outbreak Nodes' (screens) across the city. They’ll run themselves to death trying to keep up."

This was the modern version of "Fooling the Lords."

Chen Mo had lit ten thousand beacon fires.

The System was forced to send ten thousand armies.

When they arrived, they would find only a screen playing a movie.

What would the armies think?

They would be furious. Exhausted. They would begin to doubt the very ground they stood on.

Most importantly, their "Trust Value" would zero out.

"Next time," Chen Mo said, his eyes on the chaos. "The next time we send a *real* SOS, the System may not even bother to look."

"Then what?" Su Huai asked.

"That is exactly what we want."

Chen Mo turned to them.

"When the System stops trusting its own data streams, it will disable the automated filtering mechanisms."

"It will regress to its most primitive state: **MANUAL MONITORING**."

"It will send an actual human to listen."

"And that... that is our window."

Chen Mo pulled up the hard drive containing the recording of the reporter's final breaths.

"We’re going to pipe this audio directly into the listener’s ear."
