Modern Day. Chen Mo, clutching Lin Xiaocao to his chest, sprinted through the emergency stairs of the Social Security Bureau.

The footsteps behind him were gaining ground.

"Catch him! Don't let him get away!"

"Seal the Exit in Zone B!"

Alarms blared across the entire building. Red emergency lights rotated overhead, turning the corridors into the likeness of a hemorrhaging artery.

Chen Mo felt like his lungs were about to burst. He was a lifelong office drone; his stamina had long since withered. But Lin Xiaocao was light in his arms—frighteningly light, like a bundle of dried straw.

"Uncle, are we playing hide and seek?" Lin Xiaocao asked from his embrace.

"Yes, hide and seek," Chen Mo wheezed. "If we make it outside, we win."

"What’s outside?"

"There’s light. There’s wind. And... a place where nobody needs to get shots."

Chen Mo lunged toward a heavy fire door.

He shoved with all his might.

It didn't budge.

An oversized electronic lock hung on the door, its red light glaring.

**【系统封锁。权限不足。】**
**[System Lockout. Insufficient Privileges.]**

"Damn it!" Chen Mo kicked the door with a roar of frustration.

The footsteps had already reached the corner.

He was trapped.

At that moment, a violent wave of dizziness washed over Chen Mo.

That familiar *click, clack* sounded again.

The fire door began to warp and shift colors. The red emergency light transformed into a crimson lantern.

The cold metal of the door became heavy, ancient wood.

He was no longer Chen Mo.

He was Zhang San.

---

The Song Dynasty. Kaifeng Prefecture.

Zhang San woke with a start, drenched in a cold sweat.

He sat bolt upright, gasping for air.

Outside, the watchman’s drum struck three times. *Zi Shi San Ke*—the third quarter of the Hour of the Rat.

He had just had a dream.

He dreamt he was running, clutching the funeral urn containing the ashes of the girl in the red coat. Behind him, a swarm of Imperial Palace Guards in flying-fish robes (飞鱼服) were in pursuit.

He ran and ran until he reached the city gates.

The gates were sealed tight.

Across the wood hung a massive strip of paper, two bloody characters written upon it:

**“死局” (Si Ju — Death Trap).**

Zhang San wiped the cold sweat from his forehead.

"Just a dream..." he whispered to comfort himself.

But he knew it wasn't. It was an omen.

Ever since he had secretly excavated those ashes from the crematorium, fear had coiled around his neck like a venomous snake.

He had hidden the urn under his bed. Every night, he could hear a faint scratching sound coming from within the clay.

Like fingernails scraping against the walls of the jar.

"Don't be afraid," Zhang San whispered to the floor. "Just endure a little longer. Once I buy the red umbrella, I will rectify your injustice."

*Thump, thump, thump.*

Suddenly, a knock came at his door.

Soft. Rhythmic.

Three longs, two shorts.

Zhang San’s heart leaped into his throat.

Who would be knocking at this hour?

"Who’s there?" He gripped the iron ruler beneath his pillow, his voice trembling.

"San’er, are you awake?"

An aged, frail voice filtered through the wood.

Zhang San let out a breath. It was his mother.

He quickly climbed out of bed and opened the door.

A hunched old woman stood in the doorway. Her eyes were milky and blind, sunken deep into her sockets. She leaned on a bamboo cane.

"Mother, it’s so late. Why aren't you sleeping?"

"I couldn't sleep." The old woman felt her way into the room. "My heart is racing. I feel as if something terrible is coming."

She caught Zhang San’s hand. Her hands, like withered bark, were deathly cold.

"San’er, have you caused trouble out there?"

"No, Mother," Zhang San forced a smile. "I’m just a lowly *wuzuo*. What trouble could I cause? I just examine bodies and fill out forms."

"Then why do you smell of it?"

The old woman leaned in closer, sniffing the air.

"Smell of what?"

"The scent of burning. And... the scent of the dead."

Zhang San’s heart skipped a beat.

It was the smell of the crematorium. The smell of the ash. He thought he had washed it away, but the stench had seeped into his very marrow.

"Mother, that’s... that’s just the smell of the charcoal fires at the Bureau."

"No." The old woman shook her head. "That is the smell of a grieving soul. I may be blind, but my nose knows the truth."

She gripped his hand with surprising strength.

"San’er, listen to me. Quit. This trade damages your soul’s merit (损阴德). Let’s go back to the village. Even if we farm, even if we beg, it's better than dealing with the dead every single day."

Zhang San looked at his mother’s clouded eyes.

He wanted to weep.

He wanted to go back to the village, too. He wanted a life where he rose with the sun and rested with the moon.

But he couldn't go back.

He was already too deep in the system. He knew too much.

If he resigned now, Registrar Zhou would never let him go. Only the dead can keep a secret.

"Mother, just wait a little longer." Zhang San helped her sit. "After I finish this last commission... I mean, this last case. We’ll leave."

"Truly?"

"Truly. I swear it."

The old woman sighed.

She pulled a bundle of oiled paper from her robe.

"I steamed a bun for you. Eat it while it's warm."

Zhang San took the bun. It was still hot.

He took a bite.

Meat filling. Savory.

But amidst the savory flavor, he tasted salt.

The taste of his mother’s tears.

---

*BANG! BANG! BANG!*

Another knock.

This time, it wasn't three longs and two shorts. It was a frantic, violent pounding.

"OPEN UP! Imperial City Guard (皇城司) business!"

The bun slipped from Zhang San’s hand, hitting the dirt floor.

The inevitable had arrived.

"Mother, sit still. Don't move."

Zhang San pushed his mother toward the corner of the bed and kicked the ash jar further out of sight beneath the frame.

He took a deep breath and opened the door.

A squad of Imperial Palace Guards in flying-fish robes stood outside. Their leader was a brute with a scarred face, a Spring Embroidered Blade (绣春刀) hanging at his hip.

"Are you Zhang San?"

"I am."

"Come with us."

"Where to?"

"The crematorium," the man sneered. "Registrar Zhou is waiting for you. He says you stole something from the Bureau."

"I stole nothing!"

"We’ll see about that when we get there."

Two guards lunged forward to seize him.

"Wait!" His mother suddenly lunged forward, clutching the leader’s boot. "My Lord! Have mercy! My son is an honest man! He wouldn't steal anything! There must be a mistake!"

"Get back, you blind hag!"

The man kicked her away with a heavy thud.

She hit the ground, her forehead splitting open against the doorframe. Blood began to flow.

"MOTHER!"

Zhang San’s eyes turned red. He tried to lunge forward, but two blades were instantly at his throat.

"Behave!"

Zhang San was dragged out the door.

He looked back at his mother lying on the ground.

She was still groping for him in the dirt, crying out: "San’er! San’er!"

In that moment, Zhang San’s heart shattered.

He was no hero.

He was just a failure who couldn't even protect his own mother.

Modern Day.

Chen Mo kicked the fire door open with a roar.

The door swung wide.

But outside was not freedom.

It was a wall.

A wall of black-clad security guards.

They held stun guns, their faces as expressionless as stone.

Old Zhao stood behind them, still cradling his thermos.

"Chen Mo, stop running," Old Zhao sighed. "You can't get out."

"Every exit in this building is sealed. Even the ventilation shafts are locked down."

"It’s a dead end."

Chen Mo, holding Lin Xiaocao, backed away step by step.

Until his back hit the cold, hard concrete.

"Old Zhao," Chen Mo looked at him. "You win."

"No. You lost." Old Zhao walked toward him. "You lost to your emotions. You could have stayed out of this. Just like Zhang San should have."

"Zhang San?"

"Yes. Zhang San." Old Zhao smiled faintly. "I assume you saw his end?"

"He was captured. His legs were broken. He was exiled."

"And the girl he tried to save? She became ash in the end anyway."

"That is what happens when you fight the system."

Old Zhao reached out his hand.

"Give me the child. It’s for your own good."

Chen Mo looked at Lin Xiaocao in his arms.

She was terrified. She was clutching his collar so hard her knuckles were white, her whole body shaking.

"Uncle... I'm scared."

"Don't be afraid."

Chen Mo pulled her tighter.

He looked at Old Zhao. There was no fear in his eyes anymore—only the cold, final resolution of someone who has already accepted their fate.

"Old Zhao, you’re wrong."

"Zhang San lost because he was fighting alone."

"But I am not."

Chen Mo suddenly smiled.

He pressed the remote in his pocket.

The one A-K had given him.

*BOOM!*

A deafening roar.

Not an explosion.

Every single light in the Social Security Bureau building went out simultaneously.

Darkness fell.

In the void, thousands of tiny green lights flickered into existence in the air.

Drones.

Hundreds of drones, like a swarm of fireflies, smashed through the windows and flooded into the building.

Each drone carried a miniature projector.

They coalesced into a massive, glowing array in the air.

Holographic light splashed across the walls, the floors, and the faces of the guards.

It was a drawing.

A lunatic in a straitjacket, with a pair of bloody scissors hovering over his head.

Below the image, a single line of text appeared:

**【这就是我们要杀的人。】**
**[THIS IS THE PERSON WE ARE TRYING TO KILL.]**
