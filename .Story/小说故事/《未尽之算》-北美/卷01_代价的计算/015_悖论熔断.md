# 第15集：悖论熔断

> **《未尽之算》·第15集·悖论熔断**
> **本卷进度：15/24 | 系统CPU占用率：99% | 系统倒计时：995集**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: FATAL ERROR. INPUT PARAMETERS CANNOT CONVERGE. LOGICAL DEADLOCK DETECTED.*

---

"What did you sign?"

Old Zhao’s face was pressed against the security monitor in the Safe House, his features distorted like a crumpled sheet of paper. His eyes were shot through with red veins, pupils dilated in a mask of pure terror.

He stared at the main monitor back at the Bureau. It should have displayed "MISSION COMPLETE." Instead, it was a tsunami of scarlet gibberish. Red warning boxes were popping up across the interface like a digital plague, stacking layer upon layer until they obscured every control.

Chen Mo sat in his swivel chair, hands lifted from the keyboard.

His fingers were still twitching—a lingering spasm from the sheer tension. But his gaze was as calm as the surface of an ancient, bottomless well.

"I signed my name," Chen Mo said, his voice soft yet clear as it filtered through the microphone to Old Zhao’s ears.

"Bullshit! The system says it’s an 'illegal character sequence'! What the hell did you upload?!" Old Zhao roared.

"I uploaded a **Question**."

Chen Mo pointed to the spinning icon in the center of the screen.

It wasn't a standard loading wheel. It was a drawing.

The drawing the girl had made—the small man wrapped in a straitjacket.

It was more than an image.

It was a **Logical Bomb** encapsulated within the image’s metadata.

---

The Rooftop. Social Security Bureau Data Center.

This was once the quietest place in the city—climate-controlled, sterile, silent. Now, alarms shrieked in unison, a piercing metallic buzz that felt like it would shatter eardrums.

Thousands of server fans had simultaneously kicked into high gear, creating a roar like a Boeing jet at takeoff. The vibration rattled the floor, the walls, and even the chests of the technicians inside.

The temperature in the server room skyrocketed. The AC units, set to a steady 22 degrees Celsius, failed instantly. Heat waves poured from the vents of the racks, carrying the acrid scent of overheating silicon and melting solder.

On the main display, red numbers strobed frantically:

**[SYSTEM_LOG]: WARNING. CPU CORE TEMPERATURE EXCEEDS 90C.**
**[SYSTEM_LOG]: LOGIC UNIT DEADLOCK DETECTED.**
**[SYSTEM_LOG]: RECURSION DEPTH OVERFLOW.**
**[SYSTEM_LOG]: COOLING SYSTEM OVERLOADED.**

Old Zhao burst into the room. A wall of heat hit him, nearly knocking him back. His glasses fogged over instantly.

"What’s happening?!" He grabbed a technician by the collar. "Why won't it stop?!"

The technical director was drenched in sweat, his fingers flying across the keys. But no matter what he entered, the screen remained unresponsive.

"Director Zhao! The system is stuck!" the man cried, his voice bordering on a sob. "Every process is locked! We can't terminate! we can't reboot!"

"Stuck? Is it a hack? Is it Fang Yu?"

"No... it’s not a hack. The firewall is intact. All the traffic is'compliant'."

The director pointed to a line of code repeating endlessly on the screen, his hand shaking:

"It’s the **Compliance Validation**."

"Chen Mo submitted a signature file. By protocol, the system *must* perform a 'Genuineness Validation' on that signature. It’s a hard-coded command in the bottom-layer logic. It has the highest priority."

"But this signature file contains a logical paradox."

The director pulled up the signature’s metadata:

**`function AssessValue(Human h) { return AssessValue(h) * -1; }`**

*(If a human has value, then he has no value. If he has no value, he has value.)*

"It's the classic 'This sentence is a lie' paradox," the director explained, sweat dripping from his chin onto the keyboard.

"To assign a value to this signature, the system has entered infinite recursion."

"It judges it as a Positive Asset, which concludes it is a Negative Asset."
"It then judges it as a Negative Asset, which concludes it is a Positive Asset."

"Truth is Lie. Lie is Truth."

"To untie this knot, the system has diverted every spare bit of computing power it has. It’s spun up every virtual machine, hogged every bit of bandwidth. But it’s a bottomless pit. The more power it throws at it, the tighter the knot gets."

"Right now, the entire computing power of the social security network is being vacuumed up by this one signature. It’s a black hole, and it’s swallowing everything."

Old Zhao stared at the spinning "Man in the Straitjacket."

He understood now.

Chen Mo hadn't hacked the system. He didn't need to.

He had given the system a **Compliant Instruction** that it could neither refuse nor digest.

He had weaponized the system’s own **Perfectionism**.

The system could not tolerate a variable that was neither True nor False. It *had* to yield a concrete answer: Yes or No. 0 or 1.

So it kept calculating.

Calculating until it calculated itself to death.

---

The Safe House.

It was hot here, too. Not as extreme as the server room, but the sheer tension in the air felt like a physical weight pressing on their skin.

"It’s working." Fu Mu stared at the progress bar that had turned a jagged, pulsing red. He pushed his glasses up. "The system is redlining. CPU utilization is at 99.9%."

"Will it collapse?" Su Huai asked. She clutched the iron box tight, her knuckles white.

"Yes," Fu Mu said firmly. "At this rate of temperature increase, the core processors will physically melt within ten minutes. Silicon crystals will liquefy. Circuit boards will ignite."

"When that happens, not only will Lin Xiaocao’s execution order be revoked, but the entire social security database will be lost. Every debt record, every blacklist, every credit score—turned to ash."

"Did we... go too far?" A-K asked, a flicker of fear in his eyes. He was a hacker, but he had never intended to actually destroy the world’s infrastructure.

Chen Mo didn't speak.

He closed his eyes, leaning back into his chair.

In his mind, he heard the sound of wood splintering.

*Snap. Pop.*

The sound of fire.

The sound of an ancient stake.

---

The Song Dynasty. Market Square.

The sun hung high and unforgiving.

Zhang San was bound to the execution pillar. At his feet, dry wood had been piled high and soaked in tung oil.

Around him, the crowd roared with delight.

"Burn him! Burn the madman!"

It was a carnival of ignorance. A trial of the anomaly.

Registrar Zhou sat on the overseer’s platform, tea in hand, a victor’s smile on his face.

"Light it!"

The torch was tossed into the wood.

*WHOOSH.*

Orange flames erupted toward the sky. The heat instantly engulfed Zhang San’s legs. Flesh hissed and popped; grease from his body dripped into the fire, fueling the blaze.

Did it hurt?

Yes.

It hurt beyond words.

But the pain kept him conscious.

In the heart of the fire, he opened his eyes. Through the shimmering, distorted air, he locked his gaze on Registrar Zhou.

"Registrar Zhou!" Zhang San roared, his voice thick with the taste of blood. "Do you think burning me fixes anything?"

"You are wrong."

"You can burn my flesh, but you cannot burn my bone."

"You can burn the evidence, but you cannot burn the human heart."

"That scrap of cloth I swallowed has merged with my blood. When you burn me, my grievance will dissolve into the smoke of this city, into its rain, into its soil."

"So long as this sky remains, so long as this earth remains, this debt will never be settled!"

The flames roared.

Zhang San’s silhouette was swallowed by the inferno. His skin began to carbonize, his skeleton cracking under the intense heat.

But in that final moment, Chen Mo saw it—Zhang San was smiling.

The smile of a victor.

*Even as you turn to ash, did you jam the gears?*

*Even as I melt the CPU, I will jam this system.*

We are two grains of sand from different eras, jamming the same machine at the same moment.

---

"Stop it! Stop it now!"

Old Zhao’s voice crackled through the intercom. He was frantic. The high-level composure he usually wore as a mask had completely disintegrated.

"Chen Mo! I agree! I won't kill her! I’ll revoke the order!"

"Retract that signature! If this continues, the Data Center is finished! The data of hundreds of millions of people! You can't pay for that!"

Chen Mo opened his eyes.

He looked at the screen, at Old Zhao’s contorted face.

"It's too late, Old Zhao."

Chen Mo’s voice was detached.

"The system has entered 'Fusion Mode.' Nothing can stop it now except for the fuses melting themselves out."

"And besides..."

Chen Mo stood up and walked toward the camera lens.

He pointed to his heart:

"Do you think I’m doing this just to save Lin Xiaocao?"

"No."

"I’m doing this to let you, to let the Algorithm Optimization Group, to let all of you sitting at the top know one thing."

"**Machines fear the heat, but humans don't.**"

"**Because humans are made of it.**"

"You want to turn people into cold data. You want to use frozen algorithms to measure the warmth of life."

"So we use that warmth to burn your machines into scrap metal."

*BOOM.*

A muffled explosion.

The sound of a transformer blowing on the roof of the Bureau.

The lights in the Safe House went out instantly.

The monitors went black. the hum of the fans died.

The world was plunged into absolute, crushing silence.

Only the candy wrapper in Lin Xiaocao’s hand gave a tiny, brittle crinkle in the dark.

The system was down.
