# 第6集：无法填入的伤

> **《未尽之算》·第6集·无法填入的伤**
> **本卷进度：6/24 | 张三的SAN值：60% | 系统倒计时：1004集**

I thought I had woken up.

I hadn't.

That smell of burning—not the scent of melting fiber optics, but something far more nauseating, the smell of charred flesh—was like a hand, pressing me back down into the nightmare.

The **Hua Ren Chang**—The Field of Transformation.

It sounds like a place where one might practice Taoist alchemy to achieve immortality. In truth, it was just a massive furnace for burning the dead.

Located near the mass graves west of Kaifeng Prefecture, it was usually used to cremate those who died in plagues or unclaimed bodies of the nameless.

Tonight, the flames roared toward the sky.

One hundred and seven corpses had already been tossed into the furnace. The stench of charred meat mixed with the scent of pine resin, carried by the north wind for miles, haunting the dreams of half of Kaifeng.

Zhang San stood in the snow.

Before him lay the final body.

The girl in the red padded coat.

"Faster!"

Registrar Zhou stood upwind, covering his nose with a silk handkerchief, his face a mask of impatience.

"The hour has arrived. If we don’t burn them now, we’ll miss the deadline for tomorrow’s 'Clearance Report' (清零报告)."

Zhang San didn't move.

He stared Intently at the body.

The flickering firelight danced across the girl’s face, as if coating her pale skin in a layer of gold dust. She looked less like a corpse and more like a sleeping bride.

"My Lord," Zhang San’s voice was faint, shredded by the wind. "The ledger isn't finished. By law, we cannot burn her."

"The ledger be damned!"

Master Zhou spat out a curse.

He walked over and kicked the stretcher hard.

"Zhang San, I gave you a chance. Back in the morgue, I told you to write 'Exposure.' You refused. Now we’re out here, and you’re still putting on this righteous act?"

"Let me tell you: these one hundred and eight bodies are all 'Plague Victims.' That is the official narrative set by the Governor himself."

"Plague?" Zhang San looked up.

"Yes. Plague."

Master Zhou pointed to the raging fire in the furnace.

"Fire purifies everything. Whether they were beaten, starved, or had their hearts shattered by an expert—once they go into that fire, they all come out the same. ASHES."

"Ashes are clean."

"Ashes don't talk, and ashes don't file complaints."

Master Zhou patted Zhang San’s cheek, his gold ring feeling like ice against the skin.

"Be smart, Zhang San. In this world, the 'clean' ones aren't the people who haven't done anything wrong. They’re the people **who haven't left any evidence**."

---

Zhang San looked at Registrar Zhou.

Suddenly, he felt pity for this man.

Master Zhou thought he was in control of everything. In truth, he was just another broom for the system. Wherever the system told him to sweep, he swept.

To ensure Kaifeng Prefecture received an 'Exceptional' rating, to make the Governor's record look good, he had to sweep these 'dirty things' away.

Even if those 'dirty things' were human lives.

"My Lord," Zhang San said. "What about the money?"

"What money?"

"The fifty taels of compensation."

Master Zhou froze for a second, then laughed.

"Oh, that."

He pulled the silver voucher from his sleeve and waved it in front of Zhang San.

"Since you didn't write 'Exposure,' the money is saved."

"Saved?"

"Yes. According to the 'Regulation for Processing Unclaimed Bodies,' cremation only costs 500 *wen*."

Master Zhou tucked the voucher back into his robe, smugly patting his chest.

"Zhang San, by being stubborn, you didn't help the girl. You just saved the government forty-nine taels and five *mace*. I should thank you for being so frugal with the treasury."

Zhang San felt a wave of dizziness.

That familiar *click, clack* sounded in his head again.

The beads of the suanpan were in chaos.

He thought that by not signing, he was standing up for justice. Instead, his refusal became the very reason the system saved money.

If he didn't lie, the girl’s family got no money.
If he did lie, her grievance was buried forever.

And now? He hadn't lied, and he hadn't cooperated.

The result: **The money was gone, and the truth was buried anyway.**

This was the system's logic loop.

No matter what you choose, as long as you choose within its rules, you are always the loser.

"BURN IT!"

Master Zhou gave the order.

Two laborers lifted the stretcher, shouting in rhythm as they tossed the girl in the red coat into the furnace.

"One, two, three—Go!"

The girl’s body traced a red arc through the air.

Like a red butterfly with broken wings, she fell into the sea of fire.

*WHOOSH.*

The flames swallowed the red coat. They swallowed the pale face. They swallowed the shattered heart.

Zhang San reached out, trying to grab something.

But he only caught a handful of flying ash.

---

"Alright, we’re done here."

Master Zhou clapped the soot from his hands, his mood greatly improved.

"Zhang San, don't look so miserable. Go to the treasury tomorrow for your bonus. Even if you didn't finish the ledger, you at least finished the job."

He led the laborers away.

Leaving Zhang San alone, standing in the empty crematorium.

The snow fell harder.

The furnace fire slowly died down, leaving only glowing embers.

Zhang San walked to the mouth of the furnace.

The heat dry-cleaned the tear tracks from his face.

He looked at the mound of greyish-white bone ash.

"Gone," he whispered.

The flesh was gone, the wounds were gone, and the evidence of the "shattered heart" was gone.

There was no one left in the world who could prove she was murdered.

Unless...

Zhang San suddenly remembered something.

He pulled a tattered book from his robe. It was his master’s handwritten copy of the *Xi Yuan Ji Lu*.

The pages were worn to the point of disintegration.

He flipped to the very last page. There was a line of text his master had circled in red:

**“若尸骨已焚，痕迹全无，可用‘蒸骨’之法，验其骨髓之毒，或骨内之伤。”**
**[If the corpse is burned and all traces gone, one may use the 'Bone Steaming' method to verify poison in the marrow or trauma within the bone.]**

**“然此法极险。若验无伤，则为毁尸。依律，反坐死罪。”**
**[But this method is extremely dangerous. If no wound is found, it is considered desecration. By law, the penalty is death.]**

Zhang San’s finger traced those words.

"Zheng Gu"—Bone Steaming.

It was a "forbidden art" strictly prohibited by Song law.

Because it required digging up the deceased’s bones, steaming them with hot vinegar, and using a red umbrella to filter the sunlight. In an age where "peace in the earth" was sacred, this was considered a profound violation.

And if no wound was found, it was "desecration of the remains." A capital offense.

It was a gamble.

The bet was that the truth was hidden deep within the girl’s bones.
The stake was Zhang San’s own life.

"Zhang San, do you dare?"

He asked himself.

In the wind and snow, a voice seemed to answer him.

It was the voice of Chen Mo, from a thousand years in the future:

**"I dare."**

**"Because our suanpan was already missing a corner anyway."**

**"Since we can't calculate a future, let's bet our lives on the truth."**

---

Zhang San knelt.

He bowed three times toward the furnace.

"My lady, forgive me."

"I could not save your life. But I can save your name."

"Even if I have to steam you until your bones turn brittle, I will find the person who killed you."

He stood up.

From his robe, he pulled a scrap of cloth he had secretly torn away during the chaos. It was a fragment of the girl’s red coat.

He bit his finger and wrote a single character on the cloth in blood:

**“冤” (Yuan — Injustice/Grievance).**

Then, he picked up a nearby iron shovel.

He was going to dig through the ash.

He was going to collect the ashes of all one hundred and eight bodies, jar by jar.

He was going to take them home.

Back to the basement that only he knew about.

There, he would build his battlefield against the system.

This time, he was truly awake.

"Bone Steaming..."

He tried to sit up, but his body felt as heavy as lead.

*PUFF.*

A spray of fresh blood splattered directly onto his keyboard.

It was dark, blackish-red blood, carrying that unsettling metallic scent.

Chen Mo’s hand trembled as he reached up to touch the crown of his head.

A large clump of hair slid out between his fingers.

Radiation sickness. Stage Two.

His time was running out.

"What bone steaming?" Su Huai was hunched over her desk, startled by the noise. Seeing the blood on Chen Mo’s chin, her face turned deathly pale. "Chen Mo! What happened?!"

"It’s nothing," Chen Mo wiped the blood away casually, stuffing the clump of hair into his pocket. "Just stress."

"You—"

"Listen to me," Chen Mo interrupted, his eyes terrifyingly sharp. "Zhang San used the 'Red Umbrella Bone Steaming Method' back then. I saw it. I need to check the Social Security Bureau’s 'Red Umbrella.'"

"What red umbrella?"

"Data Steaming."

Chen Mo pointed at the rotating yellow question mark on the screen—Lin Xiaocao’s "Pending" status.

"The system thinks that by deleting all the data, we can't find anything."

"But it forgot: every path taken leaves a trace."

"Just like the bloodstains in the bones, deleted data leaves 'magnetic traces' (磁痕) in the deep layers of the hard drive."

"I’m going to perform 'Bone Steaming' on the Social Security Bureau’s server."

"I’m going to unearth every 'Nameless Corpse'—every person Old Zhao and the Algorithm Optimization Group tried to hide."

"And I’m going to drag them out into the sun."
