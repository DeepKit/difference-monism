# 第9集：女孩的画

> **《未尽之算》·第9集·女孩的画**
> **本卷进度：9/24 | 系统防御值：90% | 系统倒计时：1001集**

Chen Mo sat on the folding cot in the basement, holding a drawing in his hands.

Lin Xiaocao had drawn it after she woke up.

She didn't speak. She had simply picked up the red and blue pencils from the table and, on the back of a discarded box of medicine, sketched this image.

The style was childish. The lines were wobbly, and the colors bled over the edges.

But Chen Mo stared at it for an entire hour.

In the drawing was a man. He wore a black suit and held a suanpan in his hands.

It was undeniably Chen Mo.

But over his suit, there was a layer of white cloth.

It wasn't a sack.

It was a **straitjacket**.

The kind used in psychiatric wards to restrain the violent. His hands were tied behind his back, and he was barefoot, standing on sharp shards of glass. The glass was red—it was blood.

And hovering over the little man’s head was a massive pair of red scissors.

The blades were open, poised right at the man’s throat.

"It’s ugly." Old Zhao had walked in at some point, his ever-present thermos in hand.

He glanced at the drawing and remarked, "She made you look like a lunatic."

Chen Mo didn't argue.

He traced his finger over the "lunatic" in the drawing.

"No. She didn't get it wrong."

Chen Mo’s voice was soft, but certain.

"To everyone else, I’m an elite at the Social Security Bureau, well-dressed, holding the power of life and death. I press keys in an air-conditioned room and decide a person's fate."

"But in her eyes, she saw my soul."

"She saw every step I take in this system as dancing in shackles."

"That straitjacket? That's my remaining conscience. It’s the final line of defense preventing me from becoming a 'normal person'—a cold-blooded animal. Those scissors? They are the KPIs hanging over my head, the layoff blade that could fall at any second, the system that wants to turn me into waste."

Chen Mo looked up at Old Zhao.

"She understands everything."

"She knows I’m trying to save her, and she knows I can't."

"She didn't draw this to ask for help."

"She drew it... for **pity**."

Old Zhao froze for a second.

"Pity?"

"Yes. Pity." Chen Mo pressed the drawing against his chest. "A dying child is pitying an adult living in hell."

---

Old Zhao fell silent.

He took a sip of tea, masking the flicker of unease in his eyes.

"Chen Mo, don't get too into character. You’re empathizing with a 'virus'."

"She isn't a virus," Chen Mo said. "She is perfect."

"Perfect?" Old Zhao sneered. "If she’s so perfect, why does the system want her dead?"

"Because the system is broken."

Chen Mo stood and walked to the scanner.

"I’m scanning this drawing into the system."

"Are you insane? That's junk data. The system will purge it automatically."

"Maybe not."

Chen Mo started the scanner.

The blue light swept over the medicine box paper.

Analysis results popped up on the screen:

`Object: Child's Scribble`
`Artistic Value: Low`
`Commercial Value: 0`
`Emotional Sentiment: Negative (Suppression/Fear)`
`Recommended Action: Archive or Purge`

I smiled bitingly.

"Look," Chen Mo pointed at the screen. "The system can identify every pixel, analyze every bit of color psychology, but it will never understand what this drawing actually means."

"This drawing is **Irreducible**."

"You can't convert it into a QALY score, and you can't exchange it for an 850,000 yuan settlement."

"It is the only 'invalid data' in this vast, precise, and cold computing system."

"But it is this very data that, like a thorn, has punctured my defenses."

Chen Mo closed the analysis window.

"Old Zhao, you said before—if I want to save her, besides a manual review, is there any other way?"

Old Zhao looked at him warily.

"What are you planning?"

"I’m going to buy her life."

"Buy her life?"

"Yes. Since the system only recognizes money, I'll give it money."

Chen Mo pulled something from his robe.

The sealed bag. Inside were Zhang San’s ancient fragments.

"This is an antique," Chen Mo said. "The original scroll of the Song Dynasty *Yan Shi Ge Mu*. Su Huai says it’s a unique copy. If it went to auction, it would be worth at least a million yuan."

"A million. Enough to buy back her life."

Old Zhao’s eyes widened.

"Are you out of your mind? Su Huai gave that to you for research! That's a cultural relic! It’s state property!"

"In the system's eyes, it’s just a piece of paper."

Chen Mo placed the fragments on the desk.

"If I 'donate' it to the Social Security Bureau’s museum in exchange for a 'Special Contribution Credit'..."

"The Bureau has a regulation: any employee who makes a major contribution to the system can gain 'Credit Redemption Rights' at a rate of one credit to 10,000 yuan in medical quota."

"I value this scroll at one hundred credits."

"One hundred credits. One million yuan."

"I’m going to use a dead man from a thousand years ago to save a living child today."

---

Old Zhao looked at Chen Mo as if looking at a hopeless gambler.

"Chen Mo, you’re using a cannon to kill a mosquito. It makes no economic sense."

"And besides, it’s not compliant."

"A relic donation needs an evaluation committee’s approval. The process takes at least three months. Lin Xiaocao doesn't have three months."

"I have a fast-track," Chen Mo said. "I can apply for an 'Emergency Donation'."

"That requires the Bureau Chief’s signature."

"Then you help me go to the Chief."

"I'm not going," Old Zhao refused flatly. "I'm not going mad with you."

"Old Zhao!"

Chen Mo grabbed Old Zhao by the collar.

"Look at me! Look at this drawing!"

He pointed to the small man wrapped in white.

"Do you think you’re safe on the shore? You think you’re safe just because you’re holding a thermos and looking at reports?"

"No. You’re under those scissors too."

"As long as you’re in this system, you’re just data. The moment you become a negative asset, the blade will fall."

"I'm not saving her just for her sake."

"I'm doing it to prove that **in this man-eating system, there are still people who can live like human beings!**"

Old Zhao looked into Chen Mo’s bloodshot eyes.

He saw fear. But he also saw courage.

That courage reminded him of himself thirty years ago. Back then, he had stormed into the Bureau Chief's office and slammed his fist on the desk over an unjust ruling.

And what happened?

He was demoted. Marginalized.

He learned to drink tea, learned to care for his health, learned to keep his mouth shut.

He thought he had matured.

In truth, he had just died.

Old Zhao pushed Chen Mo’s hand away.

He straightened his wrinkled collar.

"Let go."

"Old Zhao..."

"I said let go." Old Zhao turned his back on Chen Mo. "I’ll go."

Chen Mo froze.

"Go where?"

"To see the Chief." Old Zhao’s voice was calm, as if he were simply mentioning he was going to the restroom. "But I can't guarantee he’ll sign. That man is sharper than the system itself."

He reached the door and stopped.

"Chen Mo, if this works, your scroll is gone."

"It’s a priceless treasure."

"I know." Chen Mo looked at the fragments on the desk. "But in my eyes, it isn't worth a single strand of Lin Xiaocao’s hair."

"**Relics are dead. People are alive.**"

"If we sacrifice a life to protect a relic, that is the ultimate desecration of civilization."

Old Zhao nodded.

"Fine. You win."

He left.

Chen Mo collapsed into his chair.

He looked at the drawing.

The little man in the picture seemed to be smiling at him.

"Thank you," he said to the drawing.

Just then, his phone rang.

It was Su Huai.

"Chen Mo! Where are you?" Su Huai sounded like she was on the verge of a breakdown. "Come quick! Something’s wrong!"

"What is it?"

"The scroll! That fragment... there's an issue!"

Chen Mo’s heart sank.

"What issue? Is it a fake?"

"No, it’s real. Too real."

Su Huai’s voice was trembling.

"I just looked at the back of the signature under a microscope. Behind the character for 'San,' there’s another line of text. It was hidden by a stain before; I just managed to clean it."

"What does it say?"

"It says... **'Do not save. To save is to fall into eternal damnation (万劫不复).'**"

Chen Mo’s hand shook, and his phone nearly hit the floor.

"What did you say?"

"It’s a warning left by Zhang San for those who come after." Su Huai broke into tears. "Back then... he might not have wanted to save that maid at all."

"Or rather, after he saved her, something far worse than death happened."

"Chen Mo, don't donate it! Whatever you do, don't! It’s a curse!"

Chen Mo looked at the fragment on the desk.

Next to the character for "San," there was indeed a dark blot of ink.

Under a microscope, would it really be a curse?

**"Do not save. To save is to fall into eternal damnation."**

This was Zhang San from a thousand years ago, issuing a final warning to Chen Mo a thousand years later.

Chen Mo hung up the phone.

The basement was deathly silent.

Only the countdown ticked away.

**`1001`**

On one side was Old Zhao’s retreating back as he went to fight for the "Blood Money."
On the other was Su Huai’s frantic warning.

On one side was the hope of "Perfect Evolution."
On the other was the curse of "Eternal Damnation."

Chen Mo looked at the fragment.

Suddenly, he picked up the utility knife from the desk.

"What are you doing?" Lin Xiaocao had woken up without him realizing. She was hugging her knees, watching Chen Mo with wide eyes.

"Uncle is performing an experiment."

Chen Mo’s hand was steady.

He used the tip of the blade to gently scrape away the stain next to the character for "San."

The black ink peeled away.

Revealing the truth beneath.

There was indeed a line of text. The handwriting was frantic, steeped in desperation.

But it wasn't what Su Huai had said.

The character was...

Chen Mo leaned in close.

His pupils contracted violently.

It was the word **"TAO" (逃 — Escape)**.

**“逃！带着她逃！这根本不是病！这是……”**
**[ESCAPE! Take her and escape! This isn't a disease at all! This is...]**

The words that followed had been completely obliterated.

Had they been burned away? Or scraped off on purpose?

Chen Mo didn't know.

But he knew one thing: Zhang San had discovered the secret back then.

He had realized the girl in the red coat wasn't an ordinary person. The "Perfection" within her wasn't a blessing; it was a catastrophe.

Because in an imperfect world, perfection itself is the original sin.

If you are an anomaly, only two fates await you: to be purged by the system, or to be exploited by it.

"Escape..." Chen Mo whispered.

He looked at Lin Xiaocao.

"Uncle, where are we going?" Lin Xiaocao asked.

"We aren't going back home."

Chen Mo tucked the scroll into his robe. He folded the drawing and put it in his pocket.

He picked up Lin Xiaocao.

"We’re running away."

"Where?"

"To a place the system cannot calculate."

Just then, heavy, rhythmic footsteps echoed from outside the door.

It wasn't Old Zhao.

It was the sound of a regular, disciplined force.

The **Security Guard Team**.

Had Old Zhao failed? Or... had Old Zhao betrayed him?

"Chen Mo! Open the door!"

A shout came from outside.

"We are the Algorithm Optimization Group Direct Enforcement Team. You are suspected of illegally possessing a high-risk prohibited item (Subject Zero). Hand over the subject immediately and submit to investigation!"

The scissors had fallen.
