# 第13集：系统的最后通牒

> **《未尽之算》·第13集·系统的最后通牒**
> **本卷进度：13/24 | 系统防御值：10% | 系统倒计时：997集**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: STRESS TEST CONCLUDED. TARGET PROFILE: EXTREMELY UNSTABLE. PROCEED WITH RESTRUCTURING.*

---

The door to the Safe House was kicked open.

It wasn't Old Zhao. It wasn't the Security Guard Team.

It was A-K.

He was drenched in sweat, clutching a crumpled slip of paper that had just spat out from a fax machine connected to the underground pipes.

"Look at this!" A-K shoved the paper into Chen Mo’s hand. "Old Zhao has gone completely insane!"

Chen Mo unfolded the slip.

It was a single line of text, printed in a harsh, blocky font:

**`SYSTEM ALERT: PURGE SEQUENCE INITIATED.`**
**`TARGET: UNDERGROUND LEVEL.`**

At that exact moment, a sharp, hissing sound echoed from the ventilation shafts overhead.

*Sss—*

It wasn't the sound of a fan. it was the sound of high-pressure gas being injected.

"Nerve gas!" Fang Yu roared, lunging to seal the vent.

But it was already too late.

Plumes of white smoke flooded the room.

Lin Xiaocao began to cough violently. Su Huai covered her nose and mouth with her sleeve.

Chen Mo felt his throat catch fire. He reached for a gas mask, but his limbs were turning to water, refusing to obey.

Dizziness.

Nausea.

Asphyxiation.

*Is this how it ends?*

Just as Chen Mo was prepared to surrender to the void, the smoke abruptly stopped.

Fresh air surged through the vents.

The basement lights flickered, then stabilized.

The door opened again.

Old Zhao stood there, cradling his thermos as if nothing had happened.

There were no guards behind him. Only the dead silence of the corridor.

"Exercise concluded."

Old Zhao blew the steam off his tea, his tone so flat it made Chen Mo want to strike him.

"This is the Social Security Bureau’s quarterly 'Emergency Response Drill.' Chen Mo, your department’s response time? Failing."

Chen Mo collapsed to the floor, lungs burning as he gasped for oxygen.

He stared at Old Zhao.

He understood now. This wasn't a drill. It was **Terrorism**.

Old Zhao was telling him: *I can kill you whenever I want. I simply choose not to. Yet.*

"This is your game?" Chen Mo wheezed, forcing himself to stand. "Using death to scare us?"

"No." Old Zhao smiled, the wrinkles around his eyes hiding a profound, hollow exhaustion. "True terror doesn't need death."

He pulled a file from his bag and set it on the table:

"**True terror uses life.**"

---

It was a **Departmental Restructuring Proposal**.

A red-header document, stamped with the Bureau’s official seal.

The content was simple:

**"In view of the recent low efficiency and numerous regulatory violations by the Exception Handling Department, it has been decided: The department is to be disbanded immediately. All personnel are to be reassigned."**

Below was a detailed placement list:

*   **Su Huai**: Repatriated to her place of origin. Academic credentials revoked. Lifetime ban from all public archives.
*   **Fu Mu**: Reassigned to the neighborhood committee to register stray dogs. Lifetime ban from civil service promotions.
*   **A-K**: Fired. Low-income housing unit revoked.
*   **Fang Yu**: Referred to judicial authorities for hacker activities.

Chen Mo’s hand shook.

This was a "Salted Earth" strategy.

Old Zhao wasn't just destroying Chen Mo. He was destroying everyone Chen Mo cared about.

He had pinpointed every person’s pressure point.

Su Huai’s dream was history. Fu Mu’s dream was politics. A-K’s bottom line was survival. Fang Yu’s bottom line was freedom.

Old Zhao was stripping it all away.

"However," Old Zhao’s voice softened slightly, "this document has not yet taken effect."

He pulled out a second document.

**Voluntary Waiver Application**.

The content: Chen Mo voluntarily renounces his protection of Subject Zero (Lin Xiaocao), admits his previous actions were a "technical error," and will cooperate with the system for specimen recovery.

"If you sign this," Old Zhao pointed to the application, "the restructuring proposal above is immediately voided."

"And not just that."

Old Zhao took a gold pen from his pocket and laid it across the paper.

"If you sign, you aren't just a专员 (officer) anymore. You’ll be promoted to Deputy Director of Risk Control. Double salary. Your mortgage, your mother’s care—none of it will be an issue."

"Your team will be protected, too. Su Huai will get special clearance for the National Archives. Fu Mu will go to the Policy Research Office. A-K will be made a permanent employee. Fang Yu will be moved to Cyber Security."

Old Zhao looked at Chen Mo, his eyes seemingly filled with sincerity:

"Chen Mo, this is a 'Win-Win' solution."

"The only loser is a child who was supposed to be dead anyway, and who is destined to die soon regardless."

"You can't save her. You’ll only die with her. If that’s the case, why not use her death to buy a future for your friends?"

---

Chen Mo looked at his teammates.

A-K kept his head down, refusing to meet his eyes. He had a sick daughter to raise; this job was his only lifeline.

Su Huai bit her lip. If she lost her credentials, a lifetime of work would be erased.

Fu Mu pushed his glasses up. He was the most rational of them all.

"Chen Mo," Fu Mu began, "From the perspective of Game Theory..."

"Shut up," Chen Mo interrupted.

*I know what you're going to say.*

*Nash Equilibrium. Local Optimal Solution. Kill one to save the family.*

*I understand the logic.*

But this felt worse than death.

The system wasn't just asking him to kill; it was asking him to kill while stepping over the careers of his friends. Or, to save his friends by stepping over the girl’s body.

No matter the choice, his soul would be torn to shreds.

This was **The Ultimate Trolley Problem**.

On one track, the innocent victim.
On the other, the innocent friends.

If you pull the lever, you kill the victim and save your friends.
If you don't, the system runs over your friends.

"One hour."

Old Zhao glanced at his watch.

"At 9:00 PM, the mandatory execution order will trigger automatically. If you sign this before then, I’ll claim the recovery was your operation. You get the credit."

"If you don't, the system executes automatically. Then it’s your failure. Your team, your career—gone."

He pushed the gold pen forward.

The tip glinted like a miniature scalpel.

"Chen Mo, stop being a child. In the adult world, the greatest sin is refusing to choose."

Old Zhao left.

Leaving the four of them with the pen.

Deathly silence.

Only the digital clock on the wall pulsed: `08:00:00`.

One hour countdown.

---

Chen Mo picked up the pen. It felt heavy.

He thought of the drawing the girl had made. The man with the scissors over his head.

The blades were falling now.

They weren't cutting off his head. They were cutting his ties to the world.

The most terrifying thing about the system wasn't its power.

It was **its ability to turn the 'Victim' into the 'Executioner'.**

It made you feel like *you* were the one killing the girl. Not the system.

"Chen Mo," Su Huai spoke suddenly, "just sign it."

Chen Mo froze.

"I don't want to be a criminal in history," Su Huai said with red-rimmed eyes. "But I also don't want you to destroy yourself for me."

"Yeah," A-K looked up. "Chen Mo, I’m just a guy who sweeps floors. I’m not worth your life. Worst case, I’ll go collect scrap."

"I concur," Fu Mu said. "If someone must be sacrificed, let it be the smallest asset."

Chen Mo looked at them.

They were all pushing him to sign.

Not out of selfishness, but out of **love**.

They didn't want to be his burden.

But that only made Chen Mo’s agony sharper.

If he signed, he betrayed Lin Xiaocao.
If he didn't, he betrayed the friends willing to die for him.

"I..." Chen Mo’s hand shook.

Suddenly, A-K—who had been silent—stood up.

He walked to Chen Mo and took the pen from his hand.

"Chen Mo, don't sign it."

A-K’s voice was raspy, but unnervingly steady.

"A-K, you don't understand..."

"I understand."

A-K pulled a crumpled cloth bundle from his shirt, unwrapping it layer by layer.

Inside was a **Blackened Bone**.

It was only the size of a thumb, charred as if passed through a furnace.

But on the surface, a faint, reddish mark was visible.

Like a fingerprint.

"This is an heirloom," A-K said. "My great-great-grandfather was a wuzuo in Kaifeng Prefecture. He said this bone was the only thing that didn't burn away."

"It has a handprint on it."

"He said as long as this print remains, **some debts never truly get settled.**"

Chen Mo stared at A-K in shock.

"You’re... a descendant of Zhang San?"

A-K nodded.

"Not a direct descendant, maybe. Records got lost in the chaos of war."

A-K rubbed his nose, a pained smile playing on his lips.

"But my grandfather named me 'A-K' to remember something. He said K is for King, or Killer. But in a deck of cards, K is only second best."

"The biggest card is the **Joker**."

"Zhang San was the Joker. He was a joke to the world, but he was the only card that could blow up the game."

"Chen Mo, my family has only one rule: **As long as the bone remains, we do not surrender.**"

A-K shoved the bone into Chen Mo’s hand.

It was ice-cold, yet it burned Chen Mo’s palm with a phantom heat.

"Don't sign," A-K said. "If you sign, this bone has stayed blackened for a thousand years for nothing."

Chen Mo gripped the bone tight.

He felt it.

This wasn't a dead piece of matter. It was Zhang San’s spine.

A thousand years ago, in the face of Registrar Zhou’s threats and promises, Zhang San hadn't bowed.
A thousand years later, his descendant stood before me with his bone, telling me the same thing.

How could I lose?

How *dare* I lose?

Chen Mo picked up the Voluntary Waiver Application.

*RIIIIIIIP—*

He tore it in half.

Then into quarters. Then eighths.

Until it was nothing but confetti.

He tossed the scraps into the air like a flurry of white snow.

"I'm not signing."

Chen Mo looked at Fu Mu, Su Huai, and Fang Yu.

"Not just me. None of you sign."

"Old Zhao thinks he’s found our weakness. He forgot that a weakness is also armor."

"It's precisely because I have you that I cannot afford to lose."

"If we don't smash this man-eating system, then even if we survive today, tomorrow we’ll die on some other 'Mandatory Execution Order'."

Chen Mo walked to the computer.

"Fang Yu, how did you say the Ford Pinto case was won?"

"The internal memo leaked," Fang Yu said. "The formula '137 million > 49.5 million' went public. It enraged the jury."

"Good."

Chen Mo pulled out a USB drive.

"We have a memo, too."

"Hidden in here is a list of every 'optimized' death in the system over the last decade. Old Man Li, Fu Mu’s sister... a thousand lives treated as 'Negative Assets' just like Lin Xiaocao."

"Old Zhao wants to use our future to threaten us?"

"I'll use the system's own secrets to gut him."

Chen Mo plugged the drive into the workstation.

"A-K, is your 'Nonsense Generator' still working?"

"It is."

"Don't send nonsense this time."

Chen Mo’s gaze turned lupine, cold and predatory.

"This time, we send a **Nuke**."

"We’re broadcasting this list to every citizen in the city paying into social security."

"Tell them: **This is the ending you’re buying with your hard-earned money.**"
