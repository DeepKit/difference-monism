# 第2集：算法的慈悲

> **《未尽之算》·第2集·算法的慈悲**
> **本卷进度：2/24 | 陈默的信用分：60 (冻结中) | 系统倒计时：1008集**

---

The air in the basement still carried the **acrid smell of burnt plastic**—the residue from when I used the fire axe to sever the fiber optic cables.

The emergency lights on the wall were still flickering. Outside the door, the sound of the engineering repair team's drills bored into my brain like a physical drill.

Old Zhao was gone. The look he gave me before he left was like he was looking at a dead man.

"You've saved the system's mother."
"And you're its first victim."

I looked at the girl in front of me, still eating her candy. She was only seven, bald, and as thin as a newly hatched bird.
She had no idea that to save this little bird, I had just cut the nerves of the entire block.

"Uncle, I don't hurt anymore."

Lin Xiaocao finished her candy and wiped her sticky fingers on her hospital gown.

"When can we go home?"

Home.

Those words stung me like a needle.

Where was her home? Her file showed that both her parents were dead, and her only guardian was the Social Security Bureau. This was her home. Or rather, this was her prison.

"Soon," I lied to her, my right hand trembling involuntarily—a muscle spasm from the heavy chopping. "Uncle just needs to settle one more account, and then I'll take you home."

I slipped the chipped abacus back into my pocket. It was cold and rough, pressing against my thigh like a heavy stone.

The "physical disconnection" just now had forced the local mode to start, saving her life. But this was only temporary.
Once the fiber optics outside were reconnected and the main office's computing power surged back in, that red "Clearance Order" would flash again.

I had to find a real, legal shield before that happened.

**Buzz—**

My work phone vibrated.

A message from the "Main Office Intranet":

**【请陈默专员立即前往B区算力中心（顶层）。赵总监有请。】**
**【备注：带上你的斧子。】**

No signature. But I felt the killing intent.

I knew the trial was coming.

---

The Social Security Bureau building had thirty floors.

The Exceptional Disposition Department was on B2. The Computing Center was on the top floor.

This thirty-story distance was the distance from hell to heaven.

The elevator zoomed upward. My eardrums throbbed from the pressure change.

"Ding—"

The doors opened.

I was met with a dazzling white light.

There was no dust here, no smell of mildew, and no sound of a leaky faucet that wouldn't stop dripping.

There were only pristine glass walls and rows of black server cabinets lined up like tombstones.

Countless green indicator lights flickered on the cabinets, as if they were breathing.

Every flicker represented a social security payment calculation being completed. It represented someone receiving their life-saving money, or being rejected.

Old Zhao stood before a massive floor-to-ceiling window, overlooking the city below.

He still held that thermos in his hand. But in this high-tech environment, that old-fashioned enamel mug with the red letters "Serve the People"—the old Party slogan of a bygone era—looked particularly jarring.

"You're here." Old Zhao didn't turn around.

"I'm here."

I walked up behind him.

"Look down there." Old Zhao pointed outside.

It was the evening rush hour. The flow of cars looked like a river of red through the streets. The lights of office buildings twinkled like stars.

"This is the world we protect." Old Zhao's voice was soft. "Twenty million people. Everyone being born, getting old, getting sick, and dying. Every day, 5,000 newborns are born, and 3,000 elderly people pass away. 20,000 go bankrupt because of illness, and 10,000 are disabled by workplace injuries."

"Without the system, this city would be paralyzed in three days."

Old Zhao turned around and looked at me:

"Chen Mo, what do you think the system is?"

"It's a tool," I said. "A tool for settling accounts."

"Wrong."

Old Zhao shook his head. He unscrewed his thermos and took a sip of water. The ring of brown tea scale around the rim wobbled with his movement.

"The system is **mercy**."

---

I was stunned, almost laughing out loud.

"Mercy? You call that algorithm that wants to kill a little girl over 850,000 yuan 'mercy'?"

"Yes." Old Zhao's eyes showed no emotion. "That is the greatest mercy."

He walked to the control console and pressed a button.

The originally transparent glass wall suddenly changed color, turning into countless surveillance screens.

They were ICU wards from various hospitals across the city.

"Look at that one." Old Zhao pointed to a screen in the top left corner.

An elderly man covered in tubes lay on a hospital bed, his chest heaving violently. The ventilator next to him let out a piercing alarm.

"Old Man Li, 82 years old. Brain hemorrhage, deep coma for three months. The daily maintenance cost is 5,000 yuan."

"His social security account is empty. For these three months, the public pool fund has been keeping him alive."

Old Zhao's finger slid across the screen:

"Look at this one. Auntie Zhang, 65 years old. Uremia. Dialysis three times a week. She’s waiting in line for a kidney."

"And this one. Worker Wang, 40 years old. A big-tech programmer, on the brink of death from overwork. Currently being resuscitated."

Old Zhao's voice grew faster:

"Resources are finite, Chen Mo. Even the social security fund is finite."

"If we save Old Man Li, we have to stop Auntie Zhang's dialysis. If we save that programmer, we have to give up on that baby with cerebral palsy."

"Who should live? Who should die? Who makes that decision?"

Old Zhao stared into my eyes:

"Do you dare make it? Do you dare stand in front of Auntie Zhang and say: 'I'm sorry, I have to take your money to save Old Man Li, so go ahead and die'?"

I fell silent.

I wouldn't dare.

No one would. That is God's authority.

"So, we need the system," Old Zhao said. "The system has no feelings, no selfish interests. It only looks at data."

"It calculates the input-output ratio. It calculates social contribution value. It calculates QALYs."

"It made that most cruel, yet also most fair, decision for us."

Old Zhao pointed at the screen with Old Man Li:

"Just now, the system determined that the probability of Old Man Li waking up is less than 0.1%. So, it cut off the payment interface."

On the screen, the lights on the ventilator went out.

Doctors and nurses rushed in. The family was wailing.

A life turned into a straight line before my eyes.

"This is cruel." Old Zhao closed his eyes. "But the 5,000 yuan saved will turn into a thousand flu vaccines tomorrow, injected into the arms of a thousand children."

"This is the mercy of the algorithm."

"Kill one to save a thousand. This is the Way of our profession."

---

I felt a chill.

This chill was deeper than the coldness of the basement.

Because Old Zhao was telling the truth.

This logic was perfect. It used mathematics to build a moral sanctuary. As long as you hid in this sanctuary, you didn't have to be responsible for anyone’s death.

You were just executing an algorithm.

"And what about Lin Xiaocao?" I asked. "Is she also that 'one'?"

"No."

Old Zhao opened his eyes, and his gaze turned sharp.

"She is an **Exception**."

He pulled up Lin Xiaocao's file.

The screen displayed a complex genetic map. The red helical structures twisted like snakes.

"Do you know why she is Test Subject Zero?"

"Because her genes carry a defect called 'Perfect Altruism'."

"What?"

"Under the influence of this gene, she will trust others unconditionally and sacrifice herself unconditionally. Just like just now, when she gave you her only piece of candy."

Old Zhao let out a cold laugh:

"That sounds beautiful, doesn't it? But in game theory, it's called a **'Prey Strategy'**."

"If this gene spreads, human society will be filled with 'fools.' Scammers will exploit the fools, and bad people will exploit the good."

"In the end, the good people die out, and the bad people starve because they have no hosts. The species goes extinct."

Old Zhao looked at the genetic map:

"The system has determined that she is a 'virus of civilization.' To protect human competitiveness and the wolf-like nature, she must be cleared."

"This isn't just an economic account. This is an **evolutionary account**."

I looked at Old Zhao.

He was no longer the middle-aged man who liked drinking tea and was a bit greasy.

At this moment, standing before the vast flow of data, he was like a cold priest reading out the divine will.

"Chen Mo, stop," Old Zhao said. "The Algorithm Group has already fixed that 'Manual Review' bug. Now, only you can withdraw the application."

"If you don't, the system will launch the 'Forced Error Correction Procedure'."

"By then, not only will the girl die, but you will also be cleared as an 'infected person'."

He pushed that A4 sheet toward me once again:

**《撤销复核申请书》**

"This is your last chance."

---

I looked at that paper.

Then I looked at the bustling city outside the window.

In this city, everyone was calculating. Calculating interests, calculating gains and losses, calculating how to step on others' heads to climb higher.

Is this what Old Zhao calls "wolf-like nature"? Is this the "competitiveness" the system wants to protect?

If the price of civilization is that we must kill the child who is willing to share her candy...

Then is there even a point to this civilization existing?

I touched the abacus in my pocket.

It was burning hot.

It was as if Zhang San, from a thousand years ago, was roaring in the palm of my hand.

**"To hell with the evolutionary account!"**

I looked up at Old Zhao.

"Old Zhao, have you ever heard of 'Article 108'?"

Old Zhao's pupils contracted sharply.

"What?"

"Article 108 of the *Social Security System Emergency Disposal Regulations*."

I recited that "dead clause" that almost no one knew about:

**“当系统判定可能会导致‘社会伦理基石’崩塌时，执行者有权启动‘道德熔断 (Moral Circuit Breaker)’。”**

**“熔断期间，所有算法逻辑暂停。由执行者进行‘人肉担保’。”**

"Do you know what you're saying?" Old Zhao's voice went up an octave. "A Moral Circuit Breaker? That's a self-destruct program designed to prevent AI from going rogue! Once activated, all your credit points, your pension, and all your future social benefits will be frozen as 'security deposit'."

"If the final judgment is that you were wrong, you're completely finished."

"You'll experience total 'Social Death.' You'll become a ghost. You'll be worse off than a beggar."

"I know."

I said calmly.

I pressed my hand onto the black control console.

I felt the pulse of the electricity through my fingertips.

"I only have 60 points. I have nothing left to lose."

"But I still have a piece of candy."

I took out the candy wrapper from my pocket and slapped it onto the desk.

"The system can calculate the calories in this candy, but it can't calculate its sweetness."

"Because it has no tongue."

"But I do."

"Activate,"

I said loudly.

Every green light on the console instantly turned red.

A piercing alarm echoed through the sky.

**【警告：检测到最高级指令。】**
**【指令代码：道德熔断 (Moral Circuit Breaker)。】**
**【担保人：陈默（ID：EX-003）。】**
**【正在冻结担保人资产……】**
**【冻结完成。】**
**【系统逻辑层……强行中断。】**

One by one, the countless surveillance screens on the large monitor went dark.

The massive algorithmic model that was calculating the "input-output ratio" stopped running.

Outside, the lights of the office buildings seemed to flicker for a second.

The entire social security system, in this moment, was like a heart that skipped a beat for a second while running.

Old Zhao slumped into his chair.

"Crazy..." he muttered to himself. "You're truly a madman."

"You let the entire system halt for five minutes just to save a 'virus.' Do you know how much money will be lost in these five minutes?"

"I don't know."

I turned toward the elevator.

"But I know that in these five minutes, Lin Xiaocao is safe."

"And," I looked back at Old Zhao, "in these five minutes, maybe Old Man Li can see his family one last time."

The elevator doors closed.

I leaned against the cold metal wall, and my body began to shake uncontrollably.

That was the exhaustion after the adrenaline faded.

I knew I had pushed myself to a dead end.

My account was frozen. My future was mortgaged.

I was now a pauper.

But I felt the candy wrapper in my pocket and smiled.

At least, this candy was quite sweet.
