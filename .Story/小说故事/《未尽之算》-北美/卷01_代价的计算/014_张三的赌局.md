Kaifeng Prefecture. Death Row.

This was the foyer to hell. There were no windows, only a single ventilation hole in the ceiling that let in a thin, mockery of light. In the corner sat an oil lamp, its wick encrusted with soot, flickering with a dim, sickly yellow glow.

The air was a stagnant slurry of moldy straw, old urine, and that metallic tang that lingering blood leaves behind when it dries into the wood.

Zhang San was curled in the corner. His legs had been broken, swollen like bloated dough, turning a bruised, necrotic purple. Even the smallest twitch sent a wash of cold sweat down his back, as if a thousand needles were being driven into the marrow of his bone.

But the hunger was worse.

He had been locked away for three days. Three days without a drop of water or a crumb of food.

His stomach felt as if a hand were clawing at his insides from the inside out. His throat was so parched it felt like it would smoke. Every time he swallowed, it felt like he was swallowing sand.

In his delirium, he began to see the bodies he had once examined.

The old beggar who froze to death, the child who starved, and the girl in the red coat. They stood around him, watching in silence.

"Zhang San, are you hungry too?" the beggar asked.
"Uncle, have some candy." The girl in the red coat offered him a sweet that didn't exist.

Zhang San reached out, his fingers grasping only at the empty air.

*CLANG.*

The iron door groaned open. The heavy hinges shrieked with a sound that shattered his hallucinations.

Registrar Zhou walked in. He was in full official robes, his powder-based black boots gleaming. He carried a fine, red-lacquered food box.

"Zhang San, still alive?"

Zhou set the box on the dirt floor and lifted the lid.

A thick, savory aroma flooded the cell. Pork and leek buns. Hot, fresh from the steamer.

The scent was like a physical hook, instantly snagging Zhang San’s stomach.

Zhang San’s throat bobbed violently. His eyes locked onto the two plump, white buns like a wolf staring at a lamb.

"Do you want them?" Zhou picked up a bun and waved it in front of Zhang San’s face. The steam brushed against Zhang San’s cheek, making his head spin.

"Want them... give me..." Zhang San’s voice was a raspy wheeze. He reached for the food.

Zhou pulled his hand back.

"I can give them to you," Zhou smiled, a smile that held the edge of a blade. "But you have to trade."

"Trade for what?"

"The evidence."

Zhou crouched down, his gaze turning venomous:

"Zhang San, stop playing the fool. In the crematorium, I saw you hide a scrap of that girl’s coat."

"It has blood on it. A bloody handprint from the killer."

"Hand it over. These buns are yours. I’ll ensure the torture stops. I’ll even let you go back to the village to care for your mother."

At the mention of his mother, Zhang San’s body convulsed.

He remembered the blind old woman lying in the dirt. He remembered her hands like withered bark, and the sound of her voice calling his name.

"My mother... how is she?"

"Still alive," Zhou said dismissively, as if discussing the health of an ant. "But if you continue to be uncooperative, that might change. You know the methods of the Imperial City Guard. They have ways to make a blind old woman talk, even if she knows nothing."

---

It was a Death Trap.

If he surrendered the evidence, he was an accomplice. The girl’s injustice would be buried forever. The high-ranking killer would continue his spree, untouchable.

If he didn't, he would starve. His mother would die.

Biological instinct screamed: **Give it to him! Just give it! Living is more important than anything! While there is life, there is hope!**

But another voice in his heart whispered: **You cannot. If you do, you can never hold your head up again. If you do, you have finally become a dog.**

Zhang San reached into his robe with a trembling hand and pulled out the filthy red strip of cloth.

He had torn it from the girl’s body during the chaos. It held the clear, undeniable handprint. It was a fingerprint. It was the truth.

Zhou’s eyes lit up with greed.

"Give it to me!" He lunged for the cloth.

Just as his fingertips were about to touch the fabric.

Zhang San pulled his hand back.

His gaze shifted. The desperation vanished, replaced by a sudden, jagged madness.

"You want to eat?" Zhang San asked.

"What?" Zhou froze.

"I want to eat."

Zhang San shoved the red cloth into his mouth.

In one fluid movement, he snatched the pork bun from Zhou’s hand and jammed it in after the cloth.

"What are you doing?!" Zhou cried in horror.

Zhang San didn't answer. He couldn't.

His mouth was stuffed with the bun and the coarse fabric. The cloth was dry and rough, catching in his throat like a mouthful of knives. The bun was scalding, peeling the skin from the roof of his mouth.

But he chewed. He chewed with everything he had.

He chewed through the grease of the meat, through the metallic tang of the blood, through his own tears and snot.

He would grind it into nothing. He would swallow it.

He would make this evidence part of his own flesh.

"Spit it out! Spit it out now!"

Zhou lunged, grabbing Zhang San by the throat, trying to dig the contents from his mouth. His fingernails dug deep into Zhang San’s neck.

But Zhang San clamped his jaws shut. His teeth tore through his own lip; warm blood began to leak out.

His face turned a bruised purple, his eyes bulging as if they might pop. The sense of suffocation felt like his lungs were about to explode.

**Swallow it! SWALLOW IT!**

He roared at himself in his mind.

**Swallow the injustice! Swallow your conscience! Turn the evidence into your own bone and blood!**

*GULP.*

A heavy, wet sound of swallowing.

The mixture slid down his esophagus and hit his stomach.

Zhang San opened his mouth, gasping for air in ragged, sawing breaths.

He looked at Zhou, showing his blood-stained teeth in a grin.

It was a smile of pure, agonizing triumph.

"Gone."

"The evidence... is gone."

"You want the proof? Fine. Cut open my belly and take it."

---

Zhou was incandescent with rage.

He kicked the food box over, the remaining bun rolling into the filth and straw.

"Madman! You absolute lunatic!"

He pointed at Zhang San, his voice shaking:

"You think swallowing the cloth makes you a winner? You are a fool!"

"Without physical evidence, I cannot be convicted. But you? For hiding evidence, destroying proof, and resisting authority—that is a capital offense."

"Tomorrow at noon. The market square. I will oversee the execution myself."

"I will watch you burn to ash. And then I will cast your ashes into the moat so you can never find peace in the afterlife!"

"As for that blind mother of yours..." Zhou sneered. "I'll send her to join you soon enough. She’s better off dead anyway."

The iron door slammed shut.

Death Row was plunged back into the dark.

Zhang San slumped to the floor, staring at the bun that had rolled into the filth.

It was the bun his mother had made for him. Not by her hands, but it was his last chance to taste one.

He crawled toward it, picking up the half-bun covered in dust and urine.

He stuffed it into his mouth, crying as he ate.

The bun was savory.

But it tasted bitter.

More bitter than any medicine.

*Mother, I'm sorry. Your son isn't coming home.*

*I've eaten my conscience. But the taste... God, it's so bitter.*

Because it wasn't printed on the surface. It was embedded in the bone itself. Zhang San had swallowed it with his life, digested it with his stomach acid, nourished it with his blood, and finally, it had been burned into the very marrow of his remains.

"He’s watching us," Chen Mo said.

He could feel the phantom heat emanating from the bone—a cross-generational frequency of rage and refusal.

That was Old Zhao’s deadline. In thirty minutes, the physical recovery sequence would initiate.

"Fu Mu, let's go."

Chen Mo’s fingers hit the keys.

**`System.Sign("Chen Mo")`**

He deleted "Chen Mo."

He entered a different string of characters.

It wasn't a name.

It was a drawing.

The drawing the girl had made—the lunatic in the straitjacket.

**`function AssessValue(Human h) { return AssessValue(h) * -1; }`**

*(If a human has value, then he has no value. If he has no value, he has value.)*

But in the "Value Assessment Core" of the social security algorithm, this was an indigestible **Toxin**.

It was the Ouroboros. The snake eating its own tail.

A perfect logical black hole.
