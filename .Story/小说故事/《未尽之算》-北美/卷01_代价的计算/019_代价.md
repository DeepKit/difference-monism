# 第19集：代价

> **《未尽之算》·第19集·代价**

---

When Chen Mo woke up, he thought he was dead.

The world before his eyes was a blur of blinding, sterile white.

It wasn't the white of heaven. It was the white of a hospital isolation ward.

He tried to move his arm, only to find his right hand encased in thick layers of gauze. It was completely void of feeling. A deep, hollow numbness extended past his elbow and up to his shoulder, like a dead snake coiled around his limb.

"Don't move."

A voice filtered through from above.

It was Old Zhao. He was clad in a full HAZMAT suit, looking like an astronaut standing beside the bed. His voice crackled through an intercom, distorted by electronic static, sounding uncharacteristically cold.

"Chen Mo, congratulations. You're not dead. Yet."

"The explosion from the carousel was minor—mostly an electrical fire. But that bead... that uranium sphere. You clutched it in your bare hand for three full minutes."

"Your right hand is gone. Total necrosis. The muscle tissue is carbonized, and the bone has turned brittle."

"Furthermore, you're now the city’s largest mobile radiation source. If you walked out of this room, anyone within a ten-meter radius would start losing their hair. Your sweat, your saliva, even the air you exhale—it’s all toxic."

Chen Mo tried to speak, but he felt his teeth rattling.

He spat out a mouthful of blood-stained fluid. Within the red slurry was a single molar. The tooth looked startlingly bright against the pristine white of the sheets.

"The girl?" he rasped, the words coming out muffled.

"Gone," Old Zhao said. "No body at the scene. Only the fragments of that abacus. Scans show the uranium sphere is missing, too."

"She escaped with a nuclear warhead."

Chen Mo closed his eyes.

*Good. She’s alive.*

"Chen Mo," Old Zhao sighed, watching him through the thick visor of his mask. "You think you saved her? You’ve only unleashed a plague."

"The radiation she released has covered the entire industrial zone. It was high-concentration ionizing radiation."

"Do you understand what that means?"

Old Zhao pointed to the adjacent room.

"It means mutation."

---

Chen Mo turned his head.

The glass wall of the isolation ward was transparent.

He saw A-K.

A-K had been injured in the blast, too—he’d inhaled some of the smoke. But now, he was sitting up in bed, looking unnervingly healthy.

He was performing high-kicks for the nurse.

"It’s a miracle! A damn miracle!" A-K shouted, beaming. "My leg—it’s been killing me for twenty years. Every time it rained, I could barely walk. Now? Nothing! No pain at all!"

He reached up and pulled off the baseball cap he always wore.

His scalp, which had been thinning for years, was now covered in a dense, dark stubble. It was growing thick and fast, like wild grass in springtime.

"Reverse aging?" Chen Mo muttered.

This didn't match the symptoms of radiation sickness. Radiation should age a person, not make them younger.

He looked to the other side.

Su Huai was sitting in a wheelchair, cradling a thick volume of the *History of the Song Dynasty*.

She had always been severely nearsighted; without her glasses, she was practically blind.

But now, her glasses were gone. She was flipping through the pages at a dizzying speed. her eyes darted back and forth like a high-precision scanner.

"Chen Mo!" Su Huai noticed him awake and pressed the book against the glass. "I remember it! I remember everything!"

"I read this once—two thousand pages—and I can recite every word!"

"My brain... it’s like a search engine. Anything I see—even the tax ID on a random receipt—is perfectly archived."

Chen Mo watched them.

Then he looked in the mirror at his own ghost-like reflection, at the hair falling from his head in clumps.

*Why am I decaying while they are evolving?*

"Because you are an Obstruction," Old Zhao’s voice rang cold.

"And they are **Adapters**."

---

"What does that mean?"

"The girl didn't release a toxin. She released a **Selection Agent**."

Old Zhao activated a holographic projection.

The screen displayed two radically different cellular reactions.

On the left: Chen Mo’s cells. Under the radiation, they were collapsing and liquefying like scalded leaves.
On the right: A-K and Su Huai’s cells. Under the radiation, they were accelerating their division and restructuring, becoming tougher, like steel forged in fire.

"Subject Zero’s DNA carries a unique 'Retrovirus'," Old Zhao explained. "This virus hitches a ride on the radiation."

"It attacks 'Old Humans'—people like you—and accelerates their terminal decay."

"But for those carrying the recessive 'New Gene'—people like A-K and Su Huai—it activates their latent potential."

"This is the true face of evolution: **Natural Selection**."

"Chen Mo, your DNA is obsolete. The girl’s virus judged you as a 'Legacy Version.' So, you rot."

"And they? They are New Humans."

---

Chen Mo stared at his blackened hand.

He finally understood why the System wanted to kill Lin Xiaocao.

it wasn't about saving money.

It was for **Self-Preservation**.

The core logic of the Social Security System is "Mean Reversion." It wants everyone to be mediocre, healthy, and stable. That is the only way it can calculate risk and generate profit.

But Lin Xiaocao represents "Mutation."

Mutation means the Uncalculable. It means that the current medical industry, the pension system, and social hierarchies will all evaporate.

If everyone becomes as long-lived as A-K, what happens to the pension funds? Who pays in? Who draws out? The system would go bankrupt in a week.

If everyone possesses Su Huai’s eidetic memory, what is the point of standardized testing? What happens to the education industry?

**Evolution is the ultimate act of vandalism against the existing order.**

The System is afraid. It doesn't fear death; it fears the **New Human**.

Because it cannot rule them. It cannot calculate them.

---

"So," Old Zhao switched off the projection. "Do you understand now?"

"We aren't murderers. We are the custodians of human social stability."

"And now, thanks to your foolishness, Pandora’s box is wide open."

"The radiation is spreading. The mutation is happening. Soon, this city will split into two species: the New and the Old."

"The war is beginning."

Old Zhao slapped a new ID badge against the glass.

**CHEN MO - ARCHIVE CLERK (INTERN)**

"Given your current physical condition, you are no longer fit for frontline work. Nor are you fit to be 'Zhang-003'."

"Go to the basement and manage the archives. It’s the furthest place from the radiation sources. Perhaps it’ll buy you a few extra days of life."

"And another thing," Old Zhao pointed at Chen Mo’s hand. "Don't try to sabotage anything else. Your current clearance won't even let you hack a time clock."

Old Zhao walked away.

Chen Mo stared at the intern badge.

From a savior to a watchdog.

That was the system’s true punishment. It wouldn't kill him; it would let him live.

It would let him watch the world mutate, piece by piece, while he, an obsolete relic of the Old Breed, withered away in a dark corner.

---

Late Night.

Chen Mo lay on the bed, staring at the ceiling.

The patterns in the acoustic tiles looked like the maps the girl used to draw.

Had he lost?

Perhaps.

But he thought of A-K’s legs. He thought of Su Huai’s eyes.

If the cost of evolution is the death of the Old Breed... is it worth it?

The system says no. Because the system represents the interests of the Old Breed.

But Life says yes.

Because the ultimate trajectory of life is to deny the past and surge into the future. Even if that future is brutal. Even if it is alien.

**"We thought we were calculating the virus. In truth, the virus was calculating us."**

Chen Mo whispered to himself.

**"It didn't want to destroy humanity. it simply wanted to switch people."**

With his good left hand, he reached under his pillow and felt the half-piece of candy.

The last piece Lin Xiaocao had left for him.

He unwrapped the paper and placed it in his mouth.

The candy had melted slightly, tasting of gauze and the faint, cold tang of iron.

But within that sweetness, he tasted something new.

The taste of **Hope**.

Even if that hope was, for him, a poison.
