The basement of the Social Security Bureau.

Chen Mo had returned.

He dragged his leaden right leg through the empty corridors like a phantom.

The basement was tomb-quiet, save for the rhythmic strobe of the emergency lights.

Su Huai was already there, waiting. She sat amidst the piles of mildewed archives, staring blankly at the fragmented yellow paper Zhang San had left behind.

"Chen Mo, are you okay?" Su Huai asked, her voice hitching when she saw him.

"I’m alive," Chen Mo said, collapsing onto a cot and exhaling a ragged breath. "Just missing a few parts."

He struggled to lift his right arm. It was wrapped in thick gauze, hanging like a dead weight. A pale yellow fluid had seeped through the wrappings, carrying a scent of charred meat and rot.

He gave a short, bitter laugh.

"Where's A-K? Where's Fu Mu?"

"A-K went to get food. Fu Mu is trying to patch the local servers," Su Huai said. "They said no matter how the world ends, we still have to eat."

"Yeah. We still have to eat."

Chen Mo looked at the fragment in Su Huai’s hand.

"What did you find?"

Su Huai’s fingers were trembling.

"Chen Mo, look at this."

She taped an enlarged rubbing to the wall. It was the symbol Zhang San had carved into the back of the iron pillar at the stake.

"We used to think this was just a desperate scribble. But now... my mind has changed. I can decode it."

Su Huai pointed to the symbol.

It was a square with a missing corner.

"It’s not just a drawing." She taped the girl’s drawing of the "chipped abacus" beside it.

Two images, separated by a millennium, aligned perfectly.

"What is it?" Chen Mo asked.

"It’s a **Lock**."

Su Huai pointed to the chipped corner:

"Look at this gap. Doesn't it look like... an interface?"

---

At that moment, Fu Mu and Fang Yu walked in.

Fang Yu held a freshly repaired laptop.

"Wait! If we fold this two-dimensional pattern into a 3D space..."

Fang Yu lunged for the workstation, his fingers dancing across the keys.

On the screen, the 2D abacus began to rotate and fold.

It transformed into a complex three-dimensional structure.

The structure looked disturbingly like a segment of a **DNA Double Helix**.

And the chipped corner corresponded exactly to the most lethal mutation segment in Lin Xiaocao’s genetic map.

"God." Fang Yu’s hands hovered over the keys, paralyzed. "Zhang San knew."

"He knew a thousand years ago."

"He didn't carve this as a curse. He carved it as a **Record**."

Su Huai picked up the thread:

"Historical records from the third year of the Xining Era mention a mysterious plague in Kaifeng. The victims died without a scratch, their pulses simply stopping. Then the plague vanished. No one knew why."

"This is why."

Su Huai pointed to the pile of fragments:

"Zhang San discovered the maid wasn't murdered. she died of a disease. A disease that was mutating."

"Registrar Zhou didn't burn the body to cover a nobleman’s crime. He burned it for **Biocontainment**."

"Zhang San didn't 'steam the bone' to reopen a court case. He did it to extract a sample of the 'Source' from the marrow—that blood print."

"He carved it into the iron pillar to leave a 'legacy code' for the future."

"He was telling us: **This demon is still alive. It’s hiding in the genes, hiding in the bone, waiting for its next alarm.**"

---

A chill ran down Chen Mo’s spine.

*We thought we were fighting a corrupt bureaucratic machine.*

*In truth, the 'System'—Registrar Zhou in the past, Old Zhao in the present—has been silently guarding humanity.*

They used cold-blooded methods—burning bodies, purging 'Negative Assets'—to suppress a demon that could erase the human race.

And I? I opened the cage.

"We were wrong," Chen Mo slumped into his chair, his voice a raspy whisper.

"No." Fang Yu stared at the screen. "Perhaps Zhang San left a counter-move."

"What kind of move?"

"The Chip," Fang Yu pointed to the DNA model. "This slot is empty. Zhang San didn't find the 'Key' a thousand years ago. He only drew the lock."

"If we can find what fills that gap..."

"We can lock it again."

*What fills the gap?*

Lin Xiaocao used **human bone** (a radioactive isotope) to patch her abacus.

And Zhang San?

Zhang San swallowed the blood-stained cloth.

Zhang San burned himself to ash.

"The bone," Chen Mo’s eyes widened. "A-K’s bone!"

The blackened bone with the handprint.

The only physical relic Zhang San had left behind.

If that handprint contained the 'Antibody' or the 'Correction Code' he had extracted...

"Where is A-K?" Chen Mo stood up suddenly. "Where is he?"

"He went to get food," Su Huai said. "He’s been gone half an hour."

"NO!"

Chen Mo sprinted out of the basement.

The corridors were echoing and empty.

Only A-K’s cleaning machine—his little sweeper—was parked in the corner.

Sitting on the machine was that familiar, crumpled cloth bundle.

Chen Mo lunged for it and tore it open.

Empty.

The bone was gone.

Beneath the cloth was a scrap of paper.

One line of crooked writing:

**“有些灰，扫不掉。得用命去填。”**
**[Some dust cannot be swept. It must be filled with a life.]**

---

Chen Mo stared at the note.

He understood.

A-K hadn't gone for food. He had gone to fill the hole.

He was the descendant of Zhang San. He was the only one who could use the key.

"Quick! The server room!" Chen Mo roared.

The trio sprinted to the rooftop data center. The main hub for the entire city.

The door was unlocked.

Inside, the sound of keyboard strokes filled the room.

*Clack-clack-clack.* The speed was torrential.

A figure sat with his back to them at the main console.

He wore a janitor’s uniform, but his posture looked immense, unyielding, and utterly alien.

On the screen, cataracts of code cascaded down.

The `Error` prompt that had bricked the system was slowly shifting into a new shape.

A closed loop.

The shape of a sideways `8`.

**`∞`** (Infinity).

"A-K?" Chen Mo called out tentatively.

The man stopped.

He turned around.

He was holding the blackened bone.

The bone had been ground into the shape of a **Key**.

"I am not A-K," he said.

He thrusted the key into the core interface of the server.

"I am **Zhang-004**."

"System Reboot. Count 1008."
