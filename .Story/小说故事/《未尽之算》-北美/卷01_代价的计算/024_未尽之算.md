Jiangcheng University. Department of History Office.

Su Huai stood in the center of the office, clutching her senior thesis—a document modified dozens of times until it was almost unrecognizable: *On the Rationality and Stability of Song Dynasty Fiscal Policy.*

It was her "entry ticket"—the work she’d bled for to impress her advisors, secure her graduate placement, and guarantee her a spot within the establishment.

But now, as she looked at every word, she felt as if she were staring at a pile of filth.

Every instance of "Rationality" was built on the blood and tears of a thousand Zhang Sans.
Every instance of "Stability" was anchored to the bones of a thousand Lin Xiaocaos.

She had once thought history was a little girl to be dressed up according to whim.
Now she knew: History is a blood-stained blade. If you do not grip the hilt, it turns and carves you instead.

*RIIIIIP—*

She tore the thesis in half.

"Are you out of your mind?" Fu Mu stood in the doorway, adjusting his glasses, his face a mask of shock. "That’s your key to graduate school. Without it, four years of labor go down the drain."

"What is the point of graduate school?" Su Huai tossed the shredded paper into the trash, watching the fragments flutter like snow. "To enter the system and become the next Old Zhao? Or the next Professor Ye—hiding in a study as an 'Observer,' watching tragedies unfold with cold indifference?"

She swung the iron box of archives onto her shoulder.

It was heavy. It was the life of Zhang San. It was the life of Professor Ye.

"I’m done studying history." Su Huai turned, her eyes burning with a clarity that only comes from staring death in the face. "I’m going to create it."

Fu Mu froze.

He looked at the girl who had always been timid—the one with the thick glasses who lived in books—and saw a soldier.

In that moment, he felt small.

He adjusted his glasses again to hide the moisture in his eyes, a bitter smirk touching his lips.

"So, Chen Mo isn't the only one who’s lost it. We’ve all gone mad."

He closed the thick volume of *Political Science Principles*—his personal bible. He dropped it into the same trash can, on top of Su Huai’s torn thesis.

"Fine. I’ll follow you into the fire. My models need new data anyway."

"What kind of data?"

"**Chaos**," Fu Mu said. "Only in chaos do you see the true face of humanity. Only at the edge of systemic collapse do you find the Global Optimal Solution."

---

The Bureau of Social Security’s underground garage.

Chen Mo had already started the modified armored van.

The frame had been hardened against radiation and coated in matte-black radar-absorbent material. In the dim fluorescent light, it looked like a crouching predator.

A-K was in the back seat, clutching a massive woven sack.

Inside was the bone key and a full case of instant ramen.

"I don't know a thing about 'Big Data'," A-K muttered, chewing on some dry noodles. "But I know you have to eat on the road. Man is iron, food is steel; miss a meal and your spirit will reel."

"Where's Fang Yu?" Chen Mo asked.

"In the trunk," A-K pointed behind him. "He said he’s building a mobile base station. He swears that as long as he’s with us, we’ll have a 5G uplink in the middle of a dead-zone."

The sound of feverish typing came from the back, along with Fang Yu’s excited muttering: *"Signal booster online... satellite link locked... even if we hit the dark side of the moon, I’ll keep us on the Grid."*

Chen Mo nodded.

The team was assembled.

*   **Chen Mo (Core / Ops)**: Carrying a dead hand and an immortal conscience.
*   **Su Huai (History / Ethics)**: Carrying the lessons of the past and the map of the future.
*   **Fu Mu (Strategy / Rationality)**: Carrying a cold brain and the wisdom of game theory.
*   **A-K (Logistics / Survival)**: Carrying the base instinct of a survivor and a mysterious lineage.
*   **Fang Yu (Data / Intel)**: Carrying technical fanaticism and a hacker’s insight into the machine.

It was a freakish squad. A bureaucrat, a student, a janitor, and a hacker.

They were the outcasts—the redundant ones, the ones the system had tried to delete.

But it was exactly these discarded souls who formed the final line of defense.

"Let’s go," Chen Mo said.

The heavy garage gate ground upward.

The sunlight outside was blinding.

It wasn't ordinary light. It was the dawn of a new world.

It might be the last time they ever saw the sun look this bright.

The next stop was the Fog of Information.

A place with no truth, only filtered fragments of speech. A place with no justice, only folded priorities.

The **Northwest**. The Wastes. The Hunting Grounds.

---

The van rolled out of the garage and merged into the sea of traffic.

The roar of the city hit them instantly. People were still busy, still rushing, still vibrating with the anxiety of their KPIs.

They didn't know that last night, their city had been on the verge of nuclear annihilation.
They didn't know that a handful of people had nearly paid with their lives to save them.

"This is the world we’re protecting," Su Huai sighed as she stared out the window. "Is it worth it?"

Chen Mo gripped the steering wheel with his good left hand. His right, wrapped in gauze, hung uselessly at his side.

With his left, he pulled the abacus from his coat.

The chipped corner was still empty. A vacancy left for the future.

The uranium was gone. Vanished into the crowd, just like the girl.

But he noticed something on the edge of the gap. A faint, dark-red stain.

Was it the blood from when Zhang San swallowed the cloth?
Was it the radiation burn from when Lin Xiaocao held it?
Or was it the rust from Old Zhao’s hands when he passed the torch?

The abacus was no longer a tool for calculation.

It was a **Vessel**.

It carried the blood, the tears, and the unfinished regrets of three generations.

"Unfinished Calculation," Chen Mo whispered.

"It’s not that we can't finish the count. It’s that we don't dare to."

"Because the moment you finish the calculation, a human becomes a number. As long as there is this missing corner, a human is still a human."

He hung the abacus from the rearview mirror.

As the van vibrated with the road, the beads let out a sharp, clicking sound.

*Click-clack. Click-clack.*

Like a send-off. Like a heartbeat. Like a countdown.

---

The Highway.

The van headed west.

The skyline shifted from skyscrapers to low-rise concrete, then to the barren, open fields.

The traffic thinned. The sky felt higher.

"Look at that," Fang Yu pointed out the window.

A massive crimson logistics truck was overtaking them.

It was a heavy-duty semi-trailer, eighteen wheels humming against the asphalt like a fortress on the move.

On the side of the trailer, a logo was emblazoned in bright red.

It was a **Chipped Abacus**.

And beneath the symbol, in small, black type:

**[EXPRESS REACH LOGISTICS: MISSION CRITICAL]**

Chen Mo slammed on the brakes.

The tires shrieked against the pavement, leaving two long, black streaks of rubber.

He stared at the truck.

That logo... it was identical to the one Lin Xiaocao had drawn.

The angle of the chip, the alignment of the beads, even the specific shade of crimson—it was perfect.

"That’s her truck," Chen Mo’s voice shook. "That’s her road sign."

"She’s already infiltrated the logistics network," Fu Mu said, his fingers flying across his tablet. "Express Reach... it’s a shelf company founded two days ago. Registered in Jiayuguan. The legal representative is... **Zhang San**."

"Zhang San?" Su Huai gasped.

"It’s a callsign," Chen Mo said. "She’s telling us she’s the new Zhang San."

"She’s using the logistics grid to move the things the digital grid has blocked."

"Moving what?" A-K asked.

"The Truth," Su Huai said. "Or... Hope."

"Or a **Bomb**," Fang Yu added. "Don't forget, she still has the code for the 'Perfect Gene'."

The red truck faded into the distance, vanishing over the horizon.

Only a thick cloud of exhaust remained, along with that mission statement: **MISSION CRITICAL.**

Chen Mo restarted the engine.

He followed the truck.

The engine’s roar sounded like a bugle call across the wastes.

**Goodbye, Kaifeng.**
**Goodbye, the Bureau.**
**Hello, the Unknown Wastes.**

The new game has started.
