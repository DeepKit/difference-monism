# 第12集：影喻时刻

> **《未尽之算》·第12集·影喻时刻**

The Safe House.

It was an air-raid shelter located beneath the old district. Fang Yu had transformed it into a geek fortress cluttered with glowing screens and a chaotic web of cables.

The air was thick with the scent of stagnant mildew and the sharp ozone tang of overheating hardware.

Chen Mo, Su Huai, Fu Mu, A-K, and the sleeping Lin Xiaocao were all here.

Outside, the city wailed with the sirens of a full-scale manhunt. Inside, this tomb-like vault was the only sanctuary left.

"What now?" Su Huai asked. She clutched the sealed bag of fragments as if it were a physical anchor to reality.

"Wait," Chen Mo said, his eyes fixed on the trending index on the screen. "Wait for the public narrative to reach its boiling point. Wait for the system to blink."

"And in the meantime?" A-K asked. "Do we play cards?"

"We watch a movie."

Fang Yu pressed a button.

A beam of light projected onto a bare, whitewashed wall.

It was a classic from 1993—*Schindler’s List*.

Black and white frames flickering in the dark. Only the little girl in the red coat was in color.

---

The four of them sat in the darkness, silence punctuated only by the low hum of the projector.

On the screen, Oskar Schindler was drinking with Nazi officers. He was all smiles, handing out cigars, gold, and women.

He was bribing them. He was corroding the machine from within.

And for what?

For a list.

The people on that list were Jews. But in Schindler’s mouth, they were "Essential Personnel."

*"This one-armed old man? Oh, he’s the best machinist I have. Without him, the factory stalls."*
*"This little girl? Her hands are small; she can reach into the machinery to clean the parts. She is indispensable."*

The Nazi officers believed him.

Because Schindler’s reasons aligned perfectly with the system’s own logic: **Efficiency**.

For the efficiency of the factory, to produce more shells for the front lines, these Jews had to live.

It was a deception.

A magnificent, desperate lie.

---

"Do you think Schindler was a hero?" Fu Mu asked suddenly.

His voice sounded cold and detached in the gloom.

"Of course he was," Su Huai said. "He saved 1,100 people. He was one of the greatest humanitarians of the war."

"No." Fu Mu pointed at Schindler on the wall. "He was a **System Exploit User**."

Fu Mu stood up, his shadow cutting through the projector beam. His silhouette overlaid Schindler’s face, their profiles merging into one.

"Schindler didn't overthrow the Nazis. Instead, he weaponized their greed. He used the system’s own rules against itself."

"The Nazi rule was: Those without value must die (Jews = Waste). Those with value may live (Machinists = Assets)."

"What did Schindler do?"

"He didn't go to the Nazis to debate 'Racial Equality'—that would have been suicide. He simply did one thing: **He disguised 'Waste' as 'Assets'.**"

"He lied to the system."

"He used the system's logic (only the useful live) to sabotage the system's purpose (extermination)."

Fu Mu turned back to look at Chen Mo.

"Chen Mo, that is exactly what you need to do."

"Shorting the system’s credit earlier felt good, but it was a suicide pact. If the system actually collapses, Lin Xiaocao dies anyway because there’s no medicine left."

"You can't save everyone. You can't dismantle the Bureau."

"You can only be like Schindler. You make a list."

"And you disguise those condemned to die as 'Indispensable Personnel'."

"You have to lie to Old Zhao. You have to lie to the Algorithm."

---

Chen Mo stared at the wall.

On the screen, the girl in the red coat moved through the black-and-white crowd. She was so vibrant, so jarringly bright.

Like Lin Xiaocao.

Like that girl in the red coat from the Song Dynasty.

The only spot of bright color in the system. The only spot of fresh blood.

She was Irreducible. No matter how the system calculated or purged, that red would always be there, stinging its eyes.

Chen Mo felt a wave of nausea. The girl on the wall suddenly split into two, then three... the afterimages danced across his retinas.

He had to lean against the table, digging his fingernails into the wood to keep himself upright.

"Disguise..." Chen Mo whispered. His voice felt distant, as if coming from deep underwater.

He remembered Chapter 1.

Back then, Old Zhao had called Lin Xiaocao a "Negative Asset." Her genetic defect would drag down the "Optimization" of the species.

But later, through the suanpan's decoding, they found she was a "Perfect Evolutionary Entity."

What if... he flipped the logic?

What if he didn't call her a "Virus," but a "Vaccine"?

What if he didn't call her a "Burden," but a "Strategic Reserve"?

Chen Mo’s eyes sharpened.

"I know what to do."

He stood up and walked to the whiteboard, grabbing a marker.

"I’m making a list."

"Not a list of names. I’m making a **Balance Sheet**."

He wrote two phrases on the board:

**NEGATIVE ASSET** -> **CORE ASSET**

"The system wants to purge Negative Assets? Fine. Then I’ll make Lin Xiaocao a Core Asset."

"I’m going to prove that by not saving her, the Social Security system won't just lose money—it will lose the **Future**."

"How?" Fang Yu asked.

"Remember that 'Perfect Immunity' gene?" Chen Mo said. "If that gene can be extracted, replicated, and turned into a serum..."

"It’s a Universal Vaccine!" Su Huai gasped.

"Exactly. A Universal Vaccine." Chen Mo let out a tired, cynical laugh. "What’s the market price for that? Billions? Trillions?"

"If the Bureau kills her, they aren't 'optimizing' a cost. They are burning trillions in potential patent revenue."

"Can Old Zhao afford that loss? Can the Algorithm Optimization Group?"

Fu Mu pushed his glasses up, a rare, thin smile appearing on his face.

"Brutal. This is called **'Value Re-evaluation'**."

"You don't beg for mercy. You tempt them with greed."

"But," Fang Yu threw a bucket of cold water on the idea, "we have no proof. The genetic map self-destructed."

"And," A-K added, "even if we had proof, the system could just kill her and take the body back to the lab for study. It wants the genes, not the girl."

Chen Mo froze.

Yes. A dead person’s genes are still genes.

If the system knew Lin Xiaocao was "God’s own blueprint," it would only kill her faster to secure the specimen.

"So, we can’t just paint her in gold." Chen Mo looked at the crying Schindler on the screen. "We have to add a lock."

"A lock that makes the system **'desire the fruit but fear the tree'**."

Chen Mo thought of Zhang San.

The bloody scrap of a coat he had swallowed.

The symbols carved into the iron pillar.

"I'm going to turn her into a **Poison Pill**."

Chen Mo’s gaze turned frantic, almost manic.

"Fang Yu, can you write a program?"

"What kind of program?"

"A **Dead Man's Switch** linked to Lin Xiaocao’s vitals."

"If her heart stops—or if she is more than a hundred meters away from me..."

"The program triggers automatically. It will upload the raw data of that 'Perfect Gene' to every open-source community on the planet."

Fang Yu’s eyes went wide.

"What are you doing? Once it’s open-source, the gene is worthless! The pharmaceutical companies will want your head on a platter!"

"Exactly," Chen Mo said. "It’s called the **'Scorched Earth Policy'**."

"Tell the system: This treasure either stays with me (so I can save her), or it belongs to humanity (open-source and free)."

"The one thing it will NEVER be is yours (proprietary to the Bureau)."

"If they dare touch a single hair on her head, I’ll turn their trillion-dollar patent dream into a puff of smoke."

It was a gamble.

The stake was the future of humanity.

The bet was on the system’s own greed.

"Are you in?" Chen Mo asked.

The trio looked at each other.

Su Huai took a deep breath. "You're a maniac."

Fu Mu wiped his glasses. "The move is game-theoretically optimal."

A-K cracked open a bottle of coke. "Let's do it. Those with nothing to lose can't be threatened by those in shoes."

Chen Mo smiled.

He looked at Schindler on the wall.

Schindler was weeping, looking at his gold ring: *"This ring... this ring could have saved two more people... why didn't I sell it..."*

I wanted to weep, too.

But I didn't have a gold ring.

I only had this suanpan.

And I was going to use this suanpan to calculate the end of the system itself.
