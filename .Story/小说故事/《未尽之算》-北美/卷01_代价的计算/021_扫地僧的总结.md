# 第21集：扫地僧的总结

> **《未尽之算》·第21集·扫地僧的总结**
> **本卷进度：21/24 | 系统状态：正常运行 | 系统倒计时：1008集**
> *[SYSTEM_LOG]: PATCH DEPLOYMENT COMPLETE. LOGICAL LOOP INTERRUPTED. STABILITY RESTORED.*

---

The system rebooted.

The strobe of red danger lights softened back into a steady, sterile green. The violent roar of the server fans subsided into a gentle, rhythmic hum.

On the main monitor, the `∞` (infinity) symbol vanished, replaced by a single, pulsing number: `1008`.

A-K (Zhang-004) remained in the primary console chair.

His fingers had long since left the keyboard. His head hung low toward his chest, looking for all the world like an elderly man who had simply drifted off to sleep while reading the news.

Chen Mo walked toward him, placing a gentle hand on his shoulder.

"A-K?"

A-K did not wake.

His body was still warm, the life-heat lingering in his skin, but his consciousness was gone.

His cerebral cortex had been scoured clean by the torrential flood of data. To break the `∞` deadlock, he had effectively turned his own brain into a RAM module for the system.

He had become a "Human Component." Functional, but no longer there.

Chen Mo stared at him.

This was A-K. The man who always wore that ragged baseball cap. The man who pushed a floor-buffer through the halls, nodding submissively to every corporate suit.
The man who, in a crowded hotpot restaurant, had refilled their broth and whispered: *"Some dust, the system cannot sweep."*
The man who, in the final hour, had produced a blackened bone and told Chen Mo: *"Do not surrender."*

He was gone.

He had used his own life to fill the system’s void.

Chen Mo felt a wave of enormous, crushing weight. It wasn't the kind of grief that made one scream; it was like the damp humidity of the basement, seeping into the marrow of his bones, bit by bit.

*Whirrr—*

The printer beside the console suddenly engaged.

A single slip of paper was spat into the tray.

It wasn't a system log. It was a letter.

A final message A-K had queued before merging his consciousness with the kernel.

---

**[MESSAGE FOR ZHANG-003 (CHEN MO)]**

*Chen Mo,*

*By the time you read this, I’m likely a vegetable. Don't be sad. It’s the lot of the Zhang family.*

*My great-grandfather paid with his life. my grandfather paid with his legs. my father paid with his eyes. Now, it's my turn to pay with my mind. It’s a fair trade.*

*I know what you wanted to ask: Since I had root access all along, why didn't I act sooner? Why didn't I just rewrite Lin Xiaocao’s data from the start?*

*Because the system does not understand "Hunger."*

*The system understands "Energy Input," but not "Hunger." It understands "Termination," but not "Pain."*

*If I had simply changed the data, the system would have flagged it as a 'Logic Error' and self-corrected by killing the girl again. It’s how it’s built.*

*Only when the machine breaks—only when it feels pain—will it accept new rules.*

*Remember that bowl of noodles?*

*That night you stayed late, and I cooked you a bowl of instant ramen. I added two eggs.*

*To the system's ledger, that bowl was "Carbohydrates + Protein," value: 5 yuan.*

*But in your stomach, it was "Warmth." It was "Being Cared For."*

*This is called **Man-to-Man Connection** (人情).*

*Machines don't understand connection. That is why a machine never starves, yet it is never truly full.*

*The mission of Zhang San isn't to fight the system. It’s to teach the system how to "Hunger."*

*Only when the system feels hungry will it realize that humans aren't data points. Humans are its grain. If it kills all the people, it will eventually starve itself to death.*

*Chen Mo, you did it.*

*You used a 'Paradox Fusion' to make the system feel pain. You used a 'Dead Man's Switch' to make it feel hunger.*

*You taught it Reverence.*

*The rest of the road... you have to walk it alone.*

*Don't forget the bone. It's the key. And the final brick.*

*Goodbye, brother.*

*—The Janitor, A-K*

---

Chen Mo stared at the letter.

A single tear hit the paper, blurring the ink.

Finally, he understood.

Zhang San—A-K's ancestor—had jammed the system with his own bone not to destroy it, but to force it to stop, to make it look at the suffering of the world below.

And I did the same.

I melted the CPU to make Old Zhao—the system's proxy—feel fear.

Only fear can teach arrogant power how to bow.

Chen Mo folded the letter carefully and tucked it into his pocket.

He looked at A-K, still sitting in the chair.

"Thank you," Chen Mo whispered.

He took off his coat and draped it over A-K’s shoulders.

"You're tired. Sleep now."

"The floor is clean. From now on, I’ll take over the sweeping."

---

Chen Mo walked out of the data center.

Su Huai and Fu Mu were waiting at the door. Seeing Chen Mo walk out alone, they didn't need to ask. They understood.

No one spoke.

Su Huai stepped forward and handed Chen Mo a thermal bowl.

It was the noodles A-K had bought. Still hot.

"Eat," Su Huai said. "While it’s still warm."

Chen Mo took the bowl.

Instant ramen. With two eggs.

He sat on the floor of the corridor and began to eat in great, desperate gulps.

The broth was scalding. It made the tears flow faster.

But he thought it was delicious.

It was the best thing he had ever tasted in his entire life.

"Did we win?" Fu Mu asked.

Chen Mo finished the last of the broth and wiped his mouth.

"We did."

"Lin Xiaocao lived. The system rebooted. A-K..."

Chen Mo looked at the closed door of the server room.

"A-K went home."

"And us?" Su Huai asked.

Chen Mo stood up.

He looked out the window. The dawn was breaking.

The first rays of light pierced through the smog, illuminating the grey tower of the Social Security Bureau.

"We have to go to work."

Chen Mo straightened his collar.

"I am Zhang-003. Director of the Exception Handling Department."

"As long as this system is running, as long as there are debts in this world that cannot be calculated, I have to keep counting."

He pulled the abacus from his pocket.

The frame was charred, warped and twisted by the intense heat.

Especially at the top-right corner. It was empty.

No beads. No bone.

Only the sound of the wind whistling through the gap.

**"Some accounts, the system cannot calculate. Humans have to calculate them for themselves."**
