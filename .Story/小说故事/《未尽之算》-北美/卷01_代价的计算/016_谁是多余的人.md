# 第16集：谁是多余的人

> **《未尽之算》·第16集·谁是多余的人**
> **本卷进度：16/24 | 系统状态：离线 | 系统倒计时：暂停**

---

The lights in the Safe House had died.

But soon, a piercing beam from a flashlight cut through the darkness. Dust motes danced in the glare, looking like a miniature blizzard.

At the end of the beam stood Old Zhao.

He stood in the doorway, holding a gun.

It wasn't the stun gun issued to security. This was a real firearm. The black eye of the muzzle was aimed directly at the center of Chen Mo’s forehead.

"You destroyed it." Old Zhao’s voice echoed in the darkness, carrying a heart-stopping calm. "The Social Security system, the Data Center, the power to this entire building. You’ve dragged us back to the Stone Age."

"I simply saved a life." Chen Mo stood to shield Lin Xiaocao.

"No. You killed a god."

Old Zhao stepped into the room, the crunch of broken glass beneath his boots echoing in the narrow space.

"Without the system, there is no order. This is a jungle now. I have the gun, I have the light, and I am the law."

He jerked the flashlight, the glare briefly illuminating Chen Mo’s pale, sweat-slicked face.

"Chen Mo, do you still think you're a 'Hero'? Right now, you're just a redundant piece of meat."

Chen Mo looked at Old Zhao.

He didn't flinch.

He was gambling.

He was gambling that Old Zhao was still the man who poured tea for his subordinates and soaked goji berries in his thermos. He was gambling on the man who always left a lamp on when Chen Mo worked late.

"Pull the trigger," Chen Mo said. "If you think killing me will reboot the system, then do it."

"You think I won't?"

Old Zhao’s finger tightened on the trigger.

*CLICK.*

Chen Mo closed his eyes.

For a heartbeat, time seemed to solidify.

But there was no pain. No spray of blood.

Only the sharp, hollow sound of a firing pin hitting an empty chamber.

*CLICK.*

Empty.

---

Chen Mo opened his eyes.

Old Zhao reversed the gun, offering the grip to him.

"No bullets." Old Zhao ejected the magazine and tossed it to the floor. "I’m not the type with the stomach for murder."

He slumped down onto a heap of tangled cables, looking as if a massive weight had just been lifted from his shoulders. His back was hunched; he seemed to have aged ten years in a single second.

"Chen Mo, that 'Paradox Fusion' move? It was colder than what I did."

"What you did?" Chen Mo paused.

Old Zhao reached into his robe and tossed something to Chen Mo.

Chen Mo caught it.

It was a suanpan.

An exact match for his own. Redwood, with a chipped corner.

The only difference was that this one was older, its wood polished to a deep dark sheen by years of touch, and it bore deep, jagged knife scars.

"Twenty years ago, I sat where you sit. I encountered a girl who had to die. I thought about blowing up the system, too."

Old Zhao touched the worn beads, his gaze turning clouded and distant.

"But I didn't have the nerve. I signed the paperwork. I took the promotion. I became Old Zhao."

"The girl died. She died the day after I signed my name."

"From that day on, I haven't slept a full night through."

Old Zhao looked up at Chen Mo:

"I am **Zhang-002**."

"Did you think 'Zhang San' was just a name? No. It’s the callsign for this department. Every 'Exception Handling Officer' is Zhang San."

"And you... you are **Zhang-003**."

---

"So... this was all a test?"

Chen Mo felt the blood in his veins turn to ice.

"The gas was a drill? The girl’s death was a drill? Even the gun?"

"No. The girl was truly going to die. The gas was real—if I hadn't shut the valve. And while the gun was empty, I can tell you truthfully: I thought about killing you."

Old Zhao stood up, unscrewing the lid of his thermos again.

"The system truly intended to purge the Negative Assets. But me? I wanted to see if this generation’s 'Zhang San' had any spine."

"If you had signed, you would have become the next 'Old Zhao.' You would have lived a very comfortable life, but you would be dead on the inside."

"But because you fought back—because you were willing to destroy the system itself..."

Old Zhao looked at the server racks, which were still radiating a dying heat even in the dark.

"Only then are you worthy of handling the *real* Exception Cases."

"What does that mean?"

"The real exceptions are never as simple as saving one girl."

Old Zhao walked to the back wall and pushed open a concealed door.

Inside was an isolated server rack. Though the main power was out, this unit hummed with its own dedicated power supply.

Green lights flickered on the interface.

"This is the core secret of the Social Security Bureau. We call it **'The Black Box'**."

---

Chen Mo stepped through the doorway.

On the screen was Lin Xiaocao’s profile.

**NAME: SUBJECT ZERO**
**STATUS: ALIVE**
**NOTE: GENETIC LOCK DISENGAGED (AUTHORIZED BY CHEN MO)**

"She isn't an ordinary person." Old Zhao pointed at the display. "She is a product of previous-generation genetic engineering. Her DNA carries a code capable of 'rewriting' the human immune system."

"That code is both the cure and the plague."

"If controlled, it can cure cancer. If it goes rogue, it is a global pandemic."

"The system judged her 'Expected Contribution Value' as negative not because she was sick. It was because **she is the ultimate Variable**."

Old Zhao turned back to Chen Mo, a thin, cruel smile touching his lips.

"Chen Mo, you saved her. To save her, you burned down the system’s firewall."

"And now, the firewall is gone. The Variable has been released."

"You aren't a hero."

"You are **Pandora**."

---

Chen Mo slumped to the floor.

The suanpan slipped from his fingers, clattering against the concrete.

Had he won?

No. He had lost and lost utterly.

He thought he was fighting evil. In truth, he was releasing an uncontrollable future.

The system was cold because it was a cage for demons. And he had just opened the cage.

"Then what do I do?" Chen Mo asked.

"There is no 'Doing' anything," Old Zhao said, patting him on the shoulder. "This is the price."

"You chose freedom? Then you must endure the chaos."

"You chose humanity? Then you must face humanity’s greed and selfishness."

Old Zhao pointed toward the darkness outside the Safe House.

"Right now, the whole city is offline. People are panicked. They are angry. If the power isn't back by morning, they will start looting the supermarkets."

"That is the gift you have given this city."

Chen Mo looked at Old Zhao.

"And you?"

"Me?" Old Zhao smiled. "I'm retired."

He set his thermos on the table.

"This cup has been with me for twenty years. It belongs to you now."

"From today on, you are the Director of the Exception Handling Department. You are Zhang-003."

"This mess? It’s yours to clean up."

Old Zhao turned to leave.

"Old Zhao!" Chen Mo called out to him. "Where are you going?"

"Home." Old Zhao waved a hand over his shoulder. "My granddaughter is waiting for me to tell her a story."

"Oh, and that suanpan."

Old Zhao stopped for a second.

"That's a real antique. It belonged to the original Zhang San, a thousand years ago."

"When he died, he swallowed it. Someone dug it out of his ashes later."

"Keep it safe. It is the **Soul** (魂) of this trade."

---

Old Zhao left.

The Safe House held only Chen Mo, Lin Xiaocao, and the humming, emerald silence of the Black Box.

Chen Mo’s back, which had been held ramrod straight all night, finally collapsed.

*Cough.*

He clutched his mouth as a spray of dark blood hit the floor.

He had been holding it back while facing Old Zhao. The metallic fluid had risen to his throat long ago, forced down by sheer will.

He was at the end of his rope.

Chen Mo looked at the Black Box.

On the screen, Lin Xiaocao’s genetic map was still rotating.

The chipped corner of the spiral had been filled.

It looked so perfect.

So perfect it was terrifying.

"Uncle," Lin Xiaocao pulled at his sleeve. "I'm hungry."

Chen Mo looked down.

He looked into those clear, wide eyes.

In those eyes, he saw no demon, no plague.

He only saw a hungry child.

"Okay." Chen Mo touched her hairless head. "Uncle will take you to get something to eat."

He picked her up and walked out of the Safe House.

The corridor ahead was pitch black.

But he wasn't afraid anymore.

Because he knew he wasn't walking alone.

Behind him, a thousand Zhang Sans were watching.

And in his arms, he held a future that could destroy the world—or save it.

That was enough.
