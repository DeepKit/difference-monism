"I am committing a crime."

Chen Mo sat in the passenger seat, a military-grade laptop cradled in his lap. The cold glow of the screen mirrored on his pale face, making his gaze look both manic and unnervingly calm.

"I’m hijacking the citywide 'Sky-Eye' surveillance network," Chen Mo said. "Since the main servers at the Bureau are fried, I’m using the backup credentials Old Zhao left me. These permissions are only meant for a 'Stage One Combat Readiness' status."

"Stage One?" Fang Yu asked while gripping the steering wheel. "Isn't that for nuclear war?"

"This *is* a nuclear war."

Chen Mo pointed to the red dot moving rapidly across the screen.

"She’s moving."

"And she’s drawing."

---

On the screen, the red dot moved like a scarlet paintbrush across the city’s grid.

Its path was bizarre. It didn't take straight lines or main boulevards. Instead, it snaked through abandoned alleys, sewer systems, and subway maintenance tunnels.

But if you connected the dots of the trail, a staggering pattern emerged on the map.

*   **Stop 1**: The Central Plaza.
*   **Stop 2**: The Financial District Spire.
*   **Stop 3**: The Old Quarter Train Station.

As the dot continued, a massive shape crystallized over the city map.

A rectangle.

With horizontal and vertical lines intersected by nodes that looked like counting beads.

It was...

"An abacus," Su Huai whispered from the back seat, her breath hitching.

And the top-right corner of this citywide abacus was missing a piece.

It was **Chen Mo’s** abacus.

It was **Zhang San’s** abacus.

Lin Xiaocao was using her very footsteps to turn the entire city into a gargantuan, ancient calculating tool.

"She’s taunting the system," Fu Mu analyzed from the back. "She’s telling them: your precision algorithms are nothing more than a broken abacus to me."

"No," Chen Mo shook his head. "She’s **Summoning**."

"Summoning what?"

"Whatever fills that chipped corner."

---

"Where is she heading?" Fang Yu asked.

"The Chip," Chen Mo pointed to the map. "If this pattern is meant to be complete, her destination is the Northeast corner of the city."

The area was a derelict industrial zone.

It used to be a chemical plant, shuttered years ago due to severe contamination.

The **Hongda Chemical Plant**.

The site where, a thousand years ago, the **Crematorium** stood.

"Hold on!" Fang Yu slammed the accelerator.

The modified van let out a roar like a wounded beast and surged into the night like a black bullet.

---

Hongda Chemical Plant.

It was a cemetery of steel. rusted pipes snaked through the tall weeds like the skeletons of titans. The air was thick with a biting scent of sulfur and that metallic tang of old rust.

The scent of death.

In the center of the complex sat an abandoned amusement park.

It had been built decades ago for the plant’s workers and their families. Now, the only thing that occupied the space was the wind.

A rusted Ferris wheel groaned in the gusts, a sound like a dying man’s wheeze. The carousel had long ago ceased its rotation; several of the horse heads were snapped off, exposing the rebar beneath—jagged and ghastly.

Chen Mo held his gun—the empty one Old Zhao had left, though he’d managed to find a few rounds for it since. He waded through waist-high weeds.

"Come out!" he shouted.

No answer.

Only the hollow whistle of the wind through the pipes.

Suddenly, Chen Mo felt a wave of nausea.

It wasn't psychological. It was biological. His stomach cramped, acid rising in his throat. A prickling, stinging sensation began to crawl across his skin.

"Chen Mo, watch out!" Fang Yu’s frantic voice crackled in his earpiece. "The Geiger counter is redlining! Radiation levels here are ten thousand times above safety limits!"

"What?"

"This isn't just a chemical plant! It’s an undeclared transfer station for nuclear waste!"

Before Chen Mo could process the warning, the world shifted.

The carousel moved.

With a shriek of sparking electricity, the ride that had been dead for a decade suddenly flared with colorful lights and began to turn.

Music blared.

A distorted, haunting version of *Jingle Bells*. Due to the aging circuits, the sound stuttered and warped, like a ghost trying to sing.

On one of the black horses sat a small figure.

She was in her hospital gown, barefoot. She was holding something.

Chen Mo instinctively patted his pocket.

Empty.

The abacus was gone.

When had she taken it? In the hospital? In the basement?

"Uncle."

Lin Xiaocao turned her head to look at him.

Her eyes were devoid of white; they were absolute, lightless voids. Her skin was a translucent, sickly grey-white, the veins beneath appearing a bruised, unearthly purple.

"Your abacus was broken."

Her voice was soft, yet it cut through the blaring music and lodged itself in Chen Mo’s brain.

"I fixed it for you."

She held up the abacus.

In the chipped corner, there was now a bead.

White. Perfectly round.

Under the flashing carnival lights, it glowed with a ghostly, subterranean blue.

It wasn't an optic light. It was cold, pulsing with a light that didn't belong to the world above.

It wasn't bone.

Chen Mo’s heart constricted.

He recognized that light.

**Cherenkov Radiation.**

The glow of high-concentration radioactive isotopes ionizing the air.

**Uranium.**

---

"Don't move!" Fang Yu screamed in the earpiece. "Chen Mo! Run! That’s a direct source! That’s a highly enriched uranium sphere! In seconds, your DNA will be shredded!"

Chen Mo didn't run.

Step by step, he walked toward the carousel.

With every stride, the nausea intensified. His hair felt statically charged, standing on end.

"Xiaocao," his voice trembled. "Put it down."

"Why?" She tilted her head. "It’s pretty."

"It will kill you."

"I'm already dead," she laughed. "The system says I'm dead. You say I'm dead. Dead people don't fear dying."

She hopped down from the horse and walked toward him.

With every step she took, the abacus in her hand glowed brighter.

Chen Mo felt as if his skin were on fire. High-energy particles were broadsiding his cells.

"Uncle, didn't you want to save me?"

Lin Xiaocao offered the abacus to him.

"Now, I have power."

"If I throw this bead into the big furnace over there..."

She pointed to the massive, beast-like reactor vessel in the distance.

"The whole city will turn to ash."

"And then the system can never find us."

"We’ll be free."

---

Chen Mo stared at her.

 the girl who had once innocently offered him a piece of candy was now a child holding a nuclear warhead.

Who had turned her into this?

The System.

The system wanted to kill her, so she learned how to kill.
The system treated her like a virus, so she became one.

This was the ultimate form of **Vicious Compliance**.

If you treat a human like a demon, they become one.

"Stop being a fool." Chen Mo tossed the gun aside.

He knelt, bringing his eyes level with hers.

"That isn't freedom. That’s extinction."

"If you destroy this city, you become the system."

"You become the person holding the scissors."

Lin Xiaocao froze.

She looked at the abacus in her hand. Then she looked at the invisible scissors poised over Chen Mo’s head.

"But Uncle, if we don't do this, how do we live?"

"We don't need to live by their rules."

Chen Mo reached out and gently gripped the glowing abacus.

*HISS—*

Agony.

The sensation of his skin being seared by radiation. It felt like plunging his hand into a vat of boiling oil.

But he didn't let go.

"We live by ours."

"Zhang San’s rule was: **Human life is paramount.**"

"My rule is: **I'm taking you home.**"

Chen Mo yanked the abacus away.

He pried the glowing bead from the corner and clenched it in a death grip inside his fist.

Using his own flesh to shroud the lethal radiation.

"Go."

He scooped Lin Xiaocao into his arms and turned to run.

"Fang Yu! Drive!"

"Where?!"

"The hospital! She's contaminated!"

---

Behind them, a massive explosion rocked the earth.

The carousel blew.

The overheated, ancient motor couldn't handle the strain and finally detonated.

Flames reached for the sky.

The shockwave threw Chen Mo and Lin Xiaocao forward.

They slammed into the weeds.

Chen Mo felt his internal organs shift. His mouth filled with the metallic taste of blood.

But he hadn't let go of the uranium bead.

He looked at Lin Xiaocao in his arms. She was unconscious, but she was breathing.

*Good.*

*As long as there is life, there is a calculation left to be made.*

Chen Mo struggled to his feet.

He looked at his hand.

The hand that had held the bead had turned a charcoal black.

The skin at the fingertips was peeling away like burnt paper, revealing the chalky white bone beneath.

There was no blood.

The blood vessels had been instantly "dry-fired" by the high-energy particles.

The nerve endings were dead; he didn't feel pain. He only felt a deep, marrow-deep numbness.

This was the **Cost**.

To mend the chip in Zhang San’s abacus, he had paid with a hand.

Perhaps more than a hand.

Perhaps his life.
