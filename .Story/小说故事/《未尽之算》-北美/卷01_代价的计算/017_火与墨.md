The Song Dynasty. Market Square.

It was a fine day for a killing. The sun hung high and unforgiving; there was not a breath of wind to stir the heavy air.

The execution grounds were packed. Vendors sold melon seeds, others hawked herbal tea, and some even carried children on their shoulders to see the spectacle.

"Burn him! Burn the corpse thief!"

Cheers erupted from the crowd. They were celebrating the death of a "criminal," but more so, they were celebrating a rare spark of excitement in their monotonous lives. They didn't know who Zhang San was, nor did they care what he had done. They only knew that today, there was a show.

Zhang San was bound to the stake. A hemp gag was stuffed into his mouth, stealing his voice. His legs had been broken; they hung limply, devoid of feeling. But he could still feel the heat.

It was the roar of the dry wood at his feet as it caught fire.

Registrar Zhou sat on the overseer’s platform, tea in hand, his gaze as cold as stone.

He watched Zhang San in the flames as if watching an ant tossed into a furnace.

*Zhang San, oh Zhang San,* he thought. *In your next life, remember to keep your eyes closed. Some things, once seen, are a death sentence.*

The flames grew.

Orange tongues of fire, like greedy snakes, climbed the stake, licking at the soles of Zhang San’s feet.

Flesh hissed. Grease from his body dripped into the fire, making the wood pop and crackle.

Did it hurt?

Yes.

It hurt beyond the capacity of words to describe.

Zhang San’s body twisted violently in the inferno like a dying fish landed on hot sand. Muffled screams issued from his throat, but blocked by the gag, they turned into low, guttural groans.

But he did not close his eyes.

Through the shimmering, distorted air, he locked his gaze on Registrar Zhou.

In those eyes, there was no fear. There was only a chilling, mockery of a smile.

He was laughing.

In the heart of the fire, using his final reserve of strength, he extended a finger—already bitten raw—toward the pillar behind him.

It was an iron pillar.

Fire cannot destroy iron.

And fire cannot destroy me.

---

*Hiss—*

His finger touched the scalding iron, sounding like a brand against raw meat.

Zhang San’s fingernail split instantly. The skin of his fingertip fried to the metal, sticking there.

But he didn't stop.

With that charred, blackened finger, he clawed at the rust on the pillar. once. Twice.

Blood mixed with rust, seeping into the microscopic crevices of the iron.

He was writing.

Not a grievance. Not a name.

He drew a symbol.

It was the shape of the palm print he had seen on the girl’s bone.

The shape of the chipped abacus.

A symbol only a madman could read.

A riddle only a person from the future could solve.

*Registrar Zhou, you can burn my ledgers, and you can burn my body. But you cannot burn this iron pillar.*

*This pillar will stand here for a hundred years, a thousand years.*

*One day, someone will see it.*

*One day, someone will understand it.*

The flames engulfed his finger. Then his arm. Then his final scrap of consciousness.

In the moment the darkness fell, Zhang San thought he saw a man.

A man in strange clothes, standing outside the crowd.

Chen Mo.

*Don't cry,* Zhang San said to him in his mind.

*Look closely. This is my final problem for you.*

*Don't let this fire burn for nothing.*

Modern Day. The Hospital.

Chen Mo woke with a violent start on the hospital bed.

"Xiaocao!"

He yanked the IV line from the back of his hand and stumbled out of the room.

"Mr. Chen! You can't move! You're still suffering from internal bleeding!" a nurse shouted after him.

Chen Mo didn't hear a word.

His memory was still stuck on that night in the Safe House. After he had collapsed vomiting blood, A-K and the others must have rushed him to the nearest emergency room.

What time was it?

He checked the wall clock.

2:00 PM.

Only two hours left until the system rebooted.

But those twenty-four hours of disruption were long enough for "Subject Zero" to vanish.

The hospital ward was empty.

The white sheets still bore the depression where someone had been lying. On the nightstand sat a cup of water, still warm.

The air still held the faint, lingering sweetness of a fruit drop.

"Where is she?" Chen Mo grabbed a passing nurse.

"I don't know!" the nurse cried, startled. "She was here a second ago. The power went out, I went to check the generator, and when I came back, she was gone."

Chen Mo lunged back into the room.

He searched under the bed, in the cabinets, in the bathroom.

Nothing.

Lin Xiaocao had evaporated.

"Uncle."

Suddenly, he heard a soft call.

Chen Mo whipped his head around.

No one.

The voice seemed to have come from the corner of the room.

He walked over.

On the baseboard in the corner, there was a line of crooked text.

Written in red crayon.

**“谢谢叔叔。游戏开始了。”**
**[Thank you, Uncle. The game has started.]**

Beside the text, a symbol had been drawn.

A rectangle. With a piece missing from the top-right corner.

The Chipped Abacus.

Chen Mo traced the symbol with his finger.

The crayon marks were fresh, holding a strange warmth. The warmth of Lin Xiaocao’s palm.

This wasn't a call for help.

This was a **Declaration of War**.

Lin Xiaocao was gone. She had taken the "Perfect Gene" that could end the world and vanished into the sea of people.

She was no longer the little girl who only wanted candy.

She was the Virus. She was the Hunter.

And the entire city was her hunting ground.

---

"Chen Mo!"

Su Huai and Fu Mu burst through the door.

"Did you find her?"

Chen Mo shook his head.

"She’s gone."

"Where?"

"I don't know." Chen Mo stood, looking out at the dark, powerless city outside the window. "Somewhere we can't see."

"Then what do we do?" Fang Yu arrived, clutching a tablet. "The system reboots soon. Once it's back, citywide surveillance returns. If the Algorithm Group finds her missing, they'll lock down everything."

"We have to find her before the system does."

"But how?"

Chen Mo pulled the suanpan from his robe.

"With this."

"An abacus?"

"Yes." Chen Mo pointed at the chipped corner. "Zhang San didn't just leave this behind as a key."

"He left it as a **Compass**."

"What do you mean?"

"Watch."

Chen Mo set the suanpan on the table.

He took a small magnet—swiped from the nurses' station—and moved it near the abacus.

The beads didn't move.

But at the chipped corner, there was an invisible attraction pulling at the magnet.

"There’s a shard of magnetite inside," Fang Yu muttered, leaning in. "And it’s been specially magnetized."

"Where does it point?"

Chen Mo laid the suanpan flat.

The magnet spun on the table several times before coming to a rest.

The tip pointed Northwest.

"Northwest." Chen Mo looked in that direction.

That was the edge of the city. An abandoned industrial zone. And...

"The Crematorium site," Su Huai said suddenly.

"What?"

"The historical site of the Song Dynasty Crematorium. It’s underneath the current Northwest Industrial Park."

Su Huai pulled up a map on her phone, overlaying an ancient map with a modern one.

"Look. The spot where Zhang San was executed... that’s exactly where the **'Hongda Chemical Plant'** stands now."

Chen Mo’s expression turned cold.

"She’s there."

"She’s waiting for us exactly where Zhang San died."
