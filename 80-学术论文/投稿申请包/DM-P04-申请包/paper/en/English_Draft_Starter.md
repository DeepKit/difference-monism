# The Hard Problem Is Not a Bridge Between Two Substances but the Formation of Internality

**Status**: English draft starter for submission package

## Abstract

This paper argues that the hard problem remains stalled not only because it is difficult, but because it is framed too strongly from the outset. Instead of asking how physical process and experience are bridged, the paper reformulates the issue as the formation of internality: what kind of structure yields states that exist only from the inside. It proposes four minimal organizational conditions and treats boundary cases as update windows rather than as conclusive proof.
