## 1. Foundation Statement

### 1.1 The Nature of P00

P00 is not an axiomatic deductive system. P00 is a minimal descriptive framework. Its goal is not to monopolize all subsequent arguments, but to provide a shared directional anchor for the entire system.

### 1.2 The Minimal Starting Point

P00 retains one minimal descriptive starting point and one first minimal hypothesis:

> **Starting point: Difference — the minimal separability that prevents complete identity from being maintained.**

Once difference is not singular and isolated, it already carries the possibility of association. Hence the complete meaning of difference is:

> **Difference = the tension of non-identity under conditions of plurality.**

The starting point is therefore:

```
Difference (including pluralized associability)
+ Non-arbitrary constraint (first minimal hypothesis)
```

This remains more economical than substance ontology (which presupposes ready-made objects), subject ontology (which presupposes a transcendental subject), and dualism (which presupposes two separate sets of starting points).

DM only claims:

> **Difference + non-arbitrary constraint is, for now, the more economical formulation that DM temporarily retains in starting-point competition, not a starting point proven to be logically the only possible one.**

Concerning the question "why the world has constraints," P00 does not pretend to offer a deeper explanation. More precisely:

> **Non-arbitrary constraint is treated in P00 first as a factual precondition of a "describable world," not as a conclusion deduced from a deeper ontology.**

Its status is fixed as:

> **Non-arbitrary constraint is the minimal metaphysical commitment of DM's foundation layer.**

This carries four restrictions:

1. **It IS an ontological commitment.** If the world were truly such that "any combination is equally feasible," then structure, repetition, regularity, and scientific objects could not stably arise.
2. **But it is NOT a second substance.** DM does not write "constraint" as a second ultimate thing alongside difference.
3. **It is NOT deduced from a deeper ontology here.** P00 only records it as a minimal bookkeeping entry.
4. **It CAN be rewritten by a thinner starting point in the future.**

### 1.2.1 Why "Difference + Non-Arbitrary Constraint" Rather Than "Relation / Process / Event / Information / Constraint"

| Candidate Starting Point | What It Smuggles In | Main Problem |
|--------------------------|---------------------|--------------|
| Difference + non-arbitrary constraint | Still requires plural conditions, associability, and a minimal constraint commitment | Not absolutely zero-presupposition |
| Relation | Multiple terms and connection methods | Plural architecture already assumed |
| Process | Time, flow, before-and-after | Temporality written back into the starting point |
| Event | Slice units, temporal discretization | Configuration units frozen in advance |
| Information | Encoding rules, distinguishable states, measurement frameworks | Already-structured differences brought in |
| Constraint | The constrained terms and feasible state space | Already-structured language brought in |

DM's advantage is not "sacred uniqueness" but:

> **It smuggles in fewer ready-made objects, ready-made temporal sequences, and ready-made state spaces.**

### 1.3 Derivation Strength Classification

P00 distinguishes four status levels:
1. **Structural necessity**: If describability is to be maintained, this structural result is almost unavoidable
2. **Minimal additional hypothesis**: The weakest structural premise retained to continue description
3. **Explanatory model**: High-compression expression connecting multiple nodes; does not claim strict deductive closure
4. **Open boundary**: Arrival conditions are given; no claim of completed closure

## 2. Starting Point: Difference

### 2.1 Difference Should Not First Be Written as Post-Hoc Comparison Between Objects

Difference is not the difference between two already-formed objects, nor the result of a subject recognizing objects. Difference is:

> **Before all objectification, the tension given by non-identity itself.**

DM's order is therefore:

```
Difference → Distinction → Association → Structure → Boundary
```

Not:

```
Object → Property → Difference
```

### 2.2 Why DM Begins with Difference

If there were complete absence of difference, there would be: nothing distinguishable, nothing associable, nothing describable.

> **DM does not claim that all possible philosophies can only begin from difference. It claims: among current candidate starting points, difference + non-arbitrary constraint is the more economical formulation with more stable main-chain coverage.**

---

## 3. Why Difference Can Form Structure

### 3.1 Difference Is Not the Completed Form of Structure

Scattered differences yield only: distinguishable but unformed. Structure requires: differences between which non-arbitrary constraint associations form.

### 3.2 Minimal Definition of Structure

> **Structure = non-arbitrary constraint associations formed between difference terms.**

"Constraint association" means: a change at one difference position systematically limits the feasible states of other difference positions.

Three minimal thresholds for structural status:
1. **Feasible state rewriting**: A change at one position must systematically rewrite feasible states at others
2. **Repeatability**: This rewriting must recur under similar conditions
3. **Having consequences**: The rewriting must produce identifiable consequences

### 3.3 From Difference to Structure

Difference alone does not yield complete structure. To move from "difference" to "structural description," at least two items must be explicitly recorded:
1. Pluralized associability
2. Non-arbitrary constraint

> **Difference gives separability, association gives connectability, constraint gives formability.**

## 4. Boundary: Structure Becoming One

### 4.1 Why Structure Must Have Boundaries

Without boundary: it cannot be distinguished from background, from others, or from its own extension. Boundary is not a later epistemological marker but the ontological condition for wholeness.

### 4.2 Definition of Boundary

> **Boundary = the membership threshold differential that a set of associations exhibits relative to its surrounding associations.**

Boundary is not an absolute wall but: decidability of membership + asymmetry of internal/external exchange conditions.

---

## 5. Configuration, Mutual Exclusion, and Orderable Transformation

### 5.1 Why Configuration Appears After Boundary

A bounded structure has: internal arrangement, role positions, coupling modes, possible states. These are collectively called the structure's **configuration**.

> **Configuration = the specific arrangement or state taken by a bounded structure in some respect.**

### 5.2 Why Mutually Exclusive Configurations Cannot Coexist

The same structure cannot, in the same respect, simultaneously and completely take two mutually exclusive configurations. This yields not yet time, but only:

> **Mutually exclusive configurations cannot coexist simultaneously.**

### 5.3 What Counts as Orderable Transformation

Once the same structure can change from configuration A to configuration B, and A and B cannot coexist, minimal sequentiality emerges:

```
A
then
B
```

## 6. Time: The Order of Trace Inheritance

### 6.1 Time Is Not an A Priori Container

DM does not treat time as a pre-existing uniform container, an external clock, or a subjective projection.

### 6.2 From Sequence to Time

What truly approaches time is:

> **The results of earlier configurations entering the constitution conditions of later configurations.**

> **Temporality = the inheritance order in which structural transformation traces enter subsequent constitution conditions.**

### 6.2.2 Partial Order Reformulation

Let S be the set of structural configurations. Define a binary relation ≺:

> **A ≺ B ≡ the set of B's constitution conditions contains non-negligible difference terms left by A.**

Properties of ≺:
1. **Irreflexivity**: A ⊀ A
2. **Asymmetry**: If A ≺ B, then B ⊀ A
3. **Transitivity**: If A ≺ B and B ≺ C, then A ≺ C

Thus ≺ is a **strict partial order**.

Temporality can be reformulated as:

> **Temporality = the strict partial order ≺ on the set S of structural configurations, where A ≺ B means B's constitution conditions contain non-negligible differences left by A.**

Irreversibility:

> **Irreversibility = the asymmetry of ≺ + the high removal cost of trace terms that have entered subsequent constitution conditions.**

### 6.2.4 Local Partial Order and Physical Time

```
Ontological layer: global partial order ≺ (implementation-independent)
Physical layer: local partial orders ≺_L (metric intervals constrained by gravity/velocity)
Clock time: cumulative measurement of local partial orders
```

> **Partial order relations are implementation-independent ontological structures; clock readings are implementation-dependent physical measurements. Relativity changes the latter, not the former.**

## 6.5 Why It Can Still Be Called "Monism"

DM's "one" does not mean the world has only one material, one object, or one level. It opposes:

```
One ontology here
Another for mind
Another for value
Another for civilization
All disconnected from each other
```

> **DM currently defends "weak monism": what is unified is the ontological starting point and structural grammar, not the claim that all level-content can be reduced to lower-level summaries.**

## 7. Irreversibility, Persistence, and Beings

### 7.1 Why Trace Inheritance Leads to Irreversibility

Sequence alone does not equal irreversibility. What truly leads to irreversibility is:

```
Traces retained
+ Traces diffused
+ Traces rewrite subsequent conditions
+ Removing these traces requires additional cost
```

### 7.3 Minimal Definition of a Being

> **Being = a bounded difference-structure capable of maintaining its association patterns across time.**

"Existence" should not first be understood as static presence but as: a structure that, across temporal unfolding, can still maintain its key associations from complete dissolution.

---

## 8. Regularity, Laws, and Axioms

### 8.1 Regularity in the World

> **Regularity = stable, repeating constraints that structural relations exhibit after repeated formation, selection, and deposition under boundary conditions.**

### 8.3 Scientific Laws and Mathematical Axioms

Three must be distinguished:
1. **Regularity**: stable constraints in the world
2. **Laws**: science's model-compressions of stable constraints
3. **Axioms**: minimal starting points within formal systems

> **The world is not made of formulas; formulas are how stable relations in the world are abstracted, compressed, and organized by subjects.**

---

## 9. Causality: The Transmissibility of Constraints

> **Causality = certain structural differences, through transmissible constraint chains, stably alter the constitution conditions of subsequent structures.**

Causality's core is not "mysterious production" but: constraint transmission, condition rewriting, stable recurrence.

> **Regularity tells us what tends to be the case; causality tells us what, through what chains, altered what.**

---

## 10. Levels and Emergence

### 10.1 Why Levels Appear

When lower-level structures form more stable, denser, more repeatable couplings, a new whole structure may appear with: new boundaries, new internal differentiation, new constitution conditions, and downward constraint on lower-level parts.

### 10.2 Minimal Criteria for Emergence

> **Emergence = when several lower-level structures couple and form new whole boundaries, whole conditions, and downward constraints.**

Emergence requires at least four things:
1. Lower-level structures can couple
2. A whole boundary appears
3. Whole conditions appear
4. Downward constraint appears

> **The upper level depends on the lower but cannot be fully replaced by lower-level descriptions; this is the true status of emergence.**
