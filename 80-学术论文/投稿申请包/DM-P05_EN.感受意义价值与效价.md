# From Structural Preconditions to Lived Experience: Feeling, Meaning, Value, and Valence

## DM.P05 · From Biological Realization to Qualia, Valence, Meaning, and Value

> **Author**: Yi Fu (付毅, ODDFounder, fuyi.it@live.cn). ORCID: 0009-0008-1251-2632

> **P05 no longer treats qualia as direct deductions from pure structural preconditions, but understands them as short-path metastable states formed by living subjects within their phylogenetically constrained neural-somatic wholes.**

---

P04 identified the structural position where experience becomes possible: boundary → internality → self-reference → internal differentiability → "the position where experience can occur."

P05 asks: **Why do these structural preconditions manifest in living organisms as specific feelings — pain, pleasure, fear, desire — rather than remaining abstract differentiable states?**

---

## 1. The Biological Turn

Pure structure can tell us where experience can occur. It cannot tell us why pain hurts or why sugar tastes sweet. For that, we need the biological layer: metabolic boundaries, homeostatic imperatives, phylogenetic constraints, and neural topology.

## 2. Valence: The Body's Shorthand for Survival

Valence (positive/negative, approach/avoid) is not a philosophical add-on. It is the body's compressed signal about survival relevance. Pain signals "this threatens boundary integrity." Pleasure signals "this supports boundary maintenance." These are not cultural constructs layered on top of biology — they are biology's most fundamental information channel.

## 3. Meaning as Compressed History

Meaning is not a mysterious property injected into the world by consciousness. It is the compressed record of what has historically promoted or threatened the organism's persistence conditions. A smell "means" danger because organisms with similar boundaries have been damaged by what lies behind that smell.

## 4. Value as Generalized Valence

Value extends valence from individual survival to social and cultural persistence. What a community values is what has historically supported its continued existence and flourishing. Value is not arbitrary preference — it is generalized, socialized valence.

## 5. The Gap That Remains

P05 does not close the explanatory gap. It explains why certain experiences carry the valence they do (survival relevance). It does not explain why there is "something it is like" at all. That question — the hard problem's irreducible core — remains open. P05's contribution is narrowing the gap: what remains unexplained is smaller and more precisely located than before.

> **P04 identified where experience can happen. P05 explains why experience comes with feeling: because organisms that didn't care about their own boundary conditions didn't persist long enough to have descendants.**
