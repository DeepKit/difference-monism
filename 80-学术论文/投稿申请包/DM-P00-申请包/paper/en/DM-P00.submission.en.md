# Why the Starting Point Should Be Written as "Difference + Non-Arbitrary Constraint"
*Minimal Comparison and Failure Conditions in Starting-Point Competition*

**Draft status**: English full draft for submission package

**Abstract**: This article no longer treats difference as the uniquely possible ultimate starting point. It defends a narrower claim: among the common candidates of relation, process, event, information, and constraint, the combination of difference plus non-arbitrary constraint is, at present, the more economical starting point because it imports fewer ready-made objects, temporal slices, and coding frameworks. The aim is not to derive the whole world from an absolutely presuppositionless beginning, but to make explicit what each candidate already smuggles in, and to explain why Difference Monism would rather state non-arbitrary constraint openly as a minimal metaphysical commitment than silently write objects, time, event units, or informational encoding back into the ground level. The article also proposes a minimal criterion of adjudication: any rival starting point that seeks to defeat difference plus non-arbitrary constraint must be not only thinner, but also able to cover the chain of structure, boundary, temporality, and layering without importing heavier assumptions. A corresponding failure line is retained: if a thinner rival language eventually achieves equivalent coverage, Difference Monism should revise its foundational statement rather than sacralize difference.

**Keywords**: starting-point competition; difference; non-arbitrary constraint; structure; temporality; ontological foundation

## 0.5. Contributions

The current contribution of this paper can be compressed into three points:

1. It rewrites starting-point competition from a rhetorical contest over "which term is more fundamental" into a problem of presupposition accounting.
2. It treats `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` not as a sacred first word, but as a comparatively economical formulation that can be challenged, replaced, or revised.
3. It makes the criterion of comparison and the failure conditions explicit, so that the debate no longer remains at the level of slogan.

## 1. Introduction

One of the most common errors in metaphysical writing is to treat one's own starting point as naturally the thinnest possible one without seriously checking what has already been smuggled in. Difference Monism has faced this risk as well. If "difference" is written as an absolute zero-presupposition beginning from which structure, boundary, time, regularity, and emergence are all supposed to follow, then plural conditions, relational possibilities, and constraint conditions are easily smuggled into the same word. Yet if one tries to avoid this risk by switching immediately to "relation," "process," "event," "information," or "constraint" as the starting point, one often ends up writing objectification, temporal slicing, or coding frameworks back into the ground level too early.

The issue handled here is therefore not which word sounds most elegant, but a narrower and harder question: in starting-point competition, which formulation imports less while still covering a longer downstream chain. This article argues that `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` still deserves to be retained, not because it has been proven to be the uniquely ultimate starting point, but because it currently imports fewer ready-made objects, ready-made time units, and ready-made coding schemes while still entering the chain of `structure / boundary / temporality / layering` with relative stability.

This means that the paper no longer aims to win the starting-point dispute unconditionally. It defends a weaker but more honest claim: `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` is currently a more economical starting-point combination, not a sacralized and unchallengeable first term. If a thinner rival formulation eventually covers the same chain, Difference Monism should revise its own foundational statement.

## 2. Literature Position

This paper does not present itself as a total survey of metaphysical traditions. It enters into dialogue only with the most relevant lines. First, it shares with structural realism the anti-substantialist intuition that relations matter more than independently given entities, yet it differs from structural realism insofar as it is more concerned with how structure enters the generation of boundary, temporality, and layering.<sup>1</sup><sup>2</sup> Second, it shares with process philosophy a resistance to static substance ontology, but refuses to treat "process" itself as a term that requires no further accounting, because doing so writes temporal flow and directional succession back into the starting point too early.<sup>3</sup> Third, it shares with informational ontologies a sensitivity to distinguishable states and encoding, but rejects the move of treating coding rules, carriers, and measurement frameworks as foundational givens.<sup>4</sup> Fourth, it shares with broader naturalistic metaphysics the refusal of an extra mysterious entity, while also resisting the move of simply writing "the physical" or any other already objectified layer into the ground floor.<sup>5</sup>

The point of entry is therefore not to prove that all rival starting points are impossible, but to compress the issue into a minimal adjudicable form: which formulations are thinner, and which formulations are powerful only because they have already loaded the structures that they later claim to derive.

## 3. Core Claim: Starting-Point Competition Is Presupposition Accounting

The core claim of this article can be compressed into two statements. First, starting-point competition should no longer be written as a contest over which term sounds more primordial, but as a contest over which formulation imports less while covering more. Second, once one asks how object, relation, boundary, temporality, and layering come to be, `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` remains, at present, the more economical combination.

Both statements deliberately weaken the ambition of the paper. The article no longer claims that "pure difference" by itself generates everything, nor does it pretend that non-arbitrary constraint has already been fully derived from a deeper base. More precisely, the ground level of Difference Monism is admitted to contain at least two explicitly recorded items: minimal distinguishability and a minimal commitment that not all combinations are equally viable.

Once written this way, starting-point competition is no longer a rhetorical fight over philosophical elevation, but a cleaner comparative task. If a rival approach claims to be thinner than Difference Monism, it must show whether it has already imported objects, time order, event units, coding rules, state spaces, or other thicker structures. If it claims to cover more, it must also show how it can do so without borrowing heavier assumptions than the ones it criticizes.

## 4. Table 1: Minimal Comparison of Starting Points

| Candidate starting point | What it tends to smuggle in | Current advantage | Current weakness |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` | still requires explicit accounting of plural conditions, relational possibility, and minimal constraint | imports fewer ready-made objects, temporal slices, and coding frameworks | not a zero-presupposition formula; must admit that the source of constraint is not closed |
| `relation` | multiple terms, modes of linkage, relational positions | enters structural language directly | already presupposes plurality and connectivity |
| `process` | time, flow, direction of succession | expresses becoming and continuity well | writes temporality back into the start too early |
| `event` | slicing units, discretization rules, temporal segmentation | fits occurrence and history narratives | freezes units of occurrence in advance |
| `information` | coding rules, distinguishable states, carriers, metrics | readily enters systems and cognition discourse | presupposes organized difference and readability conditions |
| `constraint` | constrained items, state spaces, viability domains | readily enters regularity and stability discourse | risks writing a later structural layer into the foundation |

The function of this table is not to announce that Difference Monism has already won the contest, but to lay out the presuppositional costs of each candidate. Only after this step can the claim that one option is "thinner" cease to be a mere rhetorical self-praise.

More concretely, `relation` is thicker than Difference Monism not because it is false, but because it quickly presupposes that there are already multiple terms and describable ways of linking them. `Process` is problematic not because it cannot describe the world, but because it writes flow, succession, and directed unfolding back into the ground level. `Event` becomes difficult because it usually requires prior criteria of segmentation before it can say what has happened. `Information` is attractive for later theory because it enters coding, transmission, and cognition quickly, yet as a foundational term it often presupposes distinguishable states, carriers, and a metric frame. `Constraint` comes closest to the second half of the Difference Monist starting point, yet even here the risk is to presuppose the constrained items and the state space too early.

For this reason, the present paper retains `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` not because it sounds stronger, but because it postpones the introduction of objects, time, event units, and coding frameworks by one more step.

## 5. Why Difference Monism Retains "Difference + Non-Arbitrary Constraint"

Difference Monism retains this combination not because it is more grandiose than its rivals, but because its bookkeeping order is more stable. First, `difference` provides minimal distinguishability and blocks the possibility that complete identity can hold the ground level together. Second, once difference is no longer a solitary singularity, pluralized relational possibilities must be admitted. Third, if those relations are to sediment into structure, recurrence, and regularity, one must admit that not every linkage is equally viable, that is, one must admit a minimal `non-arbitrary constraint`.

The advantage of this order is that it keeps thicker terms later. The paper does not need to assume ready-made objects, ready-made temporal order, ready-made event slicing, or ready-made informational coding at the beginning. It only needs to assume that the world exhibits minimal distinguishability and that not every combination is equally workable. Precisely because of this postponement strategy, `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` currently imports less than `relation`, `process`, `event`, and `information`.

But this retention has costs. The paper must admit three points openly: first, `difference` alone is insufficient to yield structure; second, `non-arbitrary constraint` is currently a minimal metaphysical commitment rather than a fully closed derivation; third, Difference Monism does not yet answer the deeper question of why there is constraint at all. If these costs are not written explicitly, then `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` will simply slide back into a sacralized first word.

## 6. Minimum Coverage Requirements

Starting-point competition cannot be decided merely by asking which term sounds thinner. It also has to be judged by which formulation covers a longer downstream chain. For the present paper, the minimum chain is not the whole of Difference Monism, but only four load-bearing positions: `structure / boundary / temporality / layering`.

This chain can be compressed into four middle-level propositions:

1. `Proposition 1`
Only when minimal distinguishability enters plural conditions, describable linkage, and non-arbitrary restriction can difference legitimately be written as structure.

2. `Proposition 2`
Only when a cluster of structural relations produces stable threshold differences for membership and inside/outside transitions can it be written as boundary.

3. `Proposition 3`
Only when traces of structural change enter later conditions of persistence can temporality be written as inherited sequence rather than as an a priori container.

4. `Proposition 4`
Only when the coupling of bounded structures yields new whole-level conditions, new whole-level boundaries, and downward constraint can layering be written as a real site of generation.

These propositions do not claim that formalization is complete. Their task is to prevent starting-point competition from remaining at the level of slogan. Any rival that claims to be thinner must show how it reaches these four steps without becoming thicker in the process.

### 6.1 Structure

Any rival starting point that seeks to replace Difference Monism must first explain how it reaches repeatable structure rather than treating structure as a ready-made background. For the present paper, the advantage of `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` is that it first provides distinguishability and then explains why some relations between differences repeatedly sediment into stable structure. If a rival formulation must directly presuppose a structural network, coding rule, or state space, it is already thicker than the formulation it seeks to defeat.

### 6.2 Boundary

If a starting point cannot enter the problem of boundary, it will struggle to explain what it means for something to count as one. The minimum claim here is that a boundary is not an added outline, but the result of a relational cluster that yields stable threshold differences concerning membership, integrity, and the transition between inside and outside.<sup>6</sup> A candidate starting point must therefore explain not only relation, but also the emergence of membership conditions. Relational language that already assumes relata, or event language that already assumes units of slicing, becomes thicker at exactly this point.

### 6.3 Temporality

The paper refuses to elevate `process` directly into the starting term precisely because that move writes temporal flow and succession back into the ground level. The more cautious statement offered here is that temporality should be written as the inherited sequence of structural traces entering later conditions of persistence.<sup>7</sup> This is still an explanatory model rather than an exhaustive definition of time, but it postpones temporality until after structure and trace rather than treating flow as a primitive.

### 6.4 Layering

Finally, any rival starting point that seeks genuine victory must explain how layering appears. The minimum requirement is not the derivation of life, consciousness, or normativity all at once, but only this: when relations among differences sediment into new whole-level boundaries, whole-level conditions, and downward constraints, layering acquires a legitimate structural status.<sup>8</sup> If a rival formulation must already assume stable object layers, function layers, or coding layers before it can enter layering at all, it has not remained thinner than Difference Monism.

The minimal adjudication standard is therefore not "who is most abstract" but "who can cover `structure / boundary / temporality / layering` with less smuggling."

## 7. Minimum Distinction from Rival Approaches

If the foregoing comparison remains too abstract, the paper must further state how it differs from several familiar rival lines.

Against `relation-first` approaches, the disagreement is not with relation itself, but with the move of writing relational terms and positions into the ground floor too early. Against process philosophy, the disagreement is not with becoming, but with the elevation of temporal flow and succession into an unexamined primitive. Against event ontologies, the disagreement is not with occurrence, but with the freezing of segmentation units in advance. Against information-first approaches, the disagreement is not with coding and transmission, but with the foundationalization of coding rules, carriers, and metrics. Against constraint-first approaches, the disagreement is not with regularity and stability, but with the risk of importing the constrained items and the space of viable states at the outset.

All of these distinctions converge on one claim that remains vulnerable to defeat: `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` deserves to be retained only because it currently imports less than these rivals while still reaching the same downstream burden points.

## 8. Table 2: Update Rules for Starting-Point Competition

| Pressure point | What may still be said if the pressure supports this paper | What this paper must do if stable contrary results appear |
| --- | --- | --- |
| no thinner rival starting point has yet appeared | the paper may continue to treat `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` as the currently more economical starting point | if a thinner rival language emerges and covers the same chain, the foundational statement must be rewritten |
| rival starting points still clearly smuggle in object, time, or coding assumptions | the paper may continue to emphasize Difference Monism's lower import cost | if comparison shows that Difference Monism imports the same assumptions to the same degree, the claim of economy must be reduced |
| the chain of `structure / boundary / temporality / layering` is still more smoothly covered by Difference Monism | the paper may continue to present Difference Monism as the currently broader coverage formulation | if the chain can only be sustained by repeated borrowing of thicker terms, the coverage claim must be restricted |
| `non-arbitrary constraint` can still be retained as a minimal bookkeeping item | the paper may continue to write it as a minimal metaphysical commitment | if it proves to be only a vague alias for a thicker presupposition, the starting-point combination must be rewritten |

The function of Table 2 is not to disguise philosophy as experiment. It is to force the paper to state an explicit update discipline.

More strictly, the defeat of the Difference Monist starting point by a rival language should require at least four conditions at once: the rival must be thinner; it must cover `structure / boundary / temporality / layering` without heavier assumptions; it must be able to replace the current starting sentence of Difference Monism in the mother text without producing new equivocations; and it must carry the load-bearing points that Difference Monism currently carries through `difference + non-arbitrary constraint`. If only one or two of these conditions are met, the more cautious statement is that the rival has generated pressure rather than definitive defeat.

## 9. Failure Conditions

To avoid turning `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` into an untouchable moat, the paper compresses its failure conditions into three lines.

First, `defeat by a rival starting point`. If a formulation thinner than `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` appears and still covers the chain of `structure / boundary / temporality / layering`, Difference Monism should revise its own ground statement.

Second, `failure of constraint bookkeeping`. If `non-arbitrary constraint` turns out to be only a vague alias for a much thicker presupposition, or if it can only be made intelligible by repeatedly borrowing much later-layer vocabulary, then its status as a minimal metaphysical commitment loses force.

Third, `failure of chain coverage`. If Difference Monism repeatedly and covertly borrows objects, temporal slices, event units, or coding frameworks in its core derivational steps, and if this smuggling is no lighter than that of its rivals, then the phrase "more economical starting point" must be significantly weakened.

The core sentence of the paper should therefore no longer be "difference has won the starting-point contest." It should instead be written more precisely: `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` currently retains reasons for preservation, but it must continue to submit itself to competition, comparison, and possible revision.

## 10. Scope Limits

This paper addresses only the problem of starting-point competition as framed in `P00`. It does not directly derive a complete physics, biology, consciousness theory, normative theory, or civilizational theory. It compares rival starting points only with respect to the first four burden-bearing positions of the larger chain. It establishes only a minimal comparison table and failure conditions, not a final settlement of the starting-point problem.

Its success condition is therefore modest. It needs to do three things only: rewrite starting-point competition as presupposition accounting, state the advantages and costs of `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` together, and keep the defeat conditions explicit inside the text rather than sacralizing the present formulation.

## 11. Conclusion

What this paper defends is not the claim that difference is the only legitimate starting point. It defends a narrower and more stable judgment: compared with `relation / process / event / information / constraint`, the combination of `difference + non-arbitrary constraint` is, at present, the more economical and less assumption-heavy starting point. Its advantage is not that it is more abstract in a rhetorical sense, but that it introduces objects, time, event units, and coding frameworks later. Its cost is that it must openly admit that it is not a zero-presupposition formula and that `non-arbitrary constraint` remains a minimal metaphysical commitment.

The real contribution of the article is therefore not a final verdict, but a clearer discipline of comparison: any candidate starting point that seeks to defeat Difference Monism must satisfy two demands at once. It must be thinner than `difference + non-arbitrary constraint`, and it must cover the same burden-bearing chain without importing heavier presuppositions. So long as those two demands are not jointly met, Difference Monism retains grounds for keeping its present starting-point formulation. Once they are met, however, it should revise that formulation rather than continue to treat "difference" as a sacred first word.
