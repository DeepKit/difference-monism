# Why the Starting Point Should Be Written as Difference Plus Non-Arbitrary Constraint

**Status**: English draft starter for submission package

**Subtitle**: Minimal Comparison and Failure Conditions in Starting-Point Competition

## Abstract

This paper argues that, among common candidate starting points such as relation, process, event, information, and constraint, the combination of difference plus non-arbitrary constraint remains the more economical formulation at present. Rather than treating this as a sacred first word, the paper reconstructs starting-point competition as a problem of presupposition accounting: which formulation imports fewer ready-made assumptions while still covering the chain of structure, boundary, temporality, and layering. The paper also states explicit failure conditions under which Difference Monism should revise its own foundational formulation.

## Keywords

starting-point competition; difference; non-arbitrary constraint; structure; temporality; ontological foundation
