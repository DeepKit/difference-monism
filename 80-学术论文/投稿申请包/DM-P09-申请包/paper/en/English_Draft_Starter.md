# Crisis Compression Must Remain Reversible

**Status**: English draft starter for submission package

## Abstract

This paper argues that crisis compression must remain reversible if it is to preserve political legitimacy. Rather than asking which regime is superior in the abstract, it asks a narrower procedural question: what minimum conditions distinguish reversible emergency compression from structures that consume the order they claim to protect. The paper relies on a small-sample, strongly constrained, low-extrapolation comparison and limits its claims accordingly.

## Keywords

crisis governance; reversibility; emergency power; institutional procedure; political legitimacy
