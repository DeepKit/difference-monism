## 1. Task

P11 does not pretend DM is complete. It identifies: (a) the most dangerous failure paths, (b) the strongest existing and anticipated criticisms, (c) what would force major revisions, and (d) what remains genuinely open.

---

## 2. The Six Principal Failure Paths

1. **Thinner starting point emerges**: If a language thinner than "difference + non-arbitrary constraint" can cover the same main chain, DM's claim of maximal economy collapses.

2. **Physics interface remains chronically misaligned**: If DM's ontological categories cannot form stable interfaces with established physical theory, its claim to be "compatible with science" weakens to "not yet contradicted by science."

3. **Consciousness flagship paper cannot enter external debate**: If P04's reframing of the hard problem cannot generate engagement from philosophers of mind, DM's most distinctive claim remains a monologue.

4. **Normative-institutional bridge lacks case evidence**: If the DM→TAT→ODD chain cannot be supported by systematic case comparisons, the normative layer remains a conceptual exercise.

5. **System increasingly resembles a universal explainer**: If every possible outcome can be post-hoc reframed in DM terms but no outcome could have contradicted it, DM has become unfalsifiable.

6. **Single-author accumulation without external challenge**: If DM remains a single-author system without cross-author critique, its claims cannot be distinguished from a well-organized personal worldview.

---

## 3. Strongest Existing Criticisms

**The "Elastic Concept" Criticism**: DM's core terms (difference, structure, boundary) are flexible enough to be retrofitted to any phenomenon. Is DM doing explanation or redescription?

**Response**: DM distinguishes four derivation strengths (structural necessity, minimal hypothesis, explanatory model, open boundary) precisely to prevent elastic usage. A claim marked "explanatory model" is not allowed to function as "structural necessity" in subsequent arguments.

**The "Nothing New" Criticism**: Each individual claim in DM can be found in existing traditions. What is genuinely new?

**Response**: DM's novelty is combinatorial — the chain itself. Many traditions have observed that "structure matters," "time is not a container," or "consciousness is not a second substance." DM's claim is that these observations can be derived from a single starting point and organized into a continuous chain.

---

## 4. Historical Note on RM/DM Transition

DM's philosophical predecessor — Resonance Monism (RM, 共扰一元论) — developed an ambitious mathematical mapping (G-F-H: Graph-Field-Hilbert state) from difference structures to standard physics. This mapping was exploratory and remains an open interface. Its success or failure does not determine the fate of DM's core conceptual chain (P00-P10), which stands on minimal operationalized starting points, main-chain consistency, and conceptual economy — not on the G-F-H bridge.

---

## 5. What Would Force Major Revision

- A thinner starting point with equivalent coverage
- Systematic case evidence against the correction-capability hypothesis (P09)
- A complete third-person translation scheme defeating P04's negative boundary claim
- Cross-cultural evidence that the 9+1 civilizational factors show no structural pattern
