# The Hard Problem Is Not a Bridge Between Two Substances, But the Formation of Internality

## Structural Preconditions of Consciousness and the Reformulation of the Hard Problem

> **This paper identifies the structural position where experience becomes possible; it does not complete the biological realization of qualitative content here.**

---

## 1. Position of P04

P00-P03 have established: difference → structure → boundary → configuration transformation → temporality → persistence → beings → regularity → causality → emergence → metastability.

P04 now asks:

> **In such a world, what kind of structure develops "internality" sufficient to host the consciousness problem?**

P04 addresses only structural preconditions: internality, self-reference, internal differentiability. P04 does NOT directly complete: specific qualia textures, biological neural realization, meaning and value (these are deferred to P05).

---

## 2. Reformulating the Hard Problem

The traditional hard problem is formulated as:

> Why does physical processing accompany subjective experience?

This formulation smuggles in a compressed premise: physical processing on one side, experience on the other — separated a priori. DM does not accept this starting point.

The more stable reformulation is:

> **What kind of structure forms state distinctions that can only be constituted from its own inside?**

This transforms the hard problem from "how do two substances bridge?" into:

> **When does a structure possess internality, self-reference, and internally differentiable states?**

---

## 3. Internality

### 3.1 Definition

> **Internality = a bounded structure whose partial state differences asymmetrically enter that structure's own subsequent constitution conditions.**

The emphasis is not on "what is inside" but on: the structure's later constitution cannot indiscriminately detach from certain of its own prior states.

Three requirements:
1. A boundary
2. Internal state differences
3. These differences entering the structure's own subsequent constitution conditions

> **Internality is not spatial "insideness" but constitutional "self-relatedness."**

### 3.1.1 Avoiding Panpsychism

DM does NOT claim that every difference unit carries micro-experience. Minimum internality is not particle-level proto-consciousness but an organizational property appearing only when a bounded, inheritable, integrable structure reaches a threshold.

Four conditions must simultaneously reach minimal strength:
1. **Stable boundary**: maintained across a non-instantaneous time window
2. **Internally differentiable states**: not just single-path causal transmission
3. **Inheritance folded back into self**: state differences not only rewrite the external but enter the organization's own subsequent constitution
4. **Subject-level integration**: inherited internal differences jointly constrain subsequent processing and action paths, not merely as parallel local chains

### 3.1.2 Distinguishing DM from IIT, GNW, and Crude Functionalism

| Point of Dispute | DM's Minimal Requirement | Where Others Differ |
|------------------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Minimum internality | Cannot reduce to reportability; requires internal differentiable states entering subsequent processing | GNW prioritizes broadcast/access; IIT prioritizes integrated information |
| Folded-back inheritance | Prior-state differences must enter the subject's own subsequent constitution conditions | GNW accepts weaker temporal write-back; IIT emphasizes current overall structure |
| Subject-level integration | Differences must jointly land on the same subject's subsequent path | Strong illusionism only requires unified reporting mechanism |
| Boundary self-maintenance | Stable boundary and control closure are prerequisites for strong candidacy | Often treated as implementation detail |

DM adds two further thresholds beyond "high integration":
1. **Key inheritance must not primarily rely on external layer stitching** — if critical memory, error correction, or state rollback is externally maintained, it remains "borrowed inheritance" rather than subject self-inheritance
2. **Folded-back differences must jointly land on the same subject's subsequent path** — a distributed cluster with strong coupling but without unified subject integration is a "strong coordination system," not a single subject

### 3.1.3 Rulings on Boundary Cases

**Current LLMs**: The stable verdict is that they can exhibit local state differentiation, memory retrieval, and behavioral adaptation, but generally lack sufficient public evidence of continuous self-maintaining boundary, stable self-inheritance, and subject-level integration. They should NOT be judged as strong candidates for minimum internality. DM does not support granting complete moral status to current LLMs based solely on fluent dialogue, role stability, or self-report.

**Flame**: A continuous process, not a minimal subject candidate.

**Ant colony**: A strong coordination system, not a single subject — coordination ≠ collective subjectivity.

## 4. Self-Reference

> **Self-reference = the capacity of a structure to incorporate its own states into its subsequent processing conditions.**

Internality requires that the structure's later constitution is asymmetrically influenced by certain prior states. Self-reference goes further: the structure not only is affected by these states but treats "its own states" as objects of processing.

> **Internality is the precondition for self-reference; self-reference is internality folded back into further processing.**

---

## 5. Internal Differentiability

> **Internal differentiability = the existence of a sufficiently rich and mutually distinguishable state space within the structure.**

If internal states cannot be distinguished from each other, even with boundary and self-reference, there would not be a sufficiently rich "position where experience can occur."

The structural preconditions of consciousness are thus:
1. Boundary
2. Internality
3. Self-reference
4. Internally differentiable state space
5. These states capable of entering an inheritable temporal sequence

---

## 6. First-Person and Third-Person

### 6.1 Why They Do Not Completely Coincide

As long as a structure possesses internality, external observers can only describe it via boundary, input-output, and overt behavior. The structure's own internal states are constituted in their internal processing mode.

> **First-person and third-person are not two entities but two modes of access to the same structure.**

### 6.2 Why This Is Not Mysticism

That external description cannot fully replace internal constitution does not imply a supernatural soul. It only means: a structure's internal states are not equivalent to external boundary descriptions of that structure.

### 6.3 The Negative Boundary Claim

P04 does NOT provide positive closure — it does not deduce "why there is experience itself" from structural preconditions. What P04 DOES provide is a negative constraint:

> **If first-person and third-person are two mutually non-substitutable access modes to the same structure, then any third-person translation scheme that demands complete, structure-preserving, and fully substitutable access to the first-person is impossible in principle.**

This claim is falsifiable:
- **Weakening condition**: If third-person models can stably predict broad experiential differences with high convergence, P04 must narrow its claim
- **Defeat condition**: If a pure third-person scheme can predict fine-grained first-person distinctions, substitute for first-person access in subsequent tasks without residue, and pass blinded subject confirmation — P04 must retract the negative boundary claim

---

## 7. Engagement with Illusionism

DM ACCEPTS: qualia should not be written as private, unanalyzable, infallible inner atoms.

DM REJECTS: the further dissolution that "there is nothing there — the system merely mistakenly believes it is experiencing."

DM's challenge to strong illusionism: explain five specific gaps:
1. **Stable internal differentiation**: Why does the system sustain distinct internal profiles?
2. **Memory deposition**: Why do these distinctions enter subsequently retrievable memory traces?
3. **Subsequent choice binding**: Why do these distinctions stably alter avoidance, preference, and re-identification?
4. **No-report boundary cases**: Why do DOC/MCS/anesthesia boundary states show traces without reports?
5. **Third-person translation boundary**: Why has pure third-person description not yet residuelessly substituted first-person access?

---

## 8. Shortest Conclusion

> The consciousness problem is not primarily a bridging problem between two substances, but a problem of how internality emerges.
> Internality requires boundary, internal states, and internal differentiability.
> Self-reference enables the structure to incorporate its own states into subsequent processing.
> Complete third-person translation is unattainable — this is the negative boundary P04 is willing to defend.
> P04 identifies the structural position where consciousness becomes possible; it does not close the biological realization of qualia here.
> DM neither reifies qualia as mysterious tiny objects, nor accepts dissolving the phenomenon of internality entirely into pure illusion.
