# 1 Difference Field

Let

$$\Phi(x,t)$$

represent the magnitude of local differences within a dynamic environment.

$$\Phi \geq 0$$

---

# 2 Dynamic Equation

The evolution of Φ can be described by

$$\frac{\partial\Phi}{\partial t} = \alpha\Phi - \beta\Phi^3 + D\nabla^2\Phi$$

where

| Parameter | Meaning |
| --------- | ------- |
| α | amplification coefficient |
| β | nonlinear saturation |
| D | spatial diffusion |

---

# 3 Amplification

The term

$$\alpha\Phi$$

represents positive feedback that increases differences.

This captures the essential mechanism of **self-reinforcement**.

---

# 4 Saturation

The term

$$-\beta\Phi^3$$

limits unbounded growth.

This ensures stability through nonlinear damping.

---

# 5 Diffusion

The diffusion term

$$D\nabla^2\Phi$$

spreads differences spatially.

This enables **pattern formation** and structure coupling.

---

# 6 Resonance Number

Define

$$R_{RM} = \frac{\alpha}{\beta}$$

Interpretation:

| Condition | Behavior |
| --------- | -------- |
| R < 1 | decay |
| R ≈ 1 | critical state |
| R > 1 | structure formation |

# 9 Critical Behavior

Near the critical point R ≈ 1:

$$\Phi_{eq} \sim (R - 1)^{1/2}$$

This gives the critical exponent:

$$\beta_{crit} = \frac{1}{2}$$
