# 06.11 Utility-First Software Engineering: A Paradigm Shift from Process Management to Artifact Definition

> **Authors**: Yi Fu ( ODDFounder  fuyi.it@live.cn )
> **Date**: January 12, 2026
> **Keywords**: Software Engineering, ODD, Paradigm Shift, Utility Theory, AI-Assisted Development

---

## Abstract

Traditional software engineering (SE) has evolved primarily as a discipline of **process management**—optimizing how humans collaborate to write code. However, the advent of generative AI fundamentally alters the scarcity equation: code generation is now abundant and cheap, while precise intent definition remains scarce. This paper proposes **Utility-First Software Engineering**, formalized as the **Output-Driven Development (ODD)** methodology. We argue that SE must shift its focus from managing the *process* of coding to managing the *definition* of artifacts. By treating code as an intermediate liability rather than an asset, ODD aligns software production with the fundamental economic principle of utility maximization.

---

## 1. Introduction: The Abstraction Gap

Human interaction with the physical world is mediated by tools. The history of technology is the history of increasing abstraction layers to bridge the gap between **Human Intent** and **Physical Reality**.

*   **Physical Layer**: Moving atoms (e.g., digging a hole).
*   **Digital Layer**: Moving bits (e.g., writing software to control a digger).

However, a gap remains: the **Abstraction Gap** between "what we want" (Utility) and "how we get it" (Implementation). Traditional SE bridges this gap by training humans to translate intent into code manually. This process is slow, error-prone, and lossy.

---

## 2. Literature Review: The Era of Process Management

Since the "Software Crisis" of the 1960s, SE research has focused on:
1.  **Methodologies**: Waterfall, Agile, Scrum, DevOps.
2.  **Collaboration**: Git, Jira, CI/CD.
3.  **Quality**: TDD, BDD, Code Review.

All these innovations share a common premise: **Human cognitive bandwidth is the bottleneck.** Therefore, we must manage the *process* to coordinate limited human resources efficiently.

---

## 3. The Paradigm Shift: Code as a Liability

Generative AI (LLMs) invalidates the "human bottleneck" premise.
*   **Bandwidth**: AI can generate code at 100x human speed.
*   **Concurrency**: AI can execute infinite tasks in parallel.

In this new context, the "Process-Centric" view becomes obsolete. We propose an **"Artifact-Centric"** view:
*   **Code is not an asset**: It is a liability that incurs maintenance costs.
*   **Utility is the asset**: The functional output (the running software) is the only thing that matters.

---

## 4. Methodology: The ODD Framework

Output-Driven Development (ODD) implements Utility-First SE through three mechanisms:

### 4.1 Definition over Implementation
The engineer's role shifts from "Implementer" to "Definer". The primary artifact is no longer source code, but the **Contract** (Input/Output/Constraints).

### 4.2 Automated Verification as the Truth
Since the implementation (process) is delegated to a stochastic AI, trust is established solely through **Verification**. If the output meets the Contract's acceptance criteria, the internal logic is irrelevant.

### 4.3 The "Black Box" Factory
The software production line becomes a black box.
$$Intent \rightarrow [Contract] \rightarrow [AI \ Factory] \rightarrow [Utility]$$
The factory handles the "How"; the human handles the "What".

---

## 5. Discussion: From Managing Humans to Managing Definitions

The implications of this shift are profound:
1.  **Skill Transformation**: Engineers need less syntax knowledge and more system design capability.
2.  **Tooling Evolution**: IDEs will evolve into "Factory Consoles" (like Progee) rather than text editors.
3.  **Economic Efficiency**: The marginal cost of software production approaches zero, limited only by the cost of energy (tokens).

---

## 6. Conclusion

Utility-First Software Engineering represents the maturation of our field. Just as civil engineering moved from "how to stack stones" to "how to design bridges," software engineering is moving from "how to write loops" to "how to define utility." ODD is the framework for this new era.
