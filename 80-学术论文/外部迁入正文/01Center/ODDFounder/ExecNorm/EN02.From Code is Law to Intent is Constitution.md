## Introduction: The Decade-Long Confusion After The DAO

> **Core Philosophy**: *Normative Medium Mismatch and Civilizational Misalignment*

The 2016 DAO incident became a watershed moment in blockchain history. When attackers utilized a code vulnerability to "legally" drain  million worth of Ether, the community faced a suffocating philosophical choice: uphold "Code is Law" and watch the hacker walk away with the loot? Or invoke social consensus for a hard fork, admitting that code is not supreme?

Ethereum chose the latter. While this choice recovered the assets, it left a permanent fracture in the foundation of Web3. Ten years later, DeFi governance still struggles within this fracture:

* **The Far Right**: Adheres to strict code immutability (Fundamentalist Immutability), turning Web3 into a "dark forest" where **unchecked exploitability** reigns. As long as the code runs, robbery is considered legal arbitrage.
* **The Far Left**: Fearing code exploits, hands control to Multi-sig wallets (Technocratic Oligarchy), which essentially **reintroduces centralized control**, reducing "decentralization" to mere theater.

We must admit: **Code alone is insufficient to bear the weight of complex human governance.**

This paper explicitly inherits the philosophy of the *Executable Normativism Manifesto* and is deeply inspired by the engineering practice of the **ODD (Output-Driven Development) platform**. Our experience with "output definition as control" in ODD directly catalyzed the philosophical core of Executable Normativism. We interpret the flaw of "Code is Law" as a **Normative Medium Mismatch**: the technological medium has leaped to smart contracts, while the normative form remains lagging, leading to systemic loss of control. "Intent is Constitution" is the technological implementation of this philosophy in Web3.

**Contributions**:
1. Proposing "Executable Normativism" as a mediating governance paradigm between technical systems and political institutions.
2. Explaining Web3 governance failures as a structural "Normative Medium Mismatch" rather than mere technical bugs or moral failures.
3. Introducing a layered governance model of "Intent is Constitution".
4. Demonstrating the feasibility of formal norms participating in governance while preserving human sovereignty.

## Chapter 1: The Misalignment of "Governance Medium"

> **Core Philosophy**: *The Fracture between Calculation Medium and Normative Medium*

### 1. Limitations of the EVM: Calculating without Justice

Solidity and the EVM (Ethereum Virtual Machine) were designed for Turing-complete **computation**, not for **contracts** in the social sense. They focus on if-else logic jumps and state_root hash transformations.
To the EVM, a legitimate transfer and an arbitrage transfer following a price manipulation attack are indistinguishable. Both are valid state transitions.

### 2. Semantic Loss

`
Human Intent             Compiler                 Execution Layer (EVM)
[No Unjust Enrichment] ────>  [Semantic Loss]  ────>  [balance -= amount]
      ↑                                          │
      │      (Missing Constitutional Layer)      │
      └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
`

When humans set rules, we say: "Unjust enrichment is prohibited." But when this moral norm is compiled into bytecode, severe **semantic loss** occurs. It becomes merely alance -= amount.
Hacks are essentially actions that are logically valid at the **Code Layer (L1)** but unconstitutional at the **Intent Layer (L2)**. Currently, blockchains only possess L1 (Execution Layer), lacking L2 (Intent Layer). (*Note: L1/L2 here refers to governance layering, not blockchain scaling layers.*)

**Philosophical Insight**: This is a direct manifestation of "Normative Medium Mismatch"—technology leaps, norms lag, system loses control.

> **Summary**: This chapter illustrates the specific manifestation of normative medium mismatch in Web3, validating the necessity of building a "Normative Layer" independent of the code execution layer.

## Chapter 2: Executable Norms—The On-Chain Constitutional Court

> **Core Philosophy**: *Constraints of Intent Constitution on Agentic Action*

To fix this misalignment, we introduce **Executable Norms**, a **formal constraint layer** located between code and natural language.

**Definition**: **Executable Normativism is a Web3 governance paradigm that embeds collective governance intent into the code execution flow through formal, verifiable invariant constraints, subjecting runtime system behavior to continuous normative review.**

### 1. The Intent Layer as Constitution

Before deploying smart contracts, the governance entity (DAO) must define a set of **Invariants** ($). These invariants constitute the protocol's "Constitution."

* Invariants only preserve collective intent; they do not create justice or resolve value conflicts, but they expose conflicts to the governors. (*Note: Executable norms do not solve "what is justice," but ensure "injustice cannot be executed silently."*)

Let $ be the state of the protocol, and $ be a transaction.
The validity of execution is defined not just by EVM validity ({EVM}$), but by Normative Validity ({Norm}$):

-Raw
Valid_{Norm}(S, T) \iff \forall i \in I, i(apply(S, T)) \text{ is true}
-Raw

* Example Invariant (Solvency):
  -Raw \sum \text{Debt} \leq 0.8 \times \sum \text{Collateral} -Raw

* Example (Quint Pseudocode):
  `quint
  // Invariant: Protocol Debt must not exceed 80% of Collateral
  invariant SolvencyCheck: 
    Protocol.totalDebt <= Protocol.totalCollateral * 0.8
  
  // Circuit Breaker Logic: Ensure SolvencyCheck holds after any state change
  action step(action_type) = {
    // ... execution logic ...
    ensure SolvencyCheck
  }
  `

These norms are written in specialized formal verification languages (like Quint* or specific DSLs) and anchored on-chain as **metadata**.
*\*Note: Quint is chosen for its engineering-friendliness, lowering the barrier to formal verification without requiring deep mathematical background.*

### 2. Circuit Breakers as Judicial Power

Traditional contracts are "execution by command." With executable norms, contracts become "execution by constitution."

*   Transaction is first executed by the EVM.
*   Before committing state, it passes through a **Runtime Verifier**.
    *   *Note: The verifier can be an on-chain contract (high security) or an off-chain consensus network (e.g., EigenLayer AVS, low cost).*
*   If constitutional invariants are violated, the transaction automatically **Reverts**.

This implants an **automated Supreme Court** on-chain. It does not interfere with daily business logic but holds veto power over "unconstitutional" actions.

### 3. The Separation of Powers

| Power | Executor | Description |
| --- | ----- | ------------- |
| Legislative | DAO | Votes to modify parameters or amend norms |
| Executive | Smart Contract | Efficiently matches transactions |
| Judicial | Executable Norms | Monitors executive boundaries, ensures constitutionality (non-interventionist) |

> **Summary**: This chapter constructs a complete on-chain separation of powers, establishing "Executable Norms" as the judicial layer with supreme veto power.

## Chapter 3: From Multi-sig to Agentic Governance

> **Core Philosophy**: *Depersonalized Trust in Human-Norm-Human Governance Structure*

### 1. The Death of the Human Voter & Trust Reconstruction

Asking humans to audit every line of Solidity is impossible. However, trust between DAO voters must be established. Executable norms assume the role of a "depersonalized" trust intermediary:

`
      Human A (Proposer)
          │
          ▼
   [Submit Formal Proof] ───> Executable Norm Layer (Judiciary)
          │                 │ (Auto Verification)
          │                 ▼
          └──────────> [Trust Established] <────────── Human B (Voter)
                       (Depersonalized)
`

Trust is no longer based on A's character, but on objective evidence provided by norms.

This mechanism not only solves efficiency issues but profoundly reconstructs political relations between humans. Through the normative layer, we transform "trust in people" into "verification of proofs," drastically reducing moral hazard and communication costs.

**Philosophical Note: From Negative Liberty to Positive Liberty**
In traditional Web3, liberty is often understood as "Negative Liberty" (freedom from interference, even from hackers). Executable norms introduce constraints to grant the community "Positive Liberty" (freedom to achieve collective goals). Norms are not chains of liberty, but guarantees of it—just as traffic rules guarantee the freedom of movement.

### 2. Personal Governance Agents

**Agentic Governance** digitizes human will and delegates it to AI Agents:

*   The Agent possesses **delegated power**, not sovereignty. Its legitimacy derives entirely from **personal authorization** (users retain the right to revoke or modify authorization at any time).
*   Users set values, e.g., "Only support protocols audited with inflation < 5%."
*   When a DAO proposes, the Agent pulls the code and formal proofs.
*   The Agent verifies proofs and votes automatically.

### 3. Proof-Based Communication

Proposers no longer rely on emotional copy but submit mathematical proofs (*these proofs can be AI-generated and verified, referencing ODD methodology[1], requiring humans to only check conclusions*):

*   *"Proof: The upgraded contract guarantees mathematically that no backdoor exists for admin asset extraction."*

This shift eliminates political manipulation and fraud.

> **Summary**: This chapter demonstrates how Agents act as amplifiers of human will, and how norms reconstruct trust mechanisms between humans.

## Chapter 5: Case Study—Reconstructing DeFi Security & Human Adjudication

> **Core Philosophy**: *Defense Mechanisms and the Ultimate Human Veto*

### Scenario 1: DeFi Security Defense (Auto-Circuit Break)

**Scenario**: A lending protocol suffers a price oracle attack. Hacker attempts flash loan arbitrage.

*   **Code is Law Mode**: Query oracle -> Price high -> Allow borrow -> Pool drained -> Hacker exits.
*   **Executable Norms Mode**:
    1.  **Constitutional Check**: *"Price deviation $\le$ 10%, Outflow $\le$ 50 ETH within single block"*
    2.  **Real-time Adjudication**: Transaction causes 500% deviation, attempts 1000 ETH borrow.
    3.  **Auto-Circuit Break**: Verifier detects unconstitutionality, transaction fails. (*Circuit break triggered by decentralized network, not admin private key.*)
    4.  **Result**: Hacker loses Gas; protocol safe.

### Scenario 2: Human Adjudication (Value Conflict)

**Scenario**: Community must decide on treasury compensation for a user (ethical issue, not code bug).

*   **Code is Law Mode**: Unable to handle; often leads to forks.
*   **Executable Norms Mode**:
    1.  Trigger "Adjudication Ambiguity Domain"; system pauses related pools.
    2.  Generate formal proposal listing mathematical consequences of compensation options (e.g., inflation impact). *This process employs "Output Simulation" from ODD methodology.*
    3.  Humans (or Agents) vote to adjudicate.
    4.  Adjudication result becomes a one-time executable norm to guide compensation.

> **Summary**: This chapter validates the effectiveness of executable norms in both "automated defense" and "assisted human adjudication" via dual scenarios.

Assets carry "Verified Artifact" hashes. Cross-chain or institutional settlement shifts from **T+N** (audit delay) to **T+0** (instant proof verification), bridging on-chain speed with off-chain trust.

## Chapter 7: Boundaries & Risks

Executable Norms are powerful, but not omnipotent.
*   **Creative Domain**: Art, culture, and aesthetics cannot be formalized.
*   **Moral Ambiguity**: Trolley problems require human arbitration, not automated execution.
*   **Legal Conflict**: On-chain norms may clash with off-chain jurisdictions; norms must include "exit hatches" for legal compliance.
Norms are tools for enforcement, not substitutes for value judgment.

## Conclusion: Reforging Web3 Legitimacy

The original intent of Web3 was **Trustless**, but this has been mistakenly interpreted as **Responsibility-less**.
True trustlessness is not burying one's head in the sand pretending code is flawless, but building a resilient architecture where **the system can self-correct even when code is flawed**.

**Code is Law in the Execution Layer, but must yield to Intent is Constitution in the Governance Layer.**

Executable Normativism offers Web3 a path out of the shadow of being a "lawless land." When regulatory rules (like AML, investor protection) are translated into on-chain executable norms, Web3 will no longer be the enemy of regulation, but the most compliant, transparent, and efficient financial infrastructure in human history.

Executable norms do not end politics; they force politics to be responsible for its promises.

**Code serves the Norms.**
**Norms preserve Human Will.**
This is the future Web3 deserves. This paper does not claim executable norms solve all governance problems, but attempts to provide a verifiable, retrospective minimal structure for Web3 governance.
