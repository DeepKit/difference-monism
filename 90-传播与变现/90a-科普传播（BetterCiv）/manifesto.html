Manifesto

A structural diagnosis of our time

AI is not going to destroy civilization by becoming too intelligent. The real risk is structural: decision-making power is expanding faster than accountability structures can keep up.

When a decision is made, but no one is clearly responsible for its consequences — the system has a crack. We call this Shadow Sovereignty.

This is not new. The East India Company, financial derivatives, bureaucratic delegation chains — all created the same pattern: decisions happen, consequences land somewhere, but accountability disappears in the middle. AI is just the latest form. Faster. Larger. More invisible.

Three Principles

1. Accountability Symmetry
Every decision must be owned by the entity that bears its consequences. This is not a moral demand — it is the minimum condition for structural stability.

2. Anti-Absolutism
No governance structure is final. Every system must remain questionable, correctable, replaceable. A structure that claims permanent validity has already begun to lose legitimacy.

3. Verifiability over Readability
In the AI era, we cannot trust process. We must verify output. The shift from "process compliance" to "result verification" is the core engineering paradigm of our time.

What We Are Building

Three theories — ODD, ECET, ASTO — form a unified framework: from engineering practice, to governance structure, to ontological foundation. Not for academic prestige. For actual use.

We are looking for people who feel the same structural unease — and want to do something about it.
