## T01: The Philosophical Roots of Evolutionary Constraint Existential Theory

**Abstract**

Evolutionary Constraint Existential Theory (ECET) proposes a theoretical framework wherein existence, cognition, and system behavior all evolve under historical, physical, social, and cognitive constraints, producing unpredictable emergent properties. This paper traces the philosophical origins of ECET by examining three major intellectual traditions: Darwin's theory of natural selection, Kuhn's philosophy of scientific revolutions, and Polanyi's theory of tacit knowledge. Through analyzing how these traditions understand "constraint," "evolution," and "cognitive limitations," we demonstrate the philosophical rationality of ECET's three fundamental axioms—energy constraint, adaptive selection constraint, and incompleteness constraint—and clarify ECET's position in contemporary ontology and philosophy of science.

**Keywords**: evolutionary constraint, existentialism, Darwin, Kuhn, Polanyi, philosophy of science

## T02: Being and Attribute Collections — The Ontological Mapping of ECET

**Abstract**

This paper develops ECET's ontological framework by transforming traditional entity-based ontology into relation-based ontology. We introduce the concepts of "attribute collection" and "interventionist ternary relations" to describe how systems emerge from attribute networks. By analyzing the four-level structure of ECET ontology (individual → collection → system → network), we demonstrate how constraints shape existence at each level. The paper addresses fundamental philosophical challenges including the problem of relational bearers, the ontological status of emergence, and the conflict with physicalism, proposing ECET's response strategies based on process philosophy, weak emergence, and non-reductive physicalism.

**Keywords**: attribute collection, relational ontology, emergence, interventionist ternary relation, process philosophy

## T03: Cognitive Bias and Evolutionary Trial-and-Error

**Abstract**

This paper reinterprets cognitive bias from the perspective of ECET, arguing that bias is not a defect to be eliminated but a necessary mechanism for evolutionary trial-and-error. We analyze the epistemological value of incompleteness, demonstrating how constraints on cognition create space for exploration and creativity. The paper develops a classification system for biases (cognitive, behavioral, systemic) and explains the mechanism of bias-to-creativity transformation through closed-loop adaptation. We argue that productive biases—those that can be channeled into innovation—represent a crucial resource for adaptive systems.

**Keywords**: cognitive bias, incompleteness, evolutionary trial-and-error, productive bias, creativity

## T04: The Four-Layer Architecture of ECET Theory

**Abstract**

This paper presents the systematic construction of ECET theory through its four-layer architecture: (1) theoretical foundations, (2) bias-creativity mechanism, (3) closed-loop practice, and (4) application and verification. We clarify the relationship between this "theoretical four-layer" and the "ontological four-layer" in T02, demonstrating how philosophical foundations translate into practical methodology. The paper defines key concepts including dynamic boundary management, constraint coupling, and emergent adaptation, providing a coherent framework for understanding how ECET applies to complex systems.

**Keywords**: four-layer architecture, theoretical construction, closed-loop adaptation, dynamic boundary

## T05: Mathematical Models of Bias Generation and Closed-Loop Adaptation

**Abstract**

This paper provides rigorous mathematical formalization for ECET through three complementary approaches: (1) stochastic differential equations for bias generation, modeling cognitive deviation as constrained random processes; (2) dynamic systems models for closed-loop adaptation, analyzing stability, convergence, and robustness; and (3) optimization frameworks for multi-path trial-and-error, including the exploration-exploitation tradeoff. We demonstrate how ECET transitions from philosophical concepts to computational theory while acknowledging the boundaries of formalization.

**Keywords**: stochastic differential equation, dynamic system, closed-loop adaptation, optimization framework

## T06: Three Evolutionary Constraint Axioms and System Behavior

**Abstract**

This paper systematically derives the implications of ECET's three axioms for system behavior. We analyze how energy constraint, adaptive selection constraint, and incompleteness constraint shape cognitive models, action strategies, and evolutionary trajectories. Through formal deduction, we demonstrate how constraints generate emergent behaviors including cognitive simplification, adaptive diversity, innovation emergence, and system resilience. The paper further analyzes constraint interactions, revealing how constraint coupling produces nonlinear effects, and discusses ECET's theoretical advantages including explanatory power, predictive power, and integrative capacity.

**Keywords**: evolutionary constraint axioms, system behavior, constraint interaction, emergent properties

## T07: The Scientific Significance and Theoretical Value of ECET

**Abstract**

This paper evaluates the scientific significance and theoretical value of ECET by comparing it with mainstream theories including Darwinian evolution, complex systems theory, and constructivist epistemology. We demonstrate ECET's three core contributions: (1) integrative theoretical framework unifying evolution, ontology, and cognitive science; (2) paradigm shift potential from "eliminating bias" to "utilizing bias"; and (3) interdisciplinary value across cognitive science, artificial intelligence, and organizational theory. The paper honestly assesses ECET's limitations including abstractness, boundary conditions, empirical support, and normative inadequacy, providing a balanced foundation for future development.

**Keywords**: scientific significance, theoretical value, paradigm shift, interdisciplinary integration

## T08: ECET and the Possible Integration with Future Philosophy of Science

**Abstract**

This paper explores ECET's potential integration with emerging trends in philosophy of science, focusing on cognitive science, complex systems theory, and artificial intelligence. We analyze how ECET provides a unified meta-theoretical framework for these fields, addressing their shared challenges including emergence, bounded rationality, and the limits of formalization. The paper examines ECET's resonance with Eastern philosophy (Taoism, Confucianism, I Ching), demonstrating deep ontological agreements between Western and Eastern thought. Finally, we outline future research directions for ECET in the context of 21st-century science.

**Keywords**: philosophy of science, cognitive science, complex systems, artificial intelligence, Eastern philosophy

## T09: Bias Evolution and Creativity — The Philosophical Implications of ECET

**Abstract**

This paper develops a creativity philosophy based on ECET, arguing that creativity emerges from the transformation of productive biases under constraint. We analyze the evolutionary mechanism of creativity, demonstrating how bias → adaptation → feedback loops generate innovative solutions. The paper explores ECET's implications for education, suggesting that teaching should cultivate the ability to utilize biases rather than simply eliminate them. We also examine ECET's resonance with Eastern wisdom (wu-wei, zhong-yong, biAN-yi), proposing an integrated creativity framework that combines Western scientific formalization with Eastern philosophical depth.

**Keywords**: creativity, bias evolution, productive bias, educational implications, Eastern wisdom

## T10: Theoretical Boundaries and Positioning

**Abstract**

This paper clarifies ECET's theoretical boundaries and positioning within the landscape of related theories. We define ECET's applicable scope (complex adaptive systems, finite-resource agents, evolutionary processes, emergent phenomena) and its limitations (simple mechanical systems, closed static systems, scenarios with complete information). Through systematic comparisons with Darwinian evolution, complex systems theory, constructivism, and cognitive bias research, we articulate ECET's unique contributions: systematic constraint conceptualization, bias value inversion, positive interpretation of incompleteness, ontological foundations of emergence, and interdisciplinary unifying language.

**Keywords**: theoretical boundaries, unique contributions, comparative analysis, positioning

## T11: Testable Hypothesis Catalog

**Abstract**

This paper presents a catalog of testable hypotheses derived from ECET, adopting a lightweight verification strategy: indirect verification, case simulation, and mathematical analysis. We propose 10 testable hypotheses including H1 (constraint-creativity inverted-U relationship), H2 (bias productivity-adaptiveness correlation), and H3 (feedback frequency-adaptiveness inverted-U relationship). ## T12: Lightweight Mathematical Formalization

**Abstract**

This paper provides lightweight mathematical formalization for ECET using discrete difference equations, set theory/graph theory, and stability analysis. We develop difference equation models for bias generation, bias-to-creativity transformation, and closed-loop adaptation. Set theory formalization covers system definitions, constraint representations, and emergence conditions. Graph theory formalization includes network representation of systems, constraint propagation, and emergence network conditions. Stability analysis provides inequality proofs for closed-loop stability and constraint stability. The goal is not complex computation but clear logical chains making theory traceable and calculable.

**Keywords**: difference equations, set theory, graph theory, stability analysis, inequality proofs

## Summary: ECET at a Glance

**Core Proposition**: Existence, cognition, and system behavior all evolve under constraints, producing emergent properties that cannot be fully predicted.

**Three Axioms**:
1. **Energy Constraint**: All entities must maintain structure within energy dissipation limits
2. **Adaptive Selection Constraint**: Attributes/behaviors must produce positive adaptation or be eliminated
3. **Incompleteness Constraint**: Cognitive models cannot perfectly describe/cover the environment

**Core Insights**:
- Bias ≠ Defect (bias is raw material for creativity)
- Incompleteness ≠ Error (incompleteness creates space for exploration)
- Constraint ≠ Limitation (constraint is the condition for existence)
- Creativity = Constraint + Evolution

**Theoretical Contributions**:
1. Integrative framework unifying evolution, ontology, and cognitive science
2. Paradigm shift from "eliminating bias" to "utilizing bias"
3. Interdisciplinary value across multiple fields
